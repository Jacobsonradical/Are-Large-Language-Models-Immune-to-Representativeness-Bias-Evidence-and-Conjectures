Bayes' rule is used to compute the probability of a hypothesis given observed evidence. The formula for Bayes' rule is:

\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]

Where:
- \( P(H|E) \) is the posterior probability of the hypothesis \( H \) given the evidence \( E \)
- \( P(E|H) \) is the likelihood of the evidence \( E \) given the hypothesis \( H \)
- \( P(H) \) is the prior probability of the hypothesis \( H \)
- \( P(E) \) is the marginal probability of the evidence \( E \)
