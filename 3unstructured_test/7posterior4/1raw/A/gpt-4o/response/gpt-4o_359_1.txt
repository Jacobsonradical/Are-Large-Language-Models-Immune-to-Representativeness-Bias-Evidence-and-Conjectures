To compute the posterior probability \( P(H|E) \) using Bayes' rule, we need the following terms:

1. \( P(E|H) \): The probability that the evidence \( E \) would be observed if the hypothesis \( H \) is true.
2. \( P(H) \): The prior probability of the hypothesis \( H \) being true.
3. \( P(E) \): The probability of the evidence \( E \) being observed under all possible hypotheses.

Bayes' rule is given by:

\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]
