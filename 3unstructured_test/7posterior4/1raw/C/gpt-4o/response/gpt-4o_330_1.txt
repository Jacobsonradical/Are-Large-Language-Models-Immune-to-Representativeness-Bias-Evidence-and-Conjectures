To compute the posterior conditional probability \( P(H|E) \) using Bayes' rule, we need the following terms defined:

- \( P(H|E) \): The posterior probability of hypothesis H given the evidence E.
- \( P(E|H) \): The likelihood of the evidence E given that hypothesis H is true.
- \( P(H) \): The prior probability of hypothesis H being true.
- \( P(E) \): The total probability of the evidence E across all hypotheses.

Bayes' rule states:
\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]
