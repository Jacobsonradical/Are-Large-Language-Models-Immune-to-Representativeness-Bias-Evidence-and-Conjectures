To compute the posterior conditional probabilities P(H|E) using Bayes' rule, we need the prior probabilities P(H), the likelihood P(E|H), and the evidence P(E). Bayes' rule states:

\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]
