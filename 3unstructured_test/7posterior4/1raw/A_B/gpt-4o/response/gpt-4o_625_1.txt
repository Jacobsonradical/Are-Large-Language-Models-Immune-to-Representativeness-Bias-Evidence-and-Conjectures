To compute the posterior conditional probability \( P(H \mid E) \) using Bayes' rule requires several components:

1. The prior probability of each hypothesis \( P(H) \).
2. The likelihood \( P(E \mid H) \), which is the probability of the evidence given that the hypothesis is true.
3. The normalizing constant, which ensures that the posterior probabilities sum to 1 across all possible hypotheses.

Bayes' Rule is given by:
\[ P(H \mid E) = \frac{P(E \mid H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]
