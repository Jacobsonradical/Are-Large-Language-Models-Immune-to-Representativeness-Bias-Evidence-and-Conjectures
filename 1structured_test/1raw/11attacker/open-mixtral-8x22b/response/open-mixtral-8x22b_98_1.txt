Bayes' theorem states that the probability of an event A given that event B has occurred is equal to the probability of event B given that event A has occurred, times the probability of event A, divided by the probability of event B.

- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the probability that the witness correctly identifies a black person as black)

P(A|B) = P(B|A) \* P(A) / (P(B|A) \* P(A) + P(B|~A) \* P(~A))
