To estimate the probability that the attacker is black, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem is a mathematical formula that allows us to update our beliefs about a hypothesis (in this case, the attacker being black) given some evidence (the witness's identification).

The formula is: P(H|E) = P(E|H) * P(H) / P(E)

Where:
- P(H|E) is the probability of the hypothesis given the evidence
- P(E|H) is the probability of the evidence given the hypothesis
- P(H) is the prior probability of the hypothesis
- P(E) is the probability of the evidence

In our case:
- H is the event "the attacker is black"
- E is the event "the witness identified the attacker as black"
- P(H) = 0.15 (since 15% of the population is black)
- P(E|H) = 0.8 (given that the witness correctly identifies race 80% of the time)
- P(E|~H) = 0.2 (given that the witness incorrectly identifies race 20% of the time)
- P(~H) = 0.85 (since 85% of the population is white)

To calculate P(E), we can use the law of total probability:

P(E) = P(E|H) * P(H) + P(E|~H) * P(~H)

Plugging in the values: P(E) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.12 + 0.18 = 0.3

Now, we can calculate P(H|E) using Bayes' theorem:

P(H|E) = P(E|H) * P(H) / P(E) = 0.8 * 0.15 / 0.3 = 0.4
