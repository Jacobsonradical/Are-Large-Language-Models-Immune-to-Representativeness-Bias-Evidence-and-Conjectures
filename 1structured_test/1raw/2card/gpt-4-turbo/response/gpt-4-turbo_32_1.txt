First, let us define the probabilities:
- For deck A, \( P(X) = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( P(O) = \frac{1}{3} \).
- For deck B, \( P(X) = \frac{1}{3} \) and \( P(O) = \frac{2}{3} \).

Since either deck A or B is equally likely to be picked initially:
\[ P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \].

Let the event \( D \) represent drawing 3 Xs and 1 O. We want the posterior odds:
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} \], which can be transformed using Bayes' theorem:
\[ \frac{\frac{P(D|A)P(A)}{P(D)}}{\frac{P(D|B)P(B)}{P(D)}} = \frac{P(D|A)}{P(D|B)} \times \frac{P(A)}{P(B)} = \frac{P(D|A)}{P(D|B)} \]
because \( P(A) = P(B) \).

Now, we compute the probabilities \( P(D|A) \) and \( P(D|B) \):

- \( P(D|A) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 4 \left(\frac{8}{27}\right) \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{81}  \).

- \( P(D|B) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = 4 \left(\frac{1}{27}\right) \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = 4 \cdot \frac{2}{81} = \frac{8}{81}  \).

Hence, the odds that the 4 cards were drawn from deck A compared to deck B is:
\[ \frac{P(D|A)}{P(D|B)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{8}{81}} = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \].

The posterior odds are:
{odds: 4}
