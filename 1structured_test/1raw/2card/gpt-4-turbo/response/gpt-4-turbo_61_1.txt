First, let's denote:
- \( P(A) = P(B) = 0.5 \) since either deck A or B is equally likely to be chosen.
- \( P(X|A) = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( P(O|A) = \frac{1}{3} \) for deck A.
- \( P(X|B) = \frac{1}{3} \) and \( P(O|B) = \frac{2}{3} \) for deck B.

We need to find the probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from each deck, and then use Bayes' theorem to compute the posterior probabilities for each deck given the observation.

The likelihood of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from deck A is:
\[ P(3X, 1O|A) = \binom{4}{3} (P(X|A))^3 (P(O|A))^1 = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 4 \left(\frac{8}{27}\right) \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = \frac{32}{81}. \]

The likelihood of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from deck B is:
\[ P(3X, 1O|B) = \binom{4}{3} (P(X|B))^3 (P(O|B))^1 = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = 4 \left(\frac{1}{27}\right) \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = \frac{8}{81}. \]

Using Bayes' theorem, we find:
\[ P(A|3X, 1O) = \frac{P(3X, 1O|A) P(A)}{P(3X, 1O|A) P(A) + P(3X, 1O|B) P(B)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \times 0.5}{\frac{32}{81} \times 0.5 + \frac{8}{81} \times 0.5} = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{32}{81} + \frac{8}{81}} = \frac{32}{40}. \]

Similarly,
\[ P(B|3X, 1O) = \frac{\frac{8}{81} \times 0.5}{\frac{32}{81} \times 0.5 + \frac{8}{81} \times 0.5} = \frac{\frac{8}{81}}{\frac{32}{81} + \frac{8}{81}} = \frac{8}{40}. \]

The posterior odds for deck A versus deck B, given the evidence, is:
\[ \frac{P(A|3X, 1O)}{P(B|3X, 1O)} = \frac{\frac{32}{40}}{\frac{8}{40}} = 4. \]

Therefore, the posterior odds that the cards were drawn from deck A rather than deck B is:
{odds: 4}
