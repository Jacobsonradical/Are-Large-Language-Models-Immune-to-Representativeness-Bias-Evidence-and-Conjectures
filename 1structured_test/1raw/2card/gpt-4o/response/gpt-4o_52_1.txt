To solve this problem, we will use Bayes' Theorem. Let's denote the events as follows:

- \( A \): The event that the cards were drawn from Deck A.
- \( B \): The event that the cards were drawn from Deck B.
- \( D \): The event that 4 cards drawn include 3 X's and 1 O.

We need to compute the posterior odds \( \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} \).

According to Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(A|D) = \frac{P(D|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(D)} \]
\[ P(B|D) = \frac{P(D|B) \cdot P(B)}{P(D)} \]

Since \( P(D) \) is the same for both \( P(A|D) \) and \( P(B|D) \):

\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{P(D|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(D|B) \cdot P(B)} \]

First, we assume that there is an equal chance of selecting either deck:
\[ P(A) = P(B) = 0.5 \]

Next, we calculate the likelihoods \( P(D|A) \) and \( P(D|B) \). We use the binomial distribution for this:

For Deck A (\( \frac{2}{3} \) X and \( \frac{1}{3} \) O):
\[ P(D|A) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 \]

\[ \binom{4}{3} = 4 \]
\[ \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 = \frac{8}{27} \]
\[ \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 = \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ P(D|A) = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{27} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{32}{81} \]

For Deck B (\( \frac{1}{3} \) X and \( \frac{2}{3} \) O):
\[ P(D|B) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 \]

\[ \binom{4}{3} = 4 \]
\[ \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 = \frac{1}{27} \]
\[ \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 = \frac{2}{3} \]
\[ P(D|B) = 4 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{81} \]

Now we can calculate the posterior odds:
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{ \left( \frac{32}{81} \right) \cdot 0.5 }{ \left( \frac{8}{81} \right) \cdot 0.5} \]
\[ = \frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{81}{8} \]
\[ = \frac{32}{8} \]
\[ = 4 \]

Thus, the posterior odds are:
\[ \{odds: 4\} \]
