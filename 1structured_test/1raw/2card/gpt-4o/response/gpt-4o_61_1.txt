To solve the problem, we can use Bayes' Theorem to find the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B.

Given:
- \( P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \) (equal probability for selecting either deck)
- In Deck A: \( P(X) = \frac{2}{3} \), \( P(O) = \frac{1}{3} \)
- In Deck B: \( P(X) = \frac{1}{3} \), \( P(O) = \frac{2}{3} \)

We need:
- \( P(\text{3X, 1O} | A) \) = Probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck A
- \( P(\text{3X, 1O} | B) \) = Probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck B

1. Calculate \( P(\text{3X, 1O} | A) \):

\[ P(\text{3X, 1O} | A) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^1 \]
\[ = 4 \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) \]
\[ = 4 \times \frac{8}{27} \times \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ = 4 \times \frac{8}{81} \]
\[ = \frac{32}{81} \]

2. Calculate \( P(\text{3X, 1O} | B) \):

\[ P(\text{3X, 1O} | B) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^1 \]
\[ = 4 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \]
\[ = 4 \times \frac{1}{27} \times \frac{2}{3} \]
\[ = 4 \times \frac{2}{81} \]
\[ = \frac{8}{81} \]

3. Use Bayes' Theorem to find the posterior odds:

\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{P(A | \text{3X, 1O})}{P(B | \text{3X, 1O})} \]
\[ = \frac{P(\text{3X, 1O} | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(\text{3X, 1O} | B) \cdot P(B)} \]
\[ = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}} \]
\[ = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{8}{81}} \]
\[ = \frac{32}{8} \]
\[ = 4 \]

So, the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B is:

{odds: 4}
