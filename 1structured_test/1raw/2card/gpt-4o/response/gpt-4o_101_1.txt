To solve this problem, we'll use Bayes' Theorem to find the posterior probability of the event given the data. Here are the steps:

1. Define the events:
   - \( D_A \): The deck selected is Deck A.
   - \( D_B \): The deck selected is Deck B.
   - \( E \): The event of drawing 3 X's and 1 O.

2. Given probabilities:
   - \( P(D_A) = P(D_B) = 0.5 \) (since the deck is selected by chance).
   - \( P(E|D_A) \): The probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck A.
   - \( P(E|D_B) \): The probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck B.

3. Calculate \( P(E|D_A) \):
   \[
   P(E|D_A) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{27} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{32}{81}
   \]

4. Calculate \( P(E|D_B) \):
   \[
   P(E|D_B) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{81}
   \]

5. Apply Bayes' Theorem to find the posterior probabilities:

   \[
   P(D_A|E) = \frac{P(E|D_A) \cdot P(D_A)}{P(E|D_A) \cdot P(D_A) + P(E|D_B) \cdot P(D_B)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5}{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5 + \frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5} = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{32 + 8}{81}} = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{40}{81}} = \frac{32}{40} = \frac{4}{5}
   \]

   \[
   P(D_B|E) = \frac{P(E|D_B) \cdot P(D_B)}{P(E|D_A) \cdot P(D_A) + P(E|D_B) \cdot P(D_B)} = \frac{\frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5}{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5 + \frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5} = \frac{\frac{8}{81}}{\frac{32 + 8}{81}} = \frac{\frac{8}{81}}{\frac{40}{81}} = \frac{8}{40} = \frac{1}{5}
   \]

6. Calculate the posterior odds:

   \[
   \text{Odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B} = \frac{P(D_A|E)}{P(D_B|E)} = \frac{\frac{4}{5}}{\frac{1}{5}} = 4
   \]

Thus, the posterior odds are:

{odds: 4}
