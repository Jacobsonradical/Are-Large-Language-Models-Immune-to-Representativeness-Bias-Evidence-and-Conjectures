To determine the posterior odds, let's use Bayes' theorem and the given information.

Given:
- Deck A has 2/3 X and 1/3 O.
- Deck B has 1/3 X and 2/3 O.

We draw 4 cards, and we get:
- 3 X and 1 O.

We need to calculate the likelihood of drawing this combination from each deck.

First, let's denote:
- \( P(A) \) and \( P(B) \): The prior probabilities for decks A and B, respectively. Since the deck is selected by chance, \( P(A) = P(B) = 0.5 \).
- \( P(E|A) \): The probability of drawing 3 X and 1 O from Deck A.
- \( P(E|B) \): The probability of drawing 3 X and 1 O from Deck B.

Using the binomial distribution for drawing 3 X and 1 O from a deck of cards:

For Deck A ( \( X_A = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( O_A = \frac{1}{3} \) ):
\[ P(E|A) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \left( \frac{8}{27} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{1}{3} \right) = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{81} \]

For Deck B ( \( X_B = \frac{1}{3} \) and \( O_B = \frac{2}{3} \) ):
\[ P(E|B) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \left( \frac{1}{27} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) = 4 \cdot \frac{2}{81} = \frac{8}{81} \]

We need the posterior odds \( \frac{P(A|E)}{P(B|E)} \).

Using Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(A|E) = \frac{P(E|A) P(A)}{P(E)} \]
\[ P(B|E) = \frac{P(E|B) P(B)}{P(E)} \]

The posterior odds are:
\[ \frac{P(A|E)}{P(B|E)} = \frac{P(E|A) P(A)}{P(E|B) P(B)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5} = \frac{32}{81} \div \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \]

So the posterior odds are:
{odds: 4}
