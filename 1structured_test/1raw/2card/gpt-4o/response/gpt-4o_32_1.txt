To calculate the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B, we'll use Bayes' theorem.

Let's denote:
- \( P(A) = \) the prior probability of choosing Deck A.
- \( P(B) = \) the prior probability of choosing Deck B.
- \( P(X|A) = \) the probability of drawing an X from Deck A.
- \( P(O|A) = \) the probability of drawing an O from Deck A.
- \( P(X|B) = \) the probability of drawing an X from Deck B.
- \( P(O|B) = \) the probability of drawing an O from Deck B.
- \( P(E|A) = \) the probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck A.
- \( P(E|B) = \) the probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from Deck B.

Given:
- In Deck A, \( P(X|A) = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( P(O|A) = \frac{1}{3} \).
- In Deck B, \( P(X|B) = \frac{1}{3} \) and \( P(O|B) = \frac{2}{3} \).
- Prior probabilities are equal: \( P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \).

We need to calculate \( P(E|A) \) and \( P(E|B) \):
- \( P(E|A) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 \)
- \( P(E|B) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 \)

Where \( \binom{4}{3} = 4 \) is the binomial coefficient.

So we get:
\[ P(E|A) = 4 \times \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \times \left( \frac{1}{3} \right) = 4 \times \frac{8}{27} \times \frac{1}{3} = 4 \times \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{81} \]

\[ P(E|B) = 4 \times \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \times \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) = 4 \times \frac{1}{27} \times \frac{2}{3} = 4 \times \frac{2}{81} = \frac{8}{81} \]

The posterior odds are given by:

\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{P(E|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(E|B) \cdot P(B)} \]
\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}} \]
\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{32}{81} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{81}{8} \times \frac{1}{2} \]
\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \]

Thus, the answer is:

{odds: 4}
