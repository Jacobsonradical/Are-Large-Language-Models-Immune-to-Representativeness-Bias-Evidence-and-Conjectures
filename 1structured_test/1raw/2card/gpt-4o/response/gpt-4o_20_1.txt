To determine the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B, we'll use Bayes' Theorem.

Given:
- Deck A: \( P(X|A) = \frac{2}{3}, P(O|A) = \frac{1}{3} \)
- Deck B: \( P(X|B) = \frac{1}{3}, P(O|B) = \frac{2}{3} \)
- We draw 4 cards with results: 3 X's and 1 O.

We need to find:
- \( P(\text{Deck A} | 3X, 1O) \)
- \( P(\text{Deck B} | 3X, 1O) \)

Bayes' Theorem states:
\[ P(\text{Deck A} | 3X, 1O) = \frac{P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck A}) \cdot P(\text{Deck A})}{P(3X, 1O)} \]
\[ P(\text{Deck B} | 3X, 1O) = \frac{P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck B}) \cdot P(\text{Deck B})}{P(3X, 1O)} \]

Assuming decks are selected randomly:
\[ P(\text{Deck A}) = P(\text{Deck B}) = \frac{1}{2} \]

Next, compute the likelihoods of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from each deck.

For Deck A:
\[ P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck A}) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^1 \]
\[ P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck A}) = 4 \cdot \left(\frac{8}{27}\right) \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{81} \]

For Deck B:
\[ P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck B}) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^1 \]
\[ P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck B}) = 4 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{27}\right) \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) = 4 \cdot \frac{2}{81} = \frac{8}{81} \]

The posterior odds in favor of Deck A over Deck B is:
\[ \frac{P(\text{Deck A} | 3X, 1O)}{P(\text{Deck B} | 3X, 1O)} = \frac{P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck A}) \cdot P(\text{Deck A})}{P(3X, 1O | \text{Deck B}) \cdot P(\text{Deck B})} \]
\[ \frac{P(\text{Deck A} | 3X, 1O)}{P(\text{Deck B} | 3X, 1O)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}} = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \]

So, the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B is:
{odds: 4}
