Let's denote the events as follows:
- \( A \): The selected deck is Deck A.
- \( B \): The selected deck is Deck B.
- \( X \): A card drawn is marked X.
- \( O \): A card drawn is marked O.

Given:
- \( P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \) (since the deck is selected by chance),
- In Deck A, \( P(X|A) = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( P(O|A) = \frac{1}{3} \),
- In Deck B, \( P(X|B) = \frac{1}{3} \) and \( P(O|B) = \frac{2}{3} \).

We observe 3 cards marked X and 1 card marked O. To find the posterior odds, we need to calculate:
\[ P(3X, 1O | A) \] and \[ P(3X, 1O | B) \].

Using the multinomial distribution for the 4 drawn cards, we have:

For Deck A:
\[ P(3X, 1O | A) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{27} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{32}{81} \].

For Deck B:
\[ P(3X, 1O | B) = \binom{4}{3} \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{81} \].

We now find the posterior odds:
\[ \text{Posterior odds} = \frac{P(A|3X, 1O)}{P(B|3X, 1O)} = \frac{P(3X, 1O | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(3X, 1O | B) \cdot P(B)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}} = \frac{32 \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{8 \cdot \frac{1}{2}} = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \].

Thus, the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B are:
{odds: 4}
