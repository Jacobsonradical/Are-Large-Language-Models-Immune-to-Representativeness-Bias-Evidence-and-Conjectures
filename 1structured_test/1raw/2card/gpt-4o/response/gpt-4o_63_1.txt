To solve this problem, we will use Bayes' theorem to determine the posterior odds.

Firstly, let's define the following events:
- \( A \): Drawing cards from Deck A
- \( B \): Drawing cards from Deck B
- \( E \): Drawing 3 X's and 1 O

The prior probabilities for choosing each deck are:
\[ P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \]

The likelihoods of drawing 3 X's and 1 O from each deck can be computed using the binomial distribution. Let's calculate these for decks A and B.

For Deck A:
\[ P(E|A) = \binom{4}{3} \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{27} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{81} = \frac{32}{81} \]

For Deck B:
\[ P(E|B) = \binom{4}{3} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = 4 \cdot \frac{2}{81} = \frac{8}{81} \]

Now, we can use Bayes' theorem to find the posterior probabilities:

\[ P(A|E) = \frac{P(E|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(E)} \]
\[ P(B|E) = \frac{P(E|B) \cdot P(B)}{P(E)} \]

The total probability of E, \( P(E) \), is given by:
\[ P(E) = P(E|A) \cdot P(A) + P(E|B) \cdot P(B) = \frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2} + \frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{32}{162} + \frac{8}{162} = \frac{40}{162} = \frac{20}{81} \]

Therefore, the posterior probabilities are:
\[ P(A|E) = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{20}{81}} = \frac{\frac{32}{162}}{\frac{20}{81}} = \frac{32}{162} \cdot \frac{81}{20} = \frac{32}{162} \cdot \frac{81}{20} = \frac{32 \cdot 81}{162 \cdot 20} = \frac{32}{40} = 0.8 \]

\[ P(B|E) = \frac{\frac{8}{81} \cdot \frac{1}{2}}{\frac{20}{81}} = \frac{\frac{8}{162}}{\frac{20}{81}} = \frac{8}{162} \cdot \frac{81}{20} = \frac{8}{20} = 0.4 \]

The posterior odds, which are the ratio of these probabilities, are:
\[ \text{Odds} = \frac{P(A|E)}{P(B|E)} = \frac{0.8}{0.4} = 2 \]

Hence, the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B are:
{odds: 2}
