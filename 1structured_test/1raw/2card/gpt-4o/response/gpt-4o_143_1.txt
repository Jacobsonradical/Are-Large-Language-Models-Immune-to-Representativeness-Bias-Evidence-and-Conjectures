To determine the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B, we will use Bayes' theorem. 

Given:
- Deck A: \(P(X) = \frac{2}{3}\), \(P(O) = \frac{1}{3}\)
- Deck B: \(P(X) = \frac{1}{3}\), \(P(O) = \frac{2}{3}\)
- Prior probability of selecting Deck A (\(P(A)\)) = 0.5
- Prior probability of selecting Deck B (\(P(B)\)) = 0.5

We need to find the likelihood of drawing 3 Xâ€™s and 1 O:

**Likelihood for Deck A:**
\[
P(3X, 1O \mid A) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{8}{27} \cdot \frac{1}{3} = \frac{32}{81}
\]

**Likelihood for Deck B:**
\[
P(3X, 1O \mid B) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^1 = 4 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{81}
\]

Using the Bayes' theorem, the posterior probabilities (\(P(A \mid 3X, 1O)\) and \(P(B \mid 3X, 1O)\)) are calculated as follows:

\[
P(A \mid 3X, 1O) = \frac{P(3X, 1O \mid A) P(A)}{P(3X, 1O)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5}{P(3X, 1O)}
\]
\[
P(B \mid 3X, 1O) = \frac{P(3X, 1O \mid B) P(B)}{P(3X, 1O)} = \frac{\frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5}{P(3X, 1O)}
\]

Since \(P(A) = P(B) = 0.5\), the denominator \(P(3X, 1O)\) normalizes the probabilities and does not affect the ratio. Thus, the posterior odds that the 4 cards are drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B are:

\[
\text{Posterior odds} = \frac{P(A \mid 3X, 1O)}{P(B \mid 3X, 1O)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81} \cdot 0.5}{\frac{8}{81} \cdot 0.5} = \frac{32}{8} = 4
\]

\{odds: 4\}
