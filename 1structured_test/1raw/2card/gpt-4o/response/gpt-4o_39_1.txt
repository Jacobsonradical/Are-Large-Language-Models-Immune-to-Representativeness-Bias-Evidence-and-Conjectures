To determine the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B, we will use Bayes' theorem.

Let's define the events:
- \( A \): The deck chosen is Deck A.
- \( B \): The deck chosen is Deck B.
- \( D \): The event that 4 cards drawn are composed of 3 X's and 1 O.

We need to calculate the posterior odds \( \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} \).

First, we compute the likelihood of drawing 3 X's and 1 O for each deck.

**Likelihood for Deck A:**
- Probability of drawing an X: \( \frac{2}{3} \)
- Probability of drawing an O: \( \frac{1}{3} \)

The probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O can be computed using the binomial distribution:
\[ P(D|A) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) \]
\[ P(D|A) = 4 \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right) \]
\[ P(D|A) = 4 \times \frac{8}{27} \times \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ P(D|A) = 4 \times \frac{8}{81} \]
\[ P(D|A) = \frac{32}{81} \]

**Likelihood for Deck B:**
- Probability of drawing an X: \( \frac{1}{3} \)
- Probability of drawing an O: \( \frac{2}{3} \)

Similarly, the probability of drawing 3 X's and 1 O is:
\[ P(D|B) = \binom{4}{3} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \]
\[ P(D|B) = 4 \times \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^3 \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \]
\[ P(D|B) = 4 \times \frac{1}{27} \times \left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \]
\[ P(D|B) = 4 \times \frac{2}{81} \]
\[ P(D|B) = \frac{8}{81} \]

**Prior probabilities:**
Assume \( P(A) = P(B) = \frac{1}{2} \).

Finally, we use Bayes' theorem to find the posterior odds:
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{P(D|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(D|B) \cdot P(B)} \]
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{\left(\frac{32}{81}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)}{\left(\frac{8}{81}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)} \]
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{\frac{32}{81}}{\frac{8}{81}} \]
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = \frac{32}{8} \]
\[ \frac{P(A|D)}{P(B|D)} = 4 \]

Thus the posterior odds that the 4 cards were drawn from Deck A compared to Deck B is:
{odds: 4}
