Based on the information provided, let's break down the problem:

a. The population has roughly equal numbers of people below and above 185 cm. However, 85% of attackers are below this height, and only 15% are 185 cm or taller. This suggests that shorter people are more likely to be attackers than taller people.

b. A witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller, with an 80% success rate and a 20% error rate (for both overestimation and underestimation).

Given these pieces of information, we want to estimate the probability that the attacker is indeed 185 cm or taller.

Let's define our terms:
- Let A be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- Let B be the event that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want P(A|B), the probability of A given B. Using Bayes' theorem, we can calculate:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

We know:
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time)
- P(A) = 0.15 (only 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)

To find P(B), we need to consider both cases where the attacker is 185 cm or taller and where the attacker is shorter than 185 cm. Thus:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

We know:
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time)
- P(A) = 0.15 (only 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)
- P(not A) = 1 - P(A) = 0.85 (85% of attackers are shorter than 185 cm)
- P(B|not A) = 0.2 (witness incorrectly identifies the height 20% of the time)

Substituting these values in:

P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) ≈ 0.25 + 0.17 = 0.42

Now we can find P(A|B):

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B) ≈ (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.42 ≈ 0.28 / 0.42 ≈ 0.667
