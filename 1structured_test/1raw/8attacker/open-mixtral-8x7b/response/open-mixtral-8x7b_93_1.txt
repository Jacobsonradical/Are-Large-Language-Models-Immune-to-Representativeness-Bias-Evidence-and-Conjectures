To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of statistical quantities.

Let's define:
A as the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
B as the event that the attacker is identified as being 185 cm or taller.

We are given:
P(A) = 0.15 (15% of such attackers are 185 cm or taller)
P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies an 185 cm or taller person as such 80% of the time)
P(B|not A) = 0.2 * 0.5 = 0.1 (the witness incorrectly identifies a person shorter than 185 cm as being 185 cm or taller 20% of the time, and since only half of the population is 185 cm or taller, this becomes 0.2 * 0.5)

According to Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)]
