Bayes' theorem: P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

- P(A): Probability of a random attacker being 185 cm or taller = 0.15 (15% of attackers)

Now we need to find P(B), the probability of the witness identifying an attacker as 185 cm or taller regardless of their actual height. We can calculate this using the law of total probability:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)
        = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85)
        = 0.12 + 0.17
        = 0.29

Now we can find P(A|B):

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)
           = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
