To solve this problem and estimate the probability \( P(H \geq 185) \) that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness's claim and the demographics of the attacker population, we use a form of Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem will help us update our probabilities based on new evidence (the witness's testimony).

We first analyze the provided data:

1. Population distribution:
   - Probability the population is under 185 cm, \( P(H < 185) \) = 0.5
   - Probability the population is 185 cm or taller, \( P(H \geq 185) \) = 0.5

2. Attacker distribution:
   - Probability an attacker is under 185 cm, \( P(A < 185) \) = 0.85
   - Probability an attacker is 185 cm or taller, \( P(A \geq 185) \) = 0.15

3. Witness reliability:
   - The witness correctly identified whether someone was 185 cm or taller 80% of the time.
   - Hence, the probability the witness correctly identifies someone being 185 cm or taller (True positive), \( P(W \geq 185 | H \geq 185) \) = 0.8
   - Probability the witness falsely identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller (False positive), \( P(W \geq 185 | H < 185) \) = 0.2

Using Bayes' theorem to update the probability that someone is 185 cm or taller given the witness testimony:

\[
P(H \geq 185 | W \geq 185) = \frac{P(W \geq 185 | H \geq 185) \times P(H \geq 185)}{P(W \geq 185)}
\]

Where:
- \( P(W \geq 185) \) is the total probability of the witness stating the individual is 185 cm or taller. It is given by:

\[
P(W \geq 185) = P(W \geq 185 | H \geq 185) \times P(H \geq 185) + P(W \geq 185 | H < 185) \times P(H < 185)
\]
\[
P(W \geq 185) = (0.8 \times 0.5) + (0.2 \times 0.5) = 0.4 + 0.1 = 0.5
\]

Hence,
\[
P(H \geq 185 | W \geq 185) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.5}{0.5} = 0.8
\]

Therefore, our estimate of the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification is 0.8 or 80%.
