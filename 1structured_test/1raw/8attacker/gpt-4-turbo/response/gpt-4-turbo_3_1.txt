To solve the problem, we need to employ Bayes' Theorem. Bayes' Theorem helps us update our prior belief about a probability given new evidence. Here, let's break it down:

1. **Prior Probabilities:**
   - \( P(H) \): Probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller = 0.5 (given equal population of taller and shorter than 185 cm)
   - \( P(L) \): Probability that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm = 0.5

2. **Likelihoods (Probability of Witness's Testimony given the Truth):**
   - \( P(W|H) \): Probability that the witness reports the attacker as 185 cm or taller, given that the attacker is indeed 185 cm or taller. This was correct 80% of the time = 0.8.
   - \( P(W|L) \): Probability that the witness reports the attacker as 185 cm or taller, given that the attacker is actually shorter than 185 cm. This was incorrect 20% of the time = 0.2.

3. **Probability of Witness's Testimony (Total Probability):**
   \[
   P(W) = P(W|H)P(H) + P(W|L)P(L) = (0.8 \cdot 0.5) + (0.2 \cdot 0.5) = 0.4 + 0.1 = 0.5
   \]

4. **Posterior Probability Using Bayes' Theorem:**
   - \( P(H|W) \): Probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness identified them as such:
   
   \[
   P(H|W) = \frac{P(W|H)P(H)}{P(W)} = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.5}{0.5} = 0.8
   \]

   However, we need to adjust this calculation as the probability figures relate to the ability to judge height, not to the proportion of attackers who are 185 cm or taller. Thus, incorporating the distribution of attackers' heights:
   
   - Adjusted Probability not only considering the witness's accuracy but also the likelihood of attackers' heights:
   - Given that 85% of attackers are shorter than 185 cm, we recalculate probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller:
   
   \[
   P(H|A) = \text{Probability of having a tall attacker} = 0.15
   \]
   \[
   P(L|A) = \text{Probability of having a short attacker} = 0.85
   \]
   
   Now, apply Bayes' Theorem to include base-rate information (crime statistics):
   \[
   P(H|W) = \frac{P(W|H)P(H|A)}{P(W)} = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.5} = 0.24
   \]
   \[
   P(L|W) = \frac{P(W|L)P(L|A)}{P(W)} = \frac{0.2 \times 0.85}{0.5} = 0.34
   \]
   
   Normalizing these probabilities (they must sum to 1):
   
   \[
   P(H|W)_{adjusted} = \frac{0.24}{0.24 + 0.34} \approx 0.4138
   \]

   Thus, the adjusted probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, accounting for witness accuracy and distribution of attackers' heights, is about 0.4138.
