To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' theorem. We'll represent the event of an attacker being 185 cm or taller as \( A \), and \( W \) as the event that a witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

Given from the problem:
1. \( P(W|A) \) is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, which equals 0.80.
2. \( P(W|\neg A) \) is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm, which equals 0.20.
3. The population proportion \( P(A) \), which is the probability that a randomly chosen person is 185 cm or taller, equals 0.5.
4. The probability \( P(A|C) \) that an attacker is 185 cm or taller given that they attack someone is 0.15 (only 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller).

What we want to calculate is \( P(A|W) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identifies them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
\[
P(A|W) = \frac{P(W|A)P(A)}{P(W)}
\]
We know \( P(W|A) \) and \( P(A) \), but we first need \( P(W) \), the total probability the witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller:
\[
P(W) = P(W|A)P(A) + P(W|\neg A)P(\neg A)
\]
Given \( P(\neg A) = 1 - P(A) = 0.5 \), we can substitute:
\[
P(W) = (0.80 \times 0.5) + (0.20 \times 0.5) = 0.40 + 0.10 = 0.50
\]
Then:
\[
P(A|W) = \frac{0.80 \times 0.5}{0.50} = \frac{0.40}{0.50} = 0.80
\]

Additionally, considering the population of attackers is biased towards those under 185 cm (85% under, 15% 185 cm or taller), we align with considering the base rates, leading to \( P(A|C) \) of 0.15 as described above, then:
\[
P(A|C \cap W) = \frac{P(W|A \cap C)P(A|C)}{P(W|C)}
\]
Here \( P(W|C) = P(W|A \cap C)P(A|C) + P(W|\neg A \cap C)P(\neg A|C) \) and \( P(A|C) \) differs from \( P(A) \).

Nevertheless, we have computed using straightforward \( P(A|W) \) without considering the attack context, yielding 0.80, which may be incorrect considering the bias in attacker population. Therefore, recalculating or estimating based on \( P(A|C) \) and witness reliability, a lower estimate might be more accurate:
\[
P(A|W) = \frac{P(W|A) \times 0.15}{0.50} = \frac{0.12}{0.50} = 0.24
