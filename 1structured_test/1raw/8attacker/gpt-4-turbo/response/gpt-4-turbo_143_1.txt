To calculate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, given the witness's testimony and the general population's likelihood to attack, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem calculates the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions that might be related to the event.

Let's define:

\( H \) = event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
\( W \) = witness's testimony that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
\( \neg H \) = event that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm

From the given information:

- Probability witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller (\( P(W|H) \)) = 80% or 0.8
- Probability witness incorrectly identifies someone as shorter than 185 cm (\( P(W|\neg H) \)) = 20% or 0.2
- Probability that an attacker is 185 cm or taller (\( P(H) \)) = 15% or 0.15
- Probability that an attacker is shorter than 185 cm (\( P(\neg H) \)) = 85% or 0.85

Using Bayes' theorem,

\[ P(H|W) = \frac{P(W|H) \times P(H)}{P(W)} \]

Where \( P(W) \), the total probability of the witness saying the attacker is 185 cm or taller, is calculated using the law of total probability:

\[ P(W) = P(W|H) \times P(H) + P(W|\neg H) \times P(\neg H) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.8 \times 0.15 + 0.2 \times 0.85 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Now, substitute these into Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(H|W) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H|W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]

Thus, the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, given the witness's statement, is approximately 0.4138.
