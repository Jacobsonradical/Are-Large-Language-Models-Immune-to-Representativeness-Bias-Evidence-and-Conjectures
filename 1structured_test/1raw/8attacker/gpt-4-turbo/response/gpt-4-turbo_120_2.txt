To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update the probability of a hypothesis, in this case, that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, based on new evidence.

Let $H$ be the hypothesis that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, and $E$ be the evidence that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Based on the information, we know:

- Prior Probability, $P(H)$ (that the attacker was 185 cm or taller before knowing the witness's testimony): Since the population of people shorter than 185 cm and those 185 cm and taller is roughly equal, $P(H) = 0.5$.
- Probability of Evidence given Hypothesis, $P(E \mid H)$ (probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is 185 cm or taller): From the witness's accuracy, $P(E \mid H) = 0.8$.
- Probability of Evidence given Hypothesis not true, $P(E \mid \neg H)$ (probability that witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is not 185 cm or taller): This is the rate of false positives, $P(E \mid \neg H) = 0.2$.

We seek the probability of the hypothesis given the evidence, $P(H \mid E)$:

Using Bayes' Theorem:

\[
P(H \mid E) = \frac{P(E \mid H) P(H)}{P(E)}
\]

Where $P(E)$, the total probability of evidence, can be calculated using the Law of Total Probability:

\[
P(E) = P(E \mid H) P(H) + P(E \mid \neg H) P(\neg H)
\]
\[
P(E) = (0.8 \times 0.5) + (0.2 \times 0.5) = 0.4 + 0.1 = 0.5
\]

Plugging these into Bayes' Theorem:

\[
P(H \mid E) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.5}{0.5} = \frac{0.4}{0.5} = 0.8
\]

So, the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification is 0.8.
