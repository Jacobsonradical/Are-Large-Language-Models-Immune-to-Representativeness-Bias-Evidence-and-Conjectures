To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the available evidence, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem allows us to update our prior belief about the probability of a hypothesis in light of new evidence.

1. **Define the events:**
   - Let \( A \) be the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
   - Let \( B \) be the event that a witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

2. **Known probabilities:**

From the witness's ability, this is 20% or 0.2, since when the attacker is shorter than 185 cm, the witness incorrectly reports them as being taller 20% of the time.

3. **Use Bayes' theorem:**
   \[
   P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \times P(A)}{P(B)}
   \]
   Where \( P(B) \) (probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller) can be calculated by the law of total probability:
   \[
   P(B) = P(B|A) \times P(A) + P(B|A^c) \times P(A^c)
   \]

   \[
   P(B) = 0.8 \times 0.15 + 0.2 \times 0.85 = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
   \]

4. **Calculate \( P(A|B) \):**
   \[
   P(A|B) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138
   \]
