To solve this problem, we will use Bayes' Theorem, which allows us to update our probability estimate for a hypothesis based on new evidence. In this case, our hypothesis \( H \) is that the attacker was 185 cm or taller. The new evidence \( E \) is the witness's testimony that the attacker was indeed 185 cm or taller.

1. **Prior Probability (\( P(H) \))**: 
   Initially, without considering the witness's testimony, our prior belief \( P(H) \) about the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller is based on the information that 15% attacks are by people who are 185 cm or taller. Hence, \( P(H) = 0.15 \).

2. **Prior Probability of not \( H \) (\( P(\neg H) \))**:
   Conversely, the probability the attacker was shorter than 185 cm is 85%. Thus, \( P(\neg H) = 0.85 \).

3. **Likelihood of Evidence given Hypothesis (\( P(E|H) \))**:
    This is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is indeed 185 cm or taller. The witness correctly identifies height 80% of the time, so \( P(E|H) = 0.80 \).

4. **Likelihood of Evidence given not Hypothesis (\( P(E|\neg H) \))**:
    This is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is actually shorter than 185 cm. From the witness' error rate (wrong 20% of the time), \( P(E|\neg H) = 0.20 \).

Using Bayes' Rule:
\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \times P(H)}{P(E)} \]

where
\[ P(E) = P(E|H) \times P(H) + P(E|\neg H) \times P(\neg H) \]
\[ P(E) = 0.80 \times 0.15 + 0.20 \times 0.85 = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Now substituting back:
\[ P(H|E) = \frac{0.80 \times 0.15}{0.29} = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]

Thus, the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller, given the witnessâ€™s testimony, is approximately **0.41**.
