To find the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony and the probability distribution of attackers' heights, we'll employ Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{P(W \mid H) \times P(H)}{P(W)} \]

Where:
- \( H \) (hypothesis) is the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W \) (witness) is the event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.
- \( P(H \mid W) \) is the probability the attacker was 185 cm or taller, given the witness's testimony.
- \( P(W \mid H) \) is the probability the witness correctly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller, given the person is indeed 185 cm or taller.
- \( P(H) \) is the prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( P(W) \) is the total probability of the witness identifying someone as being 185 cm or taller.

- \( P(H) = 0.15 \) (15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)
- \( P(\text{¬}H) \) is the probability the attacker is shorter than 185 cm, which is 0.85.

Given the witness's ability:
- \( P(W \mid H) = 0.80 \) (80% likelihood of correctly identifying a tall person as tall)
- \( P(W \mid \text{¬}H) = 0.20 \) (20% likelihood of mistakenly identifying a short person as tall)

Using total probability, the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as tall is:
\[ P(W) = P(W \mid H) \times P(H) + P(W \mid \text{¬}H) \times P(\text{¬}H) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.80 \times 0.15 + 0.20 \times 0.85 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Applying Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{P(W \mid H) \times P(H)}{P(W)} \]
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{0.80 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]
