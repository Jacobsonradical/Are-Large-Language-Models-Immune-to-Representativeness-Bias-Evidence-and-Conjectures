- The witness correctly identifies people 185 cm or taller 80% of the time (true positive rate).
- The witness incorrectly identifies people shorter than 185 cm as 185 cm or taller 20% of the time (false positive rate).
- 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller (prior probability).

A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
B: The witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) × P(A)) / P(B)

P(B|A) = 0.8 (true positive rate, given in the information)
P(A) = 0.15 (prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, given in the information)

To find P(B), we need to consider both cases:
P(B) = P(B|A) × P(A) + P(B|not A) × P(not A)
     = 0.8 × 0.15 + 0.2 × (1 - 0.15)

     = 0.29

Substituting the values in Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (0.8 × 0.15) / 0.29
       = 0.12 / 0.29
