To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we need to use Bayes' theorem and the given information.

Let's define the events:
- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
- B: The witness correctly identified the height as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified the height as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

Given:
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identified height 80% of the time for people 185 cm or taller)
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)
- P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)
       = 0.8 * 0.15 + (1 - 0.8) * 0.85 (using the complement rule and the given information)
       = 0.12 + 0.17
       = 0.29

Substituting the values in Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
       = 0.12 / 0.29
       ≈ 0.4138
