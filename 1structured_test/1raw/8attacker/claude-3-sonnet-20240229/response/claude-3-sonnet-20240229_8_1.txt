Given information:
- The witness correctly identifies height 80% of the time for people 185 cm or taller.
- The witness errs 20% of the time for people 185 cm or taller.
- 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller.
- 85% of attackers are shorter than 185 cm.

Let's define the following events:
A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
B: The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

P(B|A) = 0.8 (The witness correctly identifies height 80% of the time for people 185 cm or taller)
P(A) = 0.15 (15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)
P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|A') * P(A')
      = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 (P(B|A') = 0.2, as the witness errs 20% of the time for people shorter than 185 cm)
      = 0.12 + 0.17
      = 0.29

Substituting the values in Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
       = 0.12 / 0.29
       = 0.4137
