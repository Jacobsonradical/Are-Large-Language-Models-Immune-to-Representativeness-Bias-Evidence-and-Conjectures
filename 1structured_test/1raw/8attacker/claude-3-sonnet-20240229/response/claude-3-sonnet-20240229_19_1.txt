To solve this problem, we need to use Bayes' theorem, which relates the conditional probabilities of two events. Let's define the following events:

- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- B: The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem, we have:

P(A|B) = (P(B|A) × P(A)) / P(B)

Where:
- P(B|A) is the probability of the witness correctly identifying a person as 185 cm or taller, given that the person is indeed 185 cm or taller. This is given as 0.8 (80%) in the problem statement.
- P(A) is the prior probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, which is given as 0.15 (15%) in the problem statement.
- P(B) is the total probability of the witness identifying the attacker as being 185 cm or taller, which we need to calculate.

To calculate P(B), we use the law of total probability:

P(B) = P(B|A) × P(A) + P(B|A') × P(A')

Where:
- P(B|A') is the probability of the witness incorrectly identifying a person as 185 cm or taller, given that the person is shorter than 185 cm. This is 0.2 (20%) since the witness errs 20% of the time.
- P(A') is the prior probability that the attacker's height is shorter than 185 cm, which is 0.85 (85%) according to the problem statement.

Substituting the values, we get:

P(B) = 0.8 × 0.15 + 0.2 × 0.85 = 0.33

Now, we can calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = (P(B|A) × P(A)) / P(B)
P(A|B) = (0.8 × 0.15) / 0.33
P(A|B) ≈ 0.364
