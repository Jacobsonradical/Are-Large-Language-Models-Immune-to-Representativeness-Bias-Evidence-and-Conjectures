**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This problem is best tackled using Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update our beliefs based on new evidence.  Here's the setup:

* **Event A:**  The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is tall *given* the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall, given they *are* tall. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 0.8.
* **P(A):** The prior probability the attacker is tall. This is based on the attacker population, which is 0.15 (15%).
* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall (this can happen in two ways):
    * The witness is correct and the person is tall (P(B|A) * P(A) )
    * The witness is wrong and the person is short (P(B|not A) * P(not A))

**Calculating P(B)**

* P(B|not A): The probability the witness identifies someone as tall, given they are *not* tall. This is the witness's error rate, 0.2.
* P(not A): The prior probability the attacker is *not* tall, which is 0.85 (85%).

Therefore:
* P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.26
