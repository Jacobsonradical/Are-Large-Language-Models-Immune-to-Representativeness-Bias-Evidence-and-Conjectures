Here's how we can approach this problem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Information a:** This tells us the witness's reliability in judging height.  They are correct 80% of the time and incorrect 20% of the time.
* **Information b:** This tells us the relative frequency of attackers in each height group within the population.  85% of attackers are shorter than 185cm, and 15% are 185cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This problem is ideal for Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update our belief about an event (attacker's height) given new evidence (witness testimony).

Let's define:

*  **A:** Event that the attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **B:** Event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller. 

We want to find **P(A|B)**, the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the witness's testimony.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185cm or taller given the attacker *is* 185cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% (from information a).
* **P(A):**  Prior probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller. This is the population proportion, which is 15% (from information b).
* **P(B):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185cm or taller (regardless of the actual height). This requires a bit more calculation.

**Calculating P(B)**

We need to consider two scenarios:

1. **Attacker is actually 185cm or taller:** This happens with probability P(A) = 0.15. The witness will identify them correctly with probability P(B|A) = 0.80. 
2. **Attacker is actually shorter than 185cm:** This happens with probability 1 - P(A) = 0.85. The witness will identify them incorrectly as taller with probability 1 - P(B|A) = 0.20.

Therefore, P(B) can be calculated as:

P(B) = (P(A) * P(B|A)) + ((1 - P(A)) * (1 - P(B|A))) 
P(B) = (0.15 * 0.80) + (0.85 * 0.20)
P(B) = 0.29

**Putting it all Together**

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29
