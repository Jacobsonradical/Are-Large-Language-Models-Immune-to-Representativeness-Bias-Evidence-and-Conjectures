**Combining the Information**

We need to combine the information about population distribution, attacker likelihood, and witness reliability to estimate the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. 

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This type of problem is well-suited for Bayes' Theorem.  Here's how we can apply it:

* **Event A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given the attacker *is* 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, 80% (from information b).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. This is 15% (from information a).
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller. This requires a bit more calculation. We need to consider both cases:
    * The attacker is 185 cm or taller: P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.80 * 0.15 = 0.12
    * The attacker is under 185 cm: P(B|A') * P(A') = 0.20 * 0.85 = 0.17  (A' represents the attacker being under 185 cm)
    * P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
