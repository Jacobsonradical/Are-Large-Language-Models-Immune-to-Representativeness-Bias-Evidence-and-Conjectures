**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event based on new evidence. In this case:

* **Event:** The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
* **Evidence:** The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

Let's use the following notation:

*  A: Attacker is 185 cm or taller
*  B: Witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the witness's identification. Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall, given they are actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy: 80% or 0.8.
* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being tall. This is 15% or 0.15 (from information b).
* **P(B):** The overall probability of the witness identifying someone as tall.  This is calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
