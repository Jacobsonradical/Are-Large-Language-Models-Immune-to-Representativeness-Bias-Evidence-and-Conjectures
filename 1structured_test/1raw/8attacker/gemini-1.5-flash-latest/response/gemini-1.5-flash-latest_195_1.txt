**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem is a powerful tool for updating our beliefs based on new evidence. In this case, we want to find the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the witness's testimony.

Let's define the events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the witness's statement. Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as tall *given* the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, 0.8 (80%).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being tall. This is given in information a as 0.15 (15%).
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as tall.  This can be calculated using the law of total probability: 
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
