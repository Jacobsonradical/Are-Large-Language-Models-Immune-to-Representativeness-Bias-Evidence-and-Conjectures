**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (the attacker's height) based on new evidence (the witness's statement).

Let's define the events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness says the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We need to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the witness's statement. 

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** Probability the witness says the attacker is tall *given* the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, 80% or 0.8.
* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being tall. This is the proportion of attackers who are tall, which is 15% or 0.15.
* **P(B):** Probability the witness says the attacker is tall. We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
