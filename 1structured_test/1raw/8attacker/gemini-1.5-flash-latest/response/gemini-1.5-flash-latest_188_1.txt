**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about the attacker's height given new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (A) given the witness's testimony (B). This is what we want to find.
* P(B|A): Probability of the witness correctly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller, given they actually are that tall (80% from information a).
* P(A): Prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (15% from information b).
* P(B): Probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller, regardless of their actual height. This is a bit trickier to calculate.

1. **Witness is correct:** 
    * Probability of someone being 185 cm or taller: 0.15 (from information b)

    * Combined probability: 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17

Now, add these probabilities to get P(B): 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
