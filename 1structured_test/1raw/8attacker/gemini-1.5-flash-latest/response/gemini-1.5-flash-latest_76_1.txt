Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Information a:**  Tells us about the general population and the distribution of attackers within that population. It implies that attackers are more likely to be shorter than 185 cm.
* **Information b:** Provides information about a witness's reliability in identifying height under similar conditions.  We know the witness is generally accurate, but makes mistakes 20% of the time.

**Calculating the Probability**

We need to combine the information to determine the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony.  We can use Bayes' Theorem for this:

* **P(A|B):**  The probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller (A) given the witness identified them as such (B). This is what we want to find.
* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would identify someone as 185 cm or taller given they actually are (from information b, this is 80% or 0.8).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being 185 cm or taller (from information a, this is 15% or 0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller (this needs to be calculated).

**Calculating P(B)**

To calculate P(B), we need to consider the two ways the witness could identify someone as 185 cm or taller:

1. **Correctly:**  The person is actually 185 cm or taller (probability = 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12)
2. **Incorrectly:** The person is shorter than 185 cm (probability = 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17)

Therefore, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Now we can calculate P(A|B):

```
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
```
