Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information:**

* **Information a:** This gives us the witness's reliability in identifying height. They are correct 80% of the time, but incorrect 20% of the time.
* **Information b:** This tells us the distribution of attackers based on height.  Most attackers (85%) are under 185 cm, while a smaller percentage (15%) are 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony. Here's the formula:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(A|B):**  Probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (A) given the witness's testimony (B).  This is what we want to find.
* **P(B|A):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller given the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller (the witness's accuracy). This is 0.8 (80% from information a).
* **P(A):**  Prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (from information b). This is 0.15 (15%).
* **P(B):**  Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller. This needs to be calculated.

**Calculating P(B):**

We need to consider both scenarios:

1. **Scenario 1: Attacker is 185 cm or taller:**
   * Probability of this scenario: 0.15 (from information b)
   * Probability of witness correctly identifying them: 0.8 (from information a)
   * Total probability of this scenario: 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12

2. **Scenario 2: Attacker is under 185 cm:**
   * Probability of this scenario: 0.85 (from information b)
   * Probability of witness incorrectly identifying them as 185 cm or taller: 0.2 (from information a)
   * Total probability of this scenario: 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17

**P(B) = Probability of scenario 1 + Probability of scenario 2 = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29**

Now we can plug everything into Bayes' Theorem:
