Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Information "a":**  This tells us the population is split evenly in terms of height (under 185 cm vs. 185 cm or taller). However, most attackers (85%) are in the shorter group. This suggests a bias in attackers towards being shorter.
* **Information "b":** This gives us the witness's reliability. They're correct 80% of the time, but they are wrong 20% of the time.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being tall given the witness's testimony:

P(Tall | Witness) = [P(Witness | Tall) * P(Tall)] / P(Witness)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(Tall | Witness):** This is what we want to find â€“ the probability the attacker is tall *given* the witness said they were tall.
* **P(Witness | Tall):** The probability the witness would identify someone as tall *given* they are actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% (0.8).
* **P(Tall):** The prior probability of an attacker being tall. This is 15% (0.15) from information "a".
* **P(Witness):** The probability of the witness identifying someone as tall. We need to calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(Witness) = P(Witness | Tall) * P(Tall) + P(Witness | Short) * P(Short)
    * P(Witness | Short) = 0.2 (the witness's error rate)
    * P(Short) = 0.85 (from information "a")

**Calculation**

1. **Calculate P(Witness):**
   P(Witness) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.25

2. **Apply Bayes' Theorem:**
   P(Tall | Witness) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.25 = 0.48

**Answer**
