Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information:**

* **Information a:**  The witness is not perfect. They have a 80% chance of correctly identifying someone's height and a 20% chance of making a mistake.
* **Information b:**  While the population is roughly split in terms of height, attackers are disproportionately shorter (85% vs. 15%).

**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being tall given the witness's testimony.

Let:

* **A:** Event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** Event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the witness said they are tall.

Bayes' Theorem states: 
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** Probability of the witness saying the attacker is tall given they actually are tall. This is the witness's accuracy: 0.80 
* **P(A):**  Prior probability of the attacker being tall. This is the percentage of attackers who are tall: 0.15
* **P(B):** Probability of the witness saying the attacker is tall. This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Calculation:**

P(A|B) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29  â‰ˆ 0.414
