**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to combine these pieces of information and estimate the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. 

Let's define the following events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given the attacker *is* actually 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% (0.8).
* **P(A):** The prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller. This is 15% (0.15) based on the population information.
* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller. This requires a bit more calculation:
    *  P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A) 
    *  P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
