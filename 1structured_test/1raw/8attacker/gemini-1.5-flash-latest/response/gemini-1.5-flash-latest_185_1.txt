**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us combine prior information with new evidence to calculate a posterior probability.  

Let:

*  A = Event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
*  B = Event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A) = 0.8:**  The probability the witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller given they *are* 185 cm or taller (from information b).
