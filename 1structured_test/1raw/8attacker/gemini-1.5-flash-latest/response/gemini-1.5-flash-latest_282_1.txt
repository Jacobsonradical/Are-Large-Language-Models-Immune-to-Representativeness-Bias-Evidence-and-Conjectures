* **Information b:** Provides information about the witness's reliability in judging height. They are correct 80% of the time, but misidentify height 20% of the time.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller, given the witness's testimony.

Let's define our events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):**  The probability the witness identifies the attacker as tall, given the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 0.8 (80%).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being tall. This is the percentage of attackers who are tall, which is 0.15 (15%) from information 'a'.
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as tall.  This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
   * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
   * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29 

**Calculation**

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.414
