**Calculation:**

We'll use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the updated probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller, given the witness's testimony.

* **Let A** represent the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Let B** represent the event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A)**: Probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, 0.8 (80%).
* **P(A)**: Prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller. This is the base rate from information "b", 0.15 (15%).
* **P(B)**: Overall probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.  We need to calculate this.

To calculate P(B), we use the law of total probability:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

* **P(B|not A)**: Probability the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is *not* 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's error rate, 0.2 (20%).
* **P(not A)**: Prior probability the attacker is *not* 185 cm or taller. This is the complement of P(A), which is 0.85 (85%).

P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.26
