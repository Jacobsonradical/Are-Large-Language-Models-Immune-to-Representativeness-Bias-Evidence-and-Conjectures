Here's how we can break down the problem to estimate the probability:

**Understanding the Information:**

* **Information a:** Tells us that the population is roughly split 50/50 in terms of height (under 185 cm and 185 cm or taller).  However, attackers are disproportionately shorter (85% under 185 cm).
* **Information b:** Introduces a witness who is not perfectly reliable.  They correctly identify height 80% of the time.

**Combining the Information:**

We need to consider both the population distribution and the witness's reliability.  Here's a way to think about it:

1. **Prior Probability:** Before the witness statement, the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller is 15% (based on information "a"). This is our "prior" probability.

2. **Likelihood:**  The witness said the attacker was 185 cm or taller. The likelihood of this statement being true if the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller is 80% (the witness's accuracy).

3. **Likelihood (false positive):**  The likelihood of the witness saying the attacker was 185 cm or taller if the attacker is actually shorter than 185 cm is 20% (the witness's error rate).

4. **Bayes' Theorem:** We can use Bayes' Theorem to update our prior probability based on the witness's statement.  The formula is:

   P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

   Where:
   * P(A|B):  Probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (A) given the witness statement (B) 
   * P(B|A): Likelihood of the witness saying the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that they actually are (80%)
   * P(A): Prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (15%)
   * P(B):  Probability of the witness saying the attacker is 185 cm or taller (we need to calculate this)

5. **Calculating P(B):** 
   * P(B) can be calculated using the law of total probability:
     P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
     P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29
