Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Information a:**  This tells us the attacker is more likely to be shorter than 185 cm (85% probability) than taller (15% probability), assuming we have no other information.
* **Information b:** This provides the witness's reliability. They are correct about 80% of the time and incorrect 20% of the time.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the updated probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller, given the witness's statement.

Let:

*  A = Event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
*  B = Event that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each part:

* **P(B|A):**  The probability the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller *given* they are actually 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% (0.8).
* **P(A):**  The prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller. From information a, this is 15% (0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller (regardless of their actual height). We need to calculate this using the Law of Total Probability: 
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.25

**Calculation**

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.25 = 0.48
