**Understanding the Information**

* **Information a:**  This tells us that the population is roughly split 50/50 between people shorter than 185 cm and those 185 cm or taller. However, attackers are disproportionately shorter (85% vs. 15%).
* **Information b:** This tells us the witness has a relatively good ability to judge height, but they are not perfect (80% accuracy).

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being tall given the witness's testimony.

* **Let A be the event "attacker is 185 cm or taller".**
* **Let B be the event "witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller".**

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is tall given the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** Probability of the witness identifying someone as tall, given they are actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy rate: 0.8
* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being tall. This is the proportion of the population that is tall, which is approximately 0.5.
* **P(B):**  Probability of the witness identifying someone as tall. This requires considering both true positives and false positives:
    *  True positives:  P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.8 * 0.5 = 0.4
    *  False positives: P(B|¬A) * P(¬A) = 0.2 * 0.5 = 0.1 (where ¬A means "attacker is not tall")
    *  P(B) = 0.4 + 0.1 = 0.5

**Calculating the Probability**

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.5) / 0.5 = 0.8

**Answer:**

{probability: 0.8}
