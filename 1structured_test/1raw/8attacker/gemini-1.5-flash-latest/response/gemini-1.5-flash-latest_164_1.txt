* **B:** The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is taller than 185 cm given the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as taller than 185 cm given they *actually are* taller than 185 cm. This is the witness's accuracy, given as 80% from information "b".
