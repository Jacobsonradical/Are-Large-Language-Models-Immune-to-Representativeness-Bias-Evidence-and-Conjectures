**Understanding the Information**

* **Information a:**  This tells us about the general population distribution of heights and the likelihood of attackers being in each height category.

**Calculating the Probability**

We can use Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony:

**P(Attacker Tall | Witness Says Tall) = [P(Witness Says Tall | Attacker Tall) * P(Attacker Tall)] / P(Witness Says Tall)**

Let's break down each term:

* **P(Attacker Tall | Witness Says Tall):** This is what we want to find – the probability that the attacker was tall given the witness said they were tall.
* **P(Witness Says Tall | Attacker Tall):** This is the witness's accuracy in identifying tall people, which is 80% (given in information b).
* **P(Attacker Tall):** This is the prior probability of an attacker being tall, which is 15% (given in information a).
* **P(Witness Says Tall):** This is the overall probability of the witness saying someone is tall.  We need to calculate this using the law of total probability.

**Calculating P(Witness Says Tall)**

* **Scenario 1:** The attacker is tall (15% probability). The witness correctly identifies them as tall with 80% probability: 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12
* **Scenario 2:** The attacker is short (85% probability). The witness incorrectly identifies them as tall with 20% probability: 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17

**P(Witness Says Tall) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29**

**Putting it all together:**

**P(Attacker Tall | Witness Says Tall) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29  ≈ 0.41**
