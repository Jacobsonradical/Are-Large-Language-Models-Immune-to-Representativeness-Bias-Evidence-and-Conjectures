This scenario is well-suited to Bayes' Theorem, which helps update our beliefs based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(A|B):**  The probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (event A) given the witness's testimony (event B). This is what we want to find.
* **P(B|A):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller, given the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy rate (80%).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. This is the proportion of attackers in the population who are taller (15%).
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller. This is a bit more complex and requires considering both scenarios:

    * **Scenario 1:**  The attacker is taller and the witness is correct (80% * 15% = 12%).
    * **Scenario 2:** The attacker is shorter and the witness is wrong (20% * 85% = 17%).
    * **P(B) = 12% + 17% = 29%**
