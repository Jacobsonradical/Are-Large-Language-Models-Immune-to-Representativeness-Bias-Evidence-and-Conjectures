- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- B: The witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identified the height 80% of the time)
P(A) = 0.15 (15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller)

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.37 = 0.324
