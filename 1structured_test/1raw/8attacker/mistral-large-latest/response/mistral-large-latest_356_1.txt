- \( B \): The event that the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller.

- \( P(A|B) = 0.8 \) (The witness correctly identifies height 80% of the time when the attacker is 185 cm or taller.)
- \( P(A|\neg B) = 0.2 \) (The witness incorrectly identifies height 20% of the time when the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.)
- \( P(B) = 0.15 \) (Only 15% of attackers are 185 cm or taller.)
- \( P(\neg B) = 0.85 \) (85% of attackers are shorter than 185 cm.)

We want to find \( P(B|A) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B) \cdot P(B)}{P(A)} \]

First, we need to find \( P(A) \), the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller:
\[ P(A) = P(A|B) \cdot P(B) + P(A|\neg B) \cdot P(\neg B) \]
\[ P(A) = 0.8 \cdot 0.15 + 0.2 \cdot 0.85 \]
\[ P(A) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(A) = 0.29 \]

Now, we can calculate \( P(B|A) \):
\[ P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B) \cdot P(B)}{P(A)} \]
\[ P(B|A) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(B|A) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
\[ P(B|A) \approx 0.4138 \]
