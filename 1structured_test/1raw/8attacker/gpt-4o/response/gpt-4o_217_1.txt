To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we will apply Bayes' Theorem. This theorem helps us update the probability of a hypothesis based on new evidence.

Given:
1. \(P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.80\)
2. \(P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Attacker} < 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.20\)
3. \(P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.15\)
4. \(P(\text{Attacker} < 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.85\)

We first calculate the total probability \(P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm})\):

\[ P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) \cdot P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) + P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Attacker} < 185 \text{ cm}) \cdot P(\text{Attacker} < 185 \text{ cm}) \]

\[ P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(\text{Witness says } \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = 0.29 \]

Now use Bayes' Theorem to find \(P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm})\):

\[P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = \frac{P(\text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) \cdot P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm})}{P(\text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm})}\]

\[P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = \frac{0.80 \cdot 0.15}{0.29}\]

\[P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) = \frac{0.12}{0.29}\]

\[P(\text{Attacker} \geq 185 \text{ cm} \mid \text{Witness says} \geq 185 \text{ cm}) \approx 0.41 \]

Thus, the estimate of the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller is:

{probability: 0.41}
