To estimate the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller, we need to use Bayes' Theorem. Bayes' Theorem helps us update the probability of a hypothesis given new evidence.

Let's define our events:
- \(T\): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \(\neg T\): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \(W\): The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

From the given data:
- Prior probability, \( P(T) = 0.15 \)
- Prior probability, \( P(\neg T) = 0.85 \)
- Probability of identifying height correctly if the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller, \( P(W|T) = 0.80 \)
- Probability of identifying height incorrectly if the attacker is shorter than 185 cm, \( P(W|\neg T) = 0.20 \)

We need to find \( P(T|W) \), the posterior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(T|W) = \frac{P(W|T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W)} \]

Where \( P(W) \) can be calculated using the law of total probability:
\[ P(W) = P(W|T) \cdot P(T) + P(W|\neg T) \cdot P(\neg T) \]

Substituting the given values:
\[ P(W) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Now we can find \( P(T|W) \):
\[ P(T|W) = \frac{0.80 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(T|W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
\[ P(T|W) \approx 0.4138 \]

Therefore, the estimate of the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller is approximately \(0.4138\).
