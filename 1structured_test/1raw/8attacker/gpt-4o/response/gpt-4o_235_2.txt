- \( H_1 \): Attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( H_2 \): Attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

- \( P(\text{ID} | H_1) = 0.80 \) (witness correctly identifies someone 185 cm or taller).
- \( P(\text{ID} | H_2) = 0.20 \) (witness incorrectly identifies someone shorter than 185 cm as 185 cm or taller).

- \( P(H_2) = 0.85 \) (prior probability that an attacker is shorter than 185 cm).

We are interested in \( P(H_1 | \text{ID}) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Using Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(H_1 | \text{ID}) = \frac{P(\text{ID} | H_1) \cdot P(H_1)}{P(\text{ID})} \]

\[ P(\text{ID}) = P(\text{ID} | H_1) \cdot P(H_1) + P(\text{ID} | H_2) \cdot P(H_2) \]
\[ P(\text{ID}) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(\text{ID}) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(\text{ID}) = 0.29 \]

\[ P(H_1 | \text{ID}) = \frac{(0.80 \cdot 0.15)}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H_1 | \text{ID}) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H_1 | \text{ID}) \approx 0.4138 \]
