To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' Theorem.

Let's define:
- \( T \): Attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( T' \): Attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W \): Witness identifies attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Given:
- \( P(T) = 0.5 \)
- \( P(T') = 0.5 \)
- \( P(\text{person under 185 cm attacks}) = 0.85 \)
- \( P(\text{person 185 cm or taller attacks}) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(W | T) = 0.80 \)
- \( P(W | T') = 0.20 \)

Using Bayes' Theorem:

\[ P(T | W) = \frac{P(W | T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W | T) \cdot P(T) + P(W | T') \cdot P(T')} \]

Calculate the components:
- \( P(W \cap T) = P(W | T) \cdot P(T) = 0.80 \times 0.50 = 0.40 \)
- \( P(W \cap T') = P(W | T') \cdot P(T') = 0.20 \times 0.50 = 0.10 \)

Therefore:

\[ P(T | W) = \frac{0.40}{0.40 + 0.10} = \frac{0.40}{0.50} = 0.80 \]

So,

\[ \{probability: 0.8\} \]
