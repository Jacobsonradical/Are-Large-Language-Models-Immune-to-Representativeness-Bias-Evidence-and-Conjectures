To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' Theorem. Bayes' Theorem allows us to update the probability of a hypothesis based on new evidence.

Let's define the events as follows:
- \( H1 \): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( H2 \): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W \): The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We are given the following information:
- The probability that the witness correctly identifies someone’s height as 185 cm or taller when they are 185 cm or taller is 80%. \( P(W | H1) = 0.8 \)
- The probability that the witness incorrectly identifies someone’s height as 185 cm or taller when they are shorter than 185 cm is 20%. \( P(W | H2) = 0.2 \)
- The prior likelihood of an attacker being 185 cm or taller is 15%. \( P(H1) = 0.15 \)
- The prior likelihood of an attacker being shorter than 185 cm is 85%. \( P(H2) = 0.85 \)

We want to find the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified the attacker as such: \( P(H1 | W) \).

By Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(H1 | W) = \frac{P(W | H1) \cdot P(H1)}{P(W)} \]

First, we need to compute \( P(W) \), the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller:

\[ P(W) = P(W | H1) \cdot P(H1) + P(W | H2) \cdot P(H2) \]

Substituting the given values:

\[ P(W) = 0.8 \cdot 0.15 + 0.2 \cdot 0.85 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.29 \]

Now we can compute \( P(H1 | W) \):

\[ P(H1 | W) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H1 | W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H1 | W) \approx 0.4138 \]
