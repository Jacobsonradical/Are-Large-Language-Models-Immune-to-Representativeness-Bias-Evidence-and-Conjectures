Let's use Bayes' theorem to answer this question. We need to calculate the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given both the prior probability of being 185 cm or taller (15% of attackers) and the probability of the witness correctly identifying the height (80% accuracy).

Bayes' theorem states:

\[ P(A | B) = \frac{P(B | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

Where:
- \( A \) is the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( B \) is the event that the witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

First, let's define the probabilities we know:
- \( P(A) = 0.15 \) (the prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller).
- \( P(\neg A) = 0.85 \) (the prior probability that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm).
- \( P(B | A) = 0.8 \) (the probability that the witness correctly identifies a person of 185 cm or taller as such).
- \( P(B | \neg A) = 0.2 \) (the probability that the witness incorrectly identifies a person shorter than 185 cm as being 185 cm or taller).

Now, we can find \( P(B) \), the total probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller. This is done using the law of total probability:

\[ P(B) = P(B | A) \cdot P(A) + P(B | \neg A) \cdot P(\neg A) \]

Substitute the known values:

\[ P(B) = (0.8 \cdot 0.15) + (0.2 \cdot 0.85) \]

\[ P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]

\[ P(B) = 0.29 \]

Now, we can use Bayes' theorem to find \( P(A | B) \):

\[ P(A | B) = \frac{P(B | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

\[ P(A | B) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]

\[ P(A | B) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]

\[ P(A | B) \approx 0.4138 \]

Thus, the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, given the witness's identification, is approximately 0.4138.
