Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define:**

* Event A: The attacker is 185cm or taller.
* Event B: The witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller.

**We are given:**

* **Prior probabilities:** 
    * P(A) = 0.15 (15% of attackers are 185cm or taller)
    * P(not A) = 0.85 (85% of attackers are shorter than 185cm)
* **Likelihoods (based on witness reliability):**
    * P(B|A) = 0.8 (Witness correctly identifies tall people 80% of the time)
    * P(B|not A) = 0.2 (Witness incorrectly identifies short people as tall 20% of the time)

**We want to find:** P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is actually tall given the witness said so.

**We can use Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

To find P(B), we use the law of total probability:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Now we can calculate P(A|B):**

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
