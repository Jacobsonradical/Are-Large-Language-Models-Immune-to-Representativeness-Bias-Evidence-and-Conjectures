**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

* **Let "B" be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller.**

We want to find **P(A|B): the probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller *given* the witness said they were.**

**Bayes' Theorem states:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would say the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the attacker actually is. This is the witness's reliability in this scenario â€“ 80% or 0.8.
