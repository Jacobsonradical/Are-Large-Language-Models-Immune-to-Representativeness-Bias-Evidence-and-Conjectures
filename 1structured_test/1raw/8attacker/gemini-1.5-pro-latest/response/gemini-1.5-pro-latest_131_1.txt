Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

*  Event A: The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
*  Event B: The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.**

**We know:**

* **P(B|A) = 0.8** (The witness correctly identifies tall people 80% of the time)
* **P(B| not A) = 0.2** (The witness incorrectly identifies short people as tall 20% of the time) 
* **P(A) = 0.15** (Prior probability of an attacker being 185cm or taller)
* **P(not A) = 0.85** (Prior probability of an attacker being shorter than 185cm)

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
