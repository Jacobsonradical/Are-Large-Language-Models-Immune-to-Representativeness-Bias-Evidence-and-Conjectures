Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability (from statement 'a'):**  Before considering the witness, there's a 15% chance the attacker is 185cm or taller based purely on the attacker height distribution.
* **Witness Reliability (from statement 'b'):** The witness is 80% accurate in identifying height in similar situations.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is ideal for updating our belief based on new evidence. Here's how it applies:

Let:

* Event A = Attacker is 185cm or taller
* Event B = Witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states: 
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would say "185cm or taller"  *if* the attacker is actually 185cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy rate for this scenario, which is 80% or 0.8.
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller (from statement 'a'), which is 15% or 0.15.
* **P(B):** The probability the witness would say "185cm or taller"  (regardless of the actual height).  We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
    * Where ~A means "attacker is shorter than 185cm"
    * P(B|~A) = 0.2 (the witness's error rate)
    * P(~A) = 0.85 (from statement 'a')
    * Therefore: P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Calculation**

Now we have all the pieces:
