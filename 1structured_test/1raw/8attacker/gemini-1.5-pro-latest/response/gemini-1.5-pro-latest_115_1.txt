Here's how to break down this probability problem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability (a):** The witness is 80% accurate in judging height under similar conditions. This means they are right 80% of the time, but wrong 20% of the time.
* **Attacker Base Rate (b):** This tells us how common attackers of different heights are:
    * 85% of attackers are shorter than 185cm.
    * 15% of attackers are 185cm or taller. 

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This type of problem is best solved using Bayes' Theorem, which helps calculate conditional probabilities (the probability of something happening given that something else has already happened).

Here's the breakdown:

* **Let "A" be the event that the attacker is 185cm or taller.**
* **Let "B" be the event that the witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller.**

We want to find **P(A|B):** the probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller *given* that the witness said they were.

**Bayes' Theorem Formula:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would say the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually 185cm or taller (This is the witness's accuracy for this height group: 80% or 0.8).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being 185cm or taller (This is the base rate: 15% or 0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability the witness would identify someone as 185cm or taller (We'll need to calculate this).

**Calculating P(B):**

P(B) is the probability the witness says "185cm or taller".  There are two ways this could happen:

1. **True Positive:** The attacker *is* tall, and the witness is *correct*  (0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12) 
2. **False Positive:** The attacker is *short*, but the witness is *incorrect* (0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17)

P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
