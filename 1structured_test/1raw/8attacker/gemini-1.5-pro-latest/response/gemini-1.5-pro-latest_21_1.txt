Let's break down this problem using Bayes' Theorem.

**Here's what we know:**

* **Prior Probability (P(Tall)):** The probability of a random person being 185cm or taller is 0.5 (50%) based on the general population.
* **Likelihood (P(Witness Correct | Tall)):** The probability the witness correctly identifies someone as tall, *given* they are actually tall, is 0.8 (80%).

This is because if they are wrong 20% of the time overall, and the sample is split evenly, they must be wrong 20% of the time when the person is short.
* **Prior Probability (P(Short)):** The probability of a random person being shorter than 185cm is 0.5 (50%) based on the general population.
* **Attacker Profile (P(Tall | Attacker))**: The probability of an attacker being 185cm or taller is 0.15 (15%) based on historical data.
* **Attacker Profile (P(Short | Attacker))**: The probability of an attacker being shorter than 185cm is 0.85 (85%) based on historical data.

**What we want to find:**

* **Posterior Probability (P(Tall | Witness Correct)):**  The probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller, *given* the witness identified them as tall.

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(Tall | Witness Correct) = [P(Witness Correct | Tall) * P(Tall)] / P(Witness Correct)

To calculate the denominator, we use the law of total probability:

P(Witness Correct) = [P(Witness Correct | Tall) * P(Tall)] + [P(Witness Correct | Short) * P(Short)]

2. **P(Tall | Witness Correct):** (0.8 * 0.5) / 0.5 = 0.4 / 0.5 = 0.8
