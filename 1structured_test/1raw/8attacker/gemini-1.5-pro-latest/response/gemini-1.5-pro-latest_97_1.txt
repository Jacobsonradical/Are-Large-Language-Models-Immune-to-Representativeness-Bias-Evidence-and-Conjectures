**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating our beliefs based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B):  The probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller) given that event B (witness said they are tall) has occurred.
* P(B|A): The probability of event B (witness saying they are tall) given that event A (attacker is actually 185cm or taller) is true. This is 0.8 (witness reliability).
* P(A): The prior probability of event A (attacker being 185cm or taller), which is 0.15 (base rate).
* P(B): The probability of event B (witness saying they are tall). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A)P(A) + P(B|~A)P(~A) 
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Calculation**

Now we can plug the values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
