To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness testimonies, we can use Bayesian reasoning.

Let:
\[
T_1 = \text{First witness says the attacker is } \geq 185 \text{ cm}
\]
\[
T_2 = \text{Second witness says the attacker is } < 185 \text{ cm}
\]
\[
H_{\geq185} = \text{Attacker is } \geq 185 \text{ cm}
\]
\[
H_{<185} = \text{Attacker is } < 185 \text{ cm}
\]

We need to estimate:
\[
P(H_{\geq185} | T_1 \text{ and } T_2)
\]

Given:
\[
P(H_{\geq185}) = 0.15
\]
\[
P(H_{<185}) = 0.85
\]
\[
P(T_1 | H_{\geq185}) = 0.8 \quad \text{(first witness is correct 80% of the time)}
\]
\[
P(T_1 | H_{<185}) = 0.2 \quad \text{(first witness errors 20% of the time)}
\]
\[
P(T_2 | H_{<185}) = 0.7 \quad \text{(second witness is correct 70% of the time)}
\]
\[
P(T_2 | H_{\geq185}) = 0.3 \quad \text{(second witness errors 30% of the time)}
\]

We start by using the law of total probability to find \( P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2) \).

\[
P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2) = P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2 | H_{\geq185}) \cdot P(H_{\geq185}) + P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2 | H_{<185}) \cdot P(H_{<185})
\]

Where:
\[
P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2 | H_{\geq185}) = P(T_1 | H_{\geq185}) \cdot P(T_2 | H_{\geq185}) = 0.8 \cdot 0.3 = 0.24
\]
\[
P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2 | H_{<185}) = P(T_1 | H_{<185}) \cdot P(T_2 | H_{<185}) = 0.2 \cdot 0.7 = 0.14
\]

Thus:
\[
P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2) = 0.24 \cdot 0.15 + 0.14 \cdot 0.85 = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155
\]

Now apply Bayes' theorem to find \( P(H_{\geq185} | T_1 \text{ and } T_2) \):
\[
P(H_{\geq185} | T_1 \text{ and } T_2) = \frac{P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2 | H_{\geq185}) \cdot P(H_{\geq185})}{P(T_1 \text{ and } T_2)}
\]
\[
P(H_{\geq185} | T_1 \text{ and } T_2) = \frac{0.24 \cdot 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.23(2)
\]

So the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller, as claimed by the first witness, is approximately 0.232.

\{ \text{probability: 0.232} \}
