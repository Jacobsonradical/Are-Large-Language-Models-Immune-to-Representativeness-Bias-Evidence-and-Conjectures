To estimate the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller given the testimonies of both witnesses, we can apply Bayes' Theorem.

Let's define the events:
- \( T \): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( \neg T \): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W_1 \): The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W_2 \): The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

From the information given:
- \( P(T) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(\neg T) = 0.85 \)
- \( P(W_1 | T) = 0.8 \)
- \( P(W_1 | \neg T) = 0.2 \)
- \( P(W_2 | T) = 0.3 \)
- \( P(W_2 | \neg T) = 0.7 \)

We want to find \( P(T | W_1 \land W_2) \).

First, apply Bayes' Theorem:

\[ P(T | W_1 \land W_2) = \frac{P(W_1 \land W_2 | T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W_1 \land W_2)} \]

To find \( P(W_1 \land W_2) \), we use the law of total probability:

\[ P(W_1 \land W_2) = P(W_1 \land W_2 | T) \cdot P(T) + P(W_1 \land W_2 | \neg T) \cdot P(\neg T) \]

Now, we need to break down this equation:

\[ P(W_1 \land W_2 | T) = P(W_1 | T) \cdot P(W_2 | T) = 0.8 \cdot 0.3 = 0.24 \]
\[ P(W_1 \land W_2 | \neg T) = P(W_1 | \neg T) \cdot P(W_2 | \neg T) = 0.2 \cdot 0.7 = 0.14 \]

Therefore:

\[ P(W_1 \land W_2) = (0.24 \cdot 0.15) + (0.14 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(W_1 \land W_2) = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155 \]

Finally, use Bayes' Theorem to find \( P(T | W_1 \land W_2) \):

\[ P(T | W_1 \land W_2) = \frac{0.24 \cdot 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232 \]

{probability: 0.232}
