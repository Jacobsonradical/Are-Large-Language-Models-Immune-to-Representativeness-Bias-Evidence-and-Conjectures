- \( S \) (short): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

- \( P(T) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(S) = 0.85 \)

  - \( P(W2|\ T) = 0.3 \)       (30% error when the person is tall)

We need to find the probability that the attacker was tall given the witnesses' statements. We use Bayes' theorem for this:

\[
P(T|\ W1, W2) = \frac{P(W1, W2|\ T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W1, W2)}
\]

Where \( P(W1, W2) \) can be expanded as follows:
\[
P(W1, W2) = P(W1, W2|\ T) \cdot P(T) + P(W1, W2|\ S) \cdot P(S)
\]

Considering that the witnesses' assessments are conditional on the actual height:
\[
P(W1, W2|\ T) = P(W1|\ T) \cdot P(W2|\ T) = 0.8 \cdot 0.3 = 0.24
\]

\[
P(W1, W2|\ S) = P(W1|\ S) \cdot P(W2|\ S) = 0.2 \cdot 0.7 = 0.14
\]

Then, we calculate \( P(W1, W2) \):
\[

\]

Finally, applying Bayes' theorem:
\[
P(T|\ W1, W2) = \frac{0.24 \cdot 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232
