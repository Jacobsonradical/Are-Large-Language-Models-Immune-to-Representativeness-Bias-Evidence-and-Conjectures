To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witnesses' testimonies and their accuracy, we can use Bayes' theorem.

Let's define the events as:
- \( A \): Attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- \( \neg A \): Attacker's height is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W1 \): Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W2 \): Witness 2 claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

Given:
- \( P(A) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- \( P(\neg A) = 0.85 \) (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

Witness 1's accuracy:
- \( P(W1|A) = 0.80 \)
- \( P(W1|\neg A) = 0.20 \)

Witness 2's accuracy:
- \( P(W2|A) = 0.30 \)
- \( P(W2|\neg A) = 0.70 \)

We want to find \( P(A|W1 \cap \neg W2) \) - the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that Witness 1 claims they are 185 cm or taller and Witness 2 claims they are shorter than 185 cm.

Using Bayes' theorem:

\[
P(A|W1 \cap \neg W2) = \frac{P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | A) \cdot P(A)}{P(W1 \cap \neg W2)}
\]

First, we need to find \( P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | A) \):

\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | A) = P(W1 | A) \cdot P(\neg W2 | A)
\]
\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | A) = 0.80 \cdot 0.30 = 0.24
\]

Next, we need to find \( P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | \neg A) \):

\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | \neg A) = P(W1 | \neg A) \cdot P(\neg W2 | \neg A)
\]
\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | \neg A) = 0.20 \cdot 0.70 = 0.14
\]

Now we can find \( P(W1 \cap \neg W2) \):

\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2) = P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | A) \cdot P(A) + P(W1 \cap \neg W2 | \neg A) \cdot P(\neg A)
\]
\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2) = 0.24 \cdot 0.15 + 0.14 \cdot 0.85
\]
\[
P(W1 \cap \neg W2) = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155
\]

Finally, we can find \( P(A|W1 \cap \neg W2) \):

\[
P(A|W1 \cap \neg W2) = \frac{0.24 \cdot 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232
\]

Therefore, the estimate of the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, as the first witness claimed, is:

\[
\{ probability: 0.232 \}
\]
