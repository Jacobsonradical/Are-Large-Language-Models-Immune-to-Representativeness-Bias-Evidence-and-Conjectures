To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the testimony of the witnesses and the base rates, we can use Bayes' theorem.

Let's denote:

- \( T \): The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( \neg T \): The event that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W1 \): The first witness's claim.
- \( W2 \): The second witness's claim.

We start with the prior probabilities:

- \( P(T) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(\neg T) = 0.85 \)

The reliability of the witnesses can be described as:

- \( P(W1 \mid T) = 0.8 \)
- \( P(W1 \mid \neg T) = 0.2 \)
- \( P(W2 \mid T) = 0.3 \)
- \( P(W2 \mid \neg T) = 0.7 \)

We want to find \( P(T \mid W1, W2) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given both witnesses' claims. Unfortunately, combining two pieces of testimonial evidence directly can be complex, but for simplicity, let's work only with the more reliable witness, the first, to get an initial estimate.

Using Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(T \mid W1) = \frac{P(W1 \mid T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W1)} \]

Where \( P(W1) \) is the total probability of the first witness's claim:

\[ P(W1) = P(W1 \mid T) \cdot P(T) + P(W1 \mid \neg T) \cdot P(\neg T) \]

Plugging in the values:

\[ P(W1) = (0.8 \cdot 0.15) + (0.2 \cdot 0.85) \]

\[ P(W1) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]

\[ P(W1) = 0.29 \]

Now we can calculate \( P(T \mid W1) \):

\[ P(T \mid W1) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]

\[ P(T \mid W1) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]

\[ P(T \mid W1) \approx 0.414 \]

So, the estimate of the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, based on the first witness's claim, is approximately 0.414.
