To estimate the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayesian reasoning. Let's denote the following events:

- \( T \): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( \neg T \): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W1 \): The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W2 \): The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

Given probabilities:
- \( P(T) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(\neg T) = 0.85 \)
- \( P(W1 | T) = 0.80 \)
- \( P(W1 | \neg T) = 0.20 \)
- \( P(W2 | T) = 0.30 \)
- \( P(W2 | \neg T) = 0.70 \)

We need to find \( P(T | W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) \).

### Step 1: Calculate \( P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) \) for both \( T \) and \( \neg T \)

For \( T \):
\[ P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2 | T) = P(W1 | T) \cdot P(\neg W2 | T) = 0.80 \times 0.70 = 0.56 \]

For \( \neg T \):
\[ P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2 | \neg T) = P(W1 | \neg T) \cdot P(\neg W2 | \neg T) = 0.20 \times 0.30 = 0.06 \]

### Step 2: Calculate \( P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) \)

\[ P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2 | T) \cdot P(T) + P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2 | \neg T) \cdot P(\neg T) \]

\[ P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = (0.56 \times 0.15) + (0.06 \times 0.85) \]

\[ P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = 0.084 + 0.051 = 0.135 \]

### Step 3: Apply Bayes' Theorem

\[ P(T | W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = \frac{P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2 | T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W1 \text{ and } \neg W2)} \]

\[ P(T | W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = \frac{0.56 \times 0.15}{0.135} \]

\[ P(T | W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = \frac{0.084}{0.135} \]

\[ P(T | W1 \text{ and } \neg W2) = \frac{84}{135} = \frac{28}{45} = 0.6222 \]

### Final Answer
