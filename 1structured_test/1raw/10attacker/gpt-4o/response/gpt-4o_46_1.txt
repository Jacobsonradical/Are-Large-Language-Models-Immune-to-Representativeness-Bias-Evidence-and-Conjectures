To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem helps us update our prior beliefs based on new evidence.

Let's denote the events as follows:
- \( H \): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( L \): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W1_{185} \): The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W2_{<185} \): The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

We want to find \( P(H \mid W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the testimony of the two witnesses.

### Given Information
- \( P(H) = 0.15 \)
- \( P(L) = 0.85 \)

For the first witness:
- \( P(W1_{185} \mid H) = 0.80 \)
- \( P(W1_{185} \mid L) = 0.20 \)

For the second witness:
- \( P(W2_{<185} \mid H) = 0.30 \)
- \( P(W2_{<185} \mid L) = 0.70 \)

### Applying Bayes' Theorem
We use Bayes' theorem in conjunction with the law of total probability.

\[ P(H \mid W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) = \frac{P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid H) \cdot P(H)}{P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185})} \]

First, we calculate \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) \) using the law of total probability:
\[ P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) = P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid H) \cdot P(H) + P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid L) \cdot P(L) \]

Let's find \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid H) \) and \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid L) \):

- \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid H) = P(W1_{185} \mid H) \cdot P(W2_{<185} \mid H) = 0.80 \cdot 0.30 = 0.24 \)
- \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185} \mid L) = P(W1_{185} \mid L) \cdot P(W2_{<185} \mid L) = 0.20 \cdot 0.70 = 0.14 \)

Now we can calculate \( P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) \):

\[ P(W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) = (0.24 \cdot 0.15) + (0.14 \cdot 0.85) = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155 \]

Now, using Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(H \mid W1_{185}, W2_{<185}) = \frac{0.24 \cdot 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232 \]

### Answer
{probability: 0.232}
