\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap T) = P(W_1|T) \cdot P(W_2|T) \cdot P(T) \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap T) = 0.80 \cdot 0.30 \cdot 0.15 \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap T) = 0.036 \]

2. **Calculate \(P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap S)\):**
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap S) = P(W_1|S) \cdot P(W_2|S) \cdot P(S) \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap S) = 0.20 \cdot 0.70 \cdot 0.85 \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap S) = 0.119 \]

\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2) = P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap T) + P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap S) \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2) = 0.036 + 0.119 \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2) = 0.155 \]

Finally, we use Bayes' Theorem to find the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, given the witnesses' statements:
\[ P(T|W_1 \cap W_2) = \frac{P(W_1 \cap W_2 \cap T)}{P(W_1 \cap W_2)} \]
\[ P(T|W_1 \cap W_2) = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \]
\[ P(T|W_1 \cap W_2) \approx 0.232 \]
