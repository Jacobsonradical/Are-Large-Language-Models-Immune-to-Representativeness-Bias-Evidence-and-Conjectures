To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' Theorem to find the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, considering the witness statements.

Let's denote the events as follows:
- \( H_{185+} \): The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( H_{<185} \): The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
- \( W1_{185+} \): The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W2_{<185} \): The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

We want to find:
\[ P(H_{185+} \mid W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) \]

Using Bayes' Theorem, we have:
\[ P(H_{185+} \mid W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) = \frac{P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) \cdot P(H_{185+})}{P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185})} \]

First, let's find \( P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) \):
\[ P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) = P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) \cdot P(H_{185+}) + P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{<185}) \cdot P(H_{<185}) \]

Given the witness accuracies and population statistics:
- The probability the first witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller is 0.80, and incorrectly as shorter is 0.20.
- The probability the second witness correctly identifies someone as shorter than 185 cm is 0.70, and incorrectly as 185 cm or taller is 0.30.
- The population probabilities are \( P(H_{185+}) = 0.15 \) and \( P(H_{<185}) = 0.85 \).

Now compute each term:
1. \( P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) = P(W1_{185+} \mid H_{185+}) \cdot P(W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) \)
\[ P(W1_{185+} \mid H_{185+}) = 0.80 \]
\[ P(W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) = 0.30 \]
\[ P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{185+}) = 0.80 \times 0.30 = 0.24 \]

2. \( P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{<185}) = P(W1_{185+} \mid H_{<185}) \cdot P(W2_{<185} \mid H_{<185}) \)
\[ P(W1_{185+} \mid H_{<185}) = 0.20 \]
\[ P(W2_{<185} \mid H_{<185}) = 0.70 \]
\[ P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185} \mid H_{<185}) = 0.20 \times 0.70 = 0.14 \]

Next, use these to find the total probability:
\[ P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) = 0.24 \times 0.15 + 0.14 \times 0.85 \]
\[ P(W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155 \]

Now apply Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(H_{185+} \mid W1_{185+}, W2_{<185}) = \frac{0.24 \times 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232 \]

{ probability: 0.232 }
