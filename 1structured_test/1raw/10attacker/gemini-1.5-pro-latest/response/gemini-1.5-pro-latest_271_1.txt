Here's how to approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define some terms:**

* **Event A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the first witness's testimony.**

We can use Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually that height. This is the witness's accuracy rate for this scenario, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. This is based on the city's population distribution, which is 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness would claim someone is 185 cm or taller in general (regardless of the attacker's actual height). We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
   * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
   * Where ~A means the attacker is *shorter* than 185 cm.
   * We know P(B|A) = 0.8 and P(A) = 0.15.
   * P(B|~A) is the probability the witness incorrectly claims someone is tall when they are short (20% or 0.2).
   * P(~A) is the probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185 cm (85% or 0.85).
