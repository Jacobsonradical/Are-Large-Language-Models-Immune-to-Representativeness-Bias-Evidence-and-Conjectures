**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability (P(H)):** This is the probability of a person in the city being 185cm or taller. We know this is 15% or 0.15.

* **Witness 1 Inaccuracy (P(E|~H)):** This is the probability that witness 1 would claim someone is 185cm or taller *given* the person is actually *shorter* than that height. This is 20% or 0.2.
* **We want to find P(H|E):** The probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller *given* that witness 1 said they were.

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem states: 
P(H|E) = [P(E|H) * P(H)] / P(E)

To use this, we need to calculate P(E), the overall probability of witness 1 claiming someone is 185cm or taller. This can be calculated using:

P(E) = [P(E|H) * P(H)] + [P(E|~H) * P(~H)]
Where P(~H) is the probability of someone being shorter than 185cm (85% or 0.85).
