Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Let:**

*  Event A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
*  Event B: The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the first witness's claim.**

**From the information provided:**

* **P(A) = 0.15** (Prior probability: 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
* **P(B|A) = 0.80** (Witness 1's accuracy: If the attacker is actually tall, they identify them correctly 80% of the time) 
* **P(B| not A) = 0.20** (Witness 1's error rate: If the attacker is actually shorter, they incorrectly identify them as tall 20% of the time)
* **P(not A) = 0.85** (Prior probability: 85% of the population is shorter than 185cm)

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B) 

To find P(B), the probability of the witness claiming someone is tall, we use the law of total probability:

P(B) =  [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29
