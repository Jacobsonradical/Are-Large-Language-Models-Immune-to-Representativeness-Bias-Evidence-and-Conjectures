**Let's define the events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
* **W1:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **W2:** The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

* **P(W1|B) = 0.20** (Probability witness 1 incorrectly identifies short height as tall)
* **P(W2|A) = 0.30** (Probability witness 2 incorrectly identifies tall height as short)
* **P(W2|B) = 0.70** (Probability witness 2 correctly identifies short height)

**Using Bayes' Theorem and the Law of Total Probability, we can break this down:**

P(A|W1, W2) = [P(W1, W2|A) * P(A)] / P(W1, W2) 

Where:

* P(W1, W2) = [P(W1, W2|A) * P(A)] + [P(W1, W2|B) * P(B)]

* P(W1, W2|A) = P(W1|A) * P(W2|A) = 0.80 * 0.30 = 0.24
* P(W1, W2|B) = P(W1|B) * P(W2|B) = 0.20 * 0.70 = 0.14

Now we can calculate:

* P(W1, W2) = (0.24 * 0.15) + (0.14 * 0.85) = 0.155
