Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define some events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **B:** The attacker is shorter than 185cm.
* **W1:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **W2:** The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185cm.

* **P(A) = 0.15** (Prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller)
* **P(B) = 0.85** (Prior probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185cm)
* **P(W1|A) = 0.8** (Probability the first witness correctly identifies someone as 185cm or taller)
* **P(W1|B) = 0.2** (Probability the first witness incorrectly identifies someone shorter as 185cm or taller)
* **P(W2|A) = 0.3** (Probability the second witness incorrectly identifies someone taller as shorter than 185cm)
* **P(W2|B) = 0.7** (Probability the second witness correctly identifies someone shorter than 185cm)

**We want to find P(A|W1), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the first witness's testimony.**

We can use Bayes' theorem:

**P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1)**

**P(W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] + [P(W1|B) * P(B)]**
