Here's how to approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define some terms:**

*  **Event A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
*  **Event B:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

*  **P(B|A):** Probability the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker actually is 185 cm or taller (witness reliability).
*  **P(B|not A):** Probability the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually shorter than 185 cm (witness error).
*  **P(not A):** Prior probability of the attacker being shorter than 185 cm.

**We want to find P(A|B):** The probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the first witness's claim.

**Here's how to apply Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)]

* P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)

* P(B|A) = 0.80 (The first witness correctly identifies heights 80% of the time)
* P(B|not A) = 0.20 (The first witness incorrectly identifies heights 20% of the time)
