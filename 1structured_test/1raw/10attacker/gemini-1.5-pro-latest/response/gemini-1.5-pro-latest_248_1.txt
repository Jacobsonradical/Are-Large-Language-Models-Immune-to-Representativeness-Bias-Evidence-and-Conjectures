Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define:**

* **A:** The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The event that the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the first witness's statement.**

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness claims the attacker is tall, given the attacker *is* tall. This is the witness's accuracy for tall people, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being 185 cm or taller. We can use the city's height distribution for this: 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness claims the attacker is tall (regardless of the attacker's actual height).  We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
   * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
   * Where ~A means "attacker is shorter than 185 cm".
   * P(B|~A) is the probability the witness claims "tall" when the attacker is short (their error rate for short people), which is 20% or 0.2.
   * P(~A) is the probability of a person being shorter than 185 cm in the city, which is 85% or 0.85.

* P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
