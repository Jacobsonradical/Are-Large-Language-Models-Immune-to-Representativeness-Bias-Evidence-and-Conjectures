Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define some terms:**

* **A:** The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The event that the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given the first witness said so.**

**We can use Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

**Let's break down each term:**

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness says "tall" given the attacker *is* tall. This is the witness's accuracy for tall people, which is 80% or 0.8.
* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being tall. We're given this is 15% of the population, or 0.15.
* **P(B):** The probability the witness says "tall" (regardless of actual height).  We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
    * Where ~A is the event the attacker is *shorter* than 185 cm.
    * We know P(B|~A) is the witness's error rate for short people, which is 20% or 0.2.
    * P(~A) is the prior probability of an attacker being shorter, which is 85% or 0.85.

**Calculations:**

1. **P(B):**  (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

2. **P(A|B):** (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
