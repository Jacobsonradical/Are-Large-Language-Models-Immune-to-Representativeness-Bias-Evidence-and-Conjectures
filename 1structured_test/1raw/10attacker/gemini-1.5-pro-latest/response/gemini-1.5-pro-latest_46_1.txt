Here's how to approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**1. Define Events**

* Let "S" be the event that the attacker is truly shorter than 185 cm.
* Let "W1" be the event that the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

    * P(S) = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

* We're given information about the witness's accuracy:

    * P(W1 | S) = 0.20 (The first witness incorrectly identifies "tall" 20% of the time when the person is actually short)

**4. Bayes' Theorem**

We want to find P(T | W1), the probability the attacker is tall *given* the first witness's statement. Bayes' Theorem states:

P(T | W1) = [P(W1 | T) * P(T)] / P(W1)

To find P(W1), the probability of the witness saying "tall", we use the law of total probability:

P(W1) = [P(W1 | T) * P(T)] + [P(W1 | S) * P(S)]
