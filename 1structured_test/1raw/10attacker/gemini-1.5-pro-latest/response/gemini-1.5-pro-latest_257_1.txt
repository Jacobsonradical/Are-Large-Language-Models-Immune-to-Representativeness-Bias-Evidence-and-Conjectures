**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is a way to update our beliefs based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A happening given that event B happened.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B happening given that event A happened.
* P(A):  Prior probability of event A happening.
* P(B):  Prior probability of event B happening.

**Let's define our events:**

* Event A: The attacker is 185cm or taller.
* Event B: Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given Witness 1's claim.**

Here's how the probabilities break down:

* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller (base rate) = 0.15
* **P(B|A):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming the attacker is 185cm or taller GIVEN the attacker is actually 185cm or taller (W1's accuracy) = 0.80
* **P(B| not A):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming the attacker is 185cm or taller GIVEN the attacker is actually SHORTER than 185cm (W1's error rate) = 0.20
* **P(not A):** Prior probability of the attacker being shorter than 185cm = 0.85

To find P(B), the probability of Witness 1 claiming the attacker is tall (regardless of the attacker's actual height), we use the law of total probability:

P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Now we can apply Bayes' Theorem:**
