Here's how we can approach this problem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability:**  The chance of a random person in the city being 185cm or taller is 15% (0.15), based on the city's height distribution.
* **Witness Reliability:** We have information about how reliable each witness is in judging height. This is crucial for updating our belief based on their statements.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating probabilities based on new evidence.  We'll use a simplified version for clarity:

```
P(A|B) ≈ (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B) 
```

Where:

* P(A|B): The probability of event A happening given that event B happened. In our case, this is the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller (A) given the witness testimonies (B).
* P(B|A): The probability of observing the evidence (B) given that the event (A) is true.
* P(A): The prior probability of event A happening (attacker being 185cm or taller).
* P(B): The probability of observing the evidence (B) – this can get a bit complex to calculate directly.

**Calculations**

Let's break down the calculation for the probability that the attacker is 185cm or taller given the witness testimonies:

* **Event A:** Attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **Event B:** Witness 1 says "taller", Witness 2 says "shorter."

1. **P(A):** Prior probability of attacker being 185cm or taller = 0.15

2. **P(B|A):**  Probability of the witness statements if the attacker *is* 185cm or taller:
   * Witness 1 would be correct (80% chance).
   * Witness 2 would be incorrect (30% chance).
   * P(B|A) = 0.80 * 0.30 = 0.24

3. **P(B):** This is the probability of getting those specific witness statements regardless of the attacker's actual height. It's a bit trickier to calculate directly, but we can approximate.  Since the prior probability of a person being taller is low (15%), the chance of these mixed witness statements is likely somewhat low.

4. **Approximation:**  Instead of calculating P(B) precisely, we can make a reasonable assumption:  Since the first witness is more accurate (80% vs 70%) and their statement aligns with the attacker being taller, it's more likely that the attacker is indeed taller. 

**Conclusion**

While we don't have all the information for a precise Bayesian calculation, considering the evidence:

* **More reliable witness supports the attacker being taller.**

It's reasonable to estimate that the probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller is higher than the prior probability but likely not extremely high.
