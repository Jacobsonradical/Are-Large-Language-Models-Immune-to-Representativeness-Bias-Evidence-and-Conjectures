We need to use Bayesian reasoning to update our prior belief about the attacker's height (based on population data) with the new information from the witnesses.

* **Event B:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B):**  The probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the first witness's statement.

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(B|A):** Probability the witness would claim the attacker is 185cm+ *given* the attacker actually is 185cm+ (This is the witness's reliability when they say "taller": 80% or 0.8)

* **P(B):** Probability of the witness claiming "taller" (This needs further calculation).
