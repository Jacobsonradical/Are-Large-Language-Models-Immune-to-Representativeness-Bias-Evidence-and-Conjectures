Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Problem**

We need to find the probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller *given* that the first witness said so. This means we need to consider both the witness's accuracy and the general population's height distribution.

**Let's Define Events:**

*  **A:** The attacker is 185cm or taller.
*  **B:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185cm or taller.

**We Need to Calculate: P(A|B)**  (Probability of A given B)

**Bayes' Theorem:** 
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

**Let's break down each term:**

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness says "185cm or taller" *given* the attacker actually is. This is the witness's accuracy for this height category, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller.  This is given as 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness says "185cm or taller" (regardless of actual height). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) =  [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|~A) * P(~A)] 
    * Where ~A means "attacker is *shorter* than 185cm"
    * P(B|~A) is the probability the witness says "taller" when the attacker is actually shorter. This is the witness's error rate for shorter people, which is 20% or 0.2.
    * P(~A) is the probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185cm, which is 85% or 0.85.

   P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
