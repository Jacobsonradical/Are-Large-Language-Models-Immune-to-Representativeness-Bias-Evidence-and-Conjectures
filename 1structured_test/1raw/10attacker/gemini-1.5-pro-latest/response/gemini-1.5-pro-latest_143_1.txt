Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define the events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
* **W1:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **W2:** The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

**We are asked to find P(A|W1), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the first witness's testimony.**

**We know the following:**

* **Prior probabilities (from population data):**
    * P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
    * P(B) = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm) 
* **Witness reliability (likelihoods):**
    * P(W1|A) = 0.80 (First witness correctly identifies tall people 80% of the time)
    * P(W1|B) = 0.20 (First witness incorrectly identifies short people as tall 20% of the time) 
    * P(W2|A) = 0.20 (Second witness incorrectly identifies tall people as short 20% of the time)
    * P(W2|B) = 0.70 (Second witness correctly identifies short people 70% of the time)

**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1)

To find P(W1), we use the law of total probability:

P(W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] + [P(W1|B) * P(B)]
P(W1) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Now we can calculate P(A|W1):**

P(A|W1) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29
