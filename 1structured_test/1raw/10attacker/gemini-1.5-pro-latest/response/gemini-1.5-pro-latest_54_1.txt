Here's how to approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**Let's define some terms:**

* **A:** The event that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The event that the first witness claimed the attacker was 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker was 185cm or taller given the first witness's statement.**

**We can use Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would claim the attacker was 185cm or taller *given* the attacker was actually that tall. This is the witness's accuracy for this scenario, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of a person in the city being 185cm or taller.  This is given as 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness would claim the attacker was 185cm or taller (regardless of the attacker's actual height). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
    * Where ~A is the event the attacker is *shorter* than 185cm.
    * We know P(B|~A) = 0.2 (the witness's error rate when the person is shorter than 185cm)
    * We know P(~A) = 0.85 (from the city's height distribution)

   * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
