Here's how to break down this probability problem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability:**  We know how accurate each witness is likely to be.
* **Base Rate:** We know the general height distribution in the city. 

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is perfect for this kind of problem. It helps us calculate updated probabilities based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A happening given that event B happened.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B happening given that event A happened.
* P(A): Prior probability of event A happening.
* P(B): Prior probability of event B happening.

**Let's Define Our Events**

* **Event A:** Attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **Event B:** First witness claims the attacker is 185cm or taller.

**What We Need to Calculate**

* **P(A|B):** The probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* that the first witness said so. This is what we want to find.

* **P(B|A):**  The probability the first witness would say the attacker is 185cm or taller *if* the attacker actually was. This is the witness's accuracy for taller individuals (80% or 0.8).

* **P(A):** The prior probability of an attacker being 185cm or taller. This is the base rate in the city (15% or 0.15).

* **P(B):** The probability of the first witness claiming someone is 185cm or taller (regardless of whether they actually are). This requires a bit more calculation:

    * The witness could correctly identify a tall person (0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12 probability).
    * Or, they could incorrectly identify a shorter person as tall (0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17 probability). 
    * So, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Putting it All Together**
