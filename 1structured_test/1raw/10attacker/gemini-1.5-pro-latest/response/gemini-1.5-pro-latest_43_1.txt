Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Let's define the events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The attacker is shorter than 185 cm.
* **W1:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **W2:** The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm.

**We are given:**

* **P(A) = 0.15** (Prior probability: 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
* **P(B) = 0.85** (Prior probability: 85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)
* **P(W1|A) = 0.80** (Likelihood: Witness 1 correctly identifies tall people 80% of the time)
* **P(W1|B) = 0.20** (Likelihood: Witness 1 incorrectly identifies short people as tall 20% of the time)
* **P(W2|A) = 0.20** (Likelihood: Witness 2 incorrectly identifies tall people as short 20% of the time)
* **P(W2|B) = 0.70** (Likelihood: Witness 2 correctly identifies short people 70% of the time)

**We want to find P(A|W1), the probability the attacker is tall given the first witness's testimony.**

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

```
P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1) 
```

To calculate the denominator, we use the law of total probability:

```
P(W1) = P(W1|A) * P(A) + P(W1|B) * P(B)
P(W1) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29
```

**Now we can calculate P(A|W1):**

```
P(A|W1) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29
```
