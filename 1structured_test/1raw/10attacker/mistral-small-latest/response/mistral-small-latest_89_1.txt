The probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller is the probability that the attacker is indeed taller and the first witness correctly identified this, plus the probability that the attacker is shorter but the first witness mistakenly identified him as taller.

Let's denote:
- T as the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
- S as the event that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm
- C1 as the event that the first witness is correct
- E1 as the event that the first witness makes an error

Given information:
- P(T) = 0.15 (since 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- P(S) = 0.85 (since 85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)
- P(C1|T) = 0.8 (the first witness correctly identifies taller people in 80% of cases)
- P(E1|S) = 0.2 (the first witness mistakenly identifies shorter people as taller in 20% of cases)

We want to find P(T|C1), which is the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the first witness claimed so.

Using Bayes' theorem: P(T|C1) = P(C1|T) * P(T) / P(C1)

P(C1) = P(C1|T) * P(T) + P(C1|S) * P(S)
       = P(C1|T) * P(T) + P(E1|S) * P(S) (since P(C1|S) = 1 - P(E1|S))
       = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85
       ≈ 0.22

So, P(T|C1) = 0.8 * 0.15 / 0.22 ≈ 0.72}
