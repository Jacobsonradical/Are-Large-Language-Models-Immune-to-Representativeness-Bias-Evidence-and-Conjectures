Let's define the following events:
- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
- B1: The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller
- B2: The second witness claims the attacker is shorter than 185 cm

We want to calculate P(A|B1, B2), which is the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, given the testimonies of the two witnesses.

Using Bayes' theorem, we have:

P(A|B1, B2) = [P(B1|A) * P(B2|A) * P(A)] / [P(B1|A) * P(B2|A) * P(A) + P(B1|¬A) * P(B2|¬A) * P(¬A)]

Given information:
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- P(¬A) = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)
- P(B1|A) = 0.8 (The first witness correctly identifies 185 cm or taller 80% of the time)
- P(B1|¬A) = 0.2 (The first witness incorrectly identifies shorter than 185 cm as 185 cm or taller 20% of the time)
- P(B2|A) = 0.3 (The second witness incorrectly identifies 185 cm or taller as shorter 30% of the time)
- P(B2|¬A) = 0.7 (The second witness correctly identifies shorter than 185 cm 70% of the time)

Substituting the values, we get:

P(A|B1, B2) = (0.8 * 0.3 * 0.15) / [(0.8 * 0.3 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.7 * 0.85)]
