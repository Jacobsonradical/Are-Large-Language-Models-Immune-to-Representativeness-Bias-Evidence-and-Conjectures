Here's how to break down the problem and calculate the probability:

**1. Define Events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**2. Given Information:**

* **P(A) = 0.15:**  15% of the population is 185 cm or taller.
* **P(¬A) = 0.85:** 85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm.
* **P(B|A) = 0.80:** The first witness correctly identifies a tall person 80% of the time.
* **P(B|¬A) = 0.20:** The first witness incorrectly identifies a short person as tall 20% of the time.

**3.  Apply Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem allows us to calculate the probability of an event (A) given evidence (B):

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

To find P(B), we need the law of total probability:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)

**4. Calculate P(B):**

P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

**5. Calculate P(A|B):**

P(A|B) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 ≈ 0.414

**Answer:**

{probability: 0.414}
