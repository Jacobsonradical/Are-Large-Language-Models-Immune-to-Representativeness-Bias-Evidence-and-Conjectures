**Logic:**

We need to consider the probability of the attacker being tall *given* that Witness 1 said they were tall. This involves Bayes' Theorem.

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B) = Probability of event A happening given that event B has happened.
* P(B|A) = Probability of event B happening given that event A has happened.
* P(A) = Prior probability of event A.
* P(B) = Prior probability of event B.

**Applying to our problem:**

* **A:** Attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

Let's break down the components:

* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being tall = 0.15 (from population data).
* **P(B|A):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming tall given the attacker is tall = 0.80 (witness accuracy).
* **P(B|not A):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming tall given the attacker is *not* tall = 0.20 (witness error).
* **P(not A):** Prior probability of the attacker *not* being tall = 0.85.
* **P(B):**  This is the tricky part. It's the overall probability of Witness 1 claiming the attacker is tall. We need to consider both scenarios where the attacker is tall and where they are not:
    * P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|not A) * P(not A)]
    * P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29
