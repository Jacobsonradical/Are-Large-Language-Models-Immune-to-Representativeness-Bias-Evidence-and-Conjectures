* **Witness Reliability:**  The information in "a" gives us the reliability of the two witnesses:
    * Witness 1: 80% accuracy (0.8) in correctly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller. 
    * Witness 2: 70% accuracy (0.7) in correctly identifying someone as shorter than 185 cm.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (the attacker's height) based on new evidence (the witness testimonies).  Let's define the events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that Witness 1 said so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall *given* the attacker is actually tall (80% or 0.8). 
* **P(A):**  The prior probability the attacker is tall (15% or 0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall.  We need to calculate this using the Law of Total Probability:

    P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

    * **P(B|not A):** The probability Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall *given* the attacker is actually shorter (20% or 0.2, since Witness 1 is 80% accurate).
    * **P(not A):** The probability the attacker is shorter than 185 cm (85% or 0.85).
