Here's how to approach this problem:

**Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **Prior Probability:** 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller.
* **Witness Testimony:** We need to incorporate the accuracy of the witnesses into our estimate.

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (attacker's height) based on new evidence (witness testimony).  Here's how it applies:

* **Let A be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.**
* **Let W1 be the event that the first witness claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller.**

We want to find P(A|W1), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the first witness's testimony.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(W1|A):** Probability the first witness claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller given the attacker actually was. This is the witness's accuracy, 80%.
* **P(A):** Prior probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller, which is 15%.
* **P(W1):** Probability the first witness claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller (regardless of the actual height). This requires considering both cases:

    * **Case 1: Attacker is 185 cm or taller:**  P(W1|A) * P(A) = 0.80 * 0.15 = 0.12
    * **Case 2: Attacker is shorter than 185 cm:** P(W1|Not A) * P(Not A) = 0.20 * 0.85 = 0.17 

    Therefore, P(W1) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Calculating the Probability**

Now, plug the values back into Bayes' Theorem:
