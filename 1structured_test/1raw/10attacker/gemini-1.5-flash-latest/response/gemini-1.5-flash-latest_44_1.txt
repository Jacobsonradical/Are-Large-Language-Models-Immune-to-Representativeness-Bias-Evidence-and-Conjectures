* **Population:** 85% are shorter than 185 cm, 15% are 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This problem is best solved using Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update our beliefs about an event based on new evidence.

Let's define our events:

* **A:** The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is tall given Witness 1's claim.

**Bayes' Theorem Formula:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each part:

* **P(B|A):** The probability that Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall *given* the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, 80% (0.8).
* **P(A):** The prior probability that the attacker is tall, which is the proportion of the population that's tall, 15% (0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability that Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall. This is calculated using the law of total probability:
    *  P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    *  P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
