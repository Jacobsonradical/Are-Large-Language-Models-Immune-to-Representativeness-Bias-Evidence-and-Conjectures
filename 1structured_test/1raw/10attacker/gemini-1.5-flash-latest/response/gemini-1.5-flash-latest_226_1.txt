Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief based on new evidence (witness testimony):

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B) is the probability of the attacker being tall (A) given the first witness's claim (B). This is what we want to find.
* P(B|A) is the probability of the first witness claiming the attacker was tall given that the attacker was actually tall (80% accuracy).
* P(A) is the prior probability of the attacker being tall (15%).
* P(B) is the overall probability of the first witness claiming the attacker was tall.  This is a bit trickier and we'll calculate it below.
