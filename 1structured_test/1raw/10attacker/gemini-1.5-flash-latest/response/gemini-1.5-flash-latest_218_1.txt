Here's how to break down the problem and arrive at an estimate:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Population Distribution:** 85% of the city's population is shorter than 185 cm, and 15% are 185 cm or taller. This gives us a prior probability of 0.15 that a random person from the city would be 185 cm or taller.
* **Witness Accuracy:** 
    * Witness 1 (claims taller): Correct 80% of the time, incorrect 20%
    * Witness 2 (claims shorter): Correct 70% of the time, incorrect 30%

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (the attacker's height) given new evidence (the witness testimonies).

Let's define:

* **A:** The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **W1:** The event that Witness 1 claims the attacker was taller.

We want to find P(A|W1), the probability that the attacker is taller given Witness 1's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1)

Where:

* **P(W1|A):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming taller given the attacker is actually taller (0.8, their accuracy).
* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being taller (0.15, from the population distribution).
* **P(W1):** Probability of Witness 1 claiming taller (we need to calculate this).

**Calculating P(W1)**

To get P(W1), we need to consider both scenarios where the attacker is taller and shorter:

* **Scenario 1: Attacker is taller:** P(W1|A) * P(A) = 0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12
* **Scenario 2: Attacker is shorter:** P(W1|~A) * P(~A) = 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17 (Witness 1 is wrong, attacker is shorter)

P(W1) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Putting it Together**

Now we can calculate P(A|W1):

P(A|W1) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.4138
