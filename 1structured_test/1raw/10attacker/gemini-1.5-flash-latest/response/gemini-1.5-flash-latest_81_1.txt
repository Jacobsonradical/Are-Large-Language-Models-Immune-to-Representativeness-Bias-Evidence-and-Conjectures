Here's how we can solve this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Let's define our events:**

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that Witness 1 claimed so.**

**We know:**

* **P(A) = 0.15** (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
* **P(B|A) = 0.80** (Witness 1 correctly identifies height 80% of the time when it's true)
* **P(B|¬A) = 0.20** (Witness 1 incorrectly identifies height 20% of the time when it's false)

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)]

**Calculate P(¬A):**

* P(¬A) = 1 - P(A) = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85

**Plug in the values:**

P(A|B) = [0.80 * 0.15] / [0.80 * 0.15 + 0.20 * 0.85]

P(A|B) = 0.12 / 0.29

**P(A|B) ≈ 0.41**

**Answer:** {probability: 0.41}
