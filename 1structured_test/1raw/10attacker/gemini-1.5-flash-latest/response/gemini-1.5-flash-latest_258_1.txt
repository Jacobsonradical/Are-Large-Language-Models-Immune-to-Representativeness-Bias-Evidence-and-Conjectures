**1.  Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **General population:** 15% of the city's population is 185 cm or taller.
* **Witness accuracy:**  The first witness is 80% accurate in identifying height under similar conditions.

**2.  Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (the attacker being tall) based on new evidence (the witness's statement).

Let's define:

* **A:** The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The event that the first witness claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find  P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is tall given the witness's statement. 

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):**  The probability the witness claims the attacker is tall **given** the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% (0.8).
* **P(A):**  The prior probability that the attacker is tall, which is 15% (0.15) based on the city's population.
* **P(B):** The probability the witness claims the attacker is tall (regardless of the attacker's actual height). We need to calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A) 
    * P(B|not A) = 0.2 (the witness's error rate when the attacker is short)
    * P(not A) = 0.85 (the probability of the attacker being short)
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29 

**3. Calculation**

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
