* **B:**  Event that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that Witness 1 claims so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A):** Probability that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the attacker *is* actually 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 80% or 0.8.
