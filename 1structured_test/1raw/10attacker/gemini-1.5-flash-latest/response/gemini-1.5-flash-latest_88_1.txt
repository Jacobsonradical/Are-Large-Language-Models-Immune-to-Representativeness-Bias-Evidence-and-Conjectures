**2.  Applying Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (in this case, the attacker's height) based on new evidence (the witness testimonies).

Let's define:

* **A:** Event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B1:** Event that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find **P(A|B1)**, the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that Witness 1 claims so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B1) = [P(B1|A) * P(A)] / P(B1)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B1|A):** Probability that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller. This is the accuracy of Witness 1, which is 80% or 0.8.
* **P(A):** Prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, which is 15% or 0.15 (based on the city's population).
* **P(B1):** Probability of Witness 1 making the claim (regardless of the actual height). This is a bit more complex and requires considering both scenarios:

    * **Scenario 1:** The attacker is actually 185 cm or taller (probability 0.15). Witness 1 is correct with probability 0.8. So, the probability of this scenario is 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12.
    * **Scenario 2:** The attacker is actually shorter than 185 cm (probability 0.85). Witness 1 is incorrect with probability 0.2. So, the probability of this scenario is 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17.

    Therefore, P(B1) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
