**Reasoning**

We need to consider the probability of the attacker being tall given the conflicting testimonies of the witnesses.  Since we don't know the attacker's height, we'll use Bayes' Theorem to update our belief based on the witness statements.

**Bayes' Theorem**

Let's define:

*  A: The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
*  B: The event that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is tall given Witness 1's statement. Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

**Breaking Down the Components**

* **P(B|A):** The probability Witness 1 claims the attacker is tall *given* the attacker is actually tall. This is Witness 1's accuracy rate: 0.8.
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being tall, which is the percentage of the population that is 185 cm or taller: 0.15.
* **P(B):** The probability of Witness 1 claiming the attacker is tall, regardless of the attacker's actual height. This is a bit trickier and needs to be calculated.

**Calculating P(B)**

We need to consider two scenarios to get P(B):

1. **Attacker is tall:**  P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12
2. **Attacker is short:**  P(B|A') * P(A') = 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17  (A' represents the attacker being shorter than 185 cm)

Therefore, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
