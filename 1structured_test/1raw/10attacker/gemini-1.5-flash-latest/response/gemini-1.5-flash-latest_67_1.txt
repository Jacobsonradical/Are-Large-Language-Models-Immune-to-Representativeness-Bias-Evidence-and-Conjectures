Here's how we can calculate the probability:

**Understanding the Problem:**

We have two pieces of conflicting evidence:

* **Witness 1:** Claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller, with an 80% accuracy rate.
* **Witness 2:** Claims the attacker was shorter than 185 cm, with a 70% accuracy rate.
* **Prior Probability:** 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller.

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event based on new evidence. In this case, we want to find the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller (A) given the first witness's claim (W1):

P(A|W1) = [P(W1|A) * P(A)] / P(W1)

Let's break down each part:

* **P(W1|A):** Probability of witness 1 claiming taller given the attacker is actually taller. This is the witness's accuracy rate, 0.8.
* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being taller, 0.15.
* **P(W1):** Probability of witness 1 making their claim. This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(W1) = P(W1|A) * P(A) + P(W1|~A) * P(~A) 
       * P(W1|~A): Probability of witness 1 claiming taller given the attacker is actually shorter (0.2, the error rate).
       * P(~A): Probability of the attacker being shorter (0.85).
       * P(W1) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Calculation:**

P(A|W1) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
