- \( H \) be the hypothesis that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.

- \( P(W_2 | H) = 0.3 \) (second witness mistakenly identifies tall people as short 30% of the time)

We are asked to find \( P(H | W_1 \cap W_2) \), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given both witnesses' testimonies. Using Bayes' theorem and the law of total probability:
\[ P(H | W_1 \cap W_2) = \frac{P(W_1 \cap W_2 | H) P(H)}{P(W_1 \cap W_2)} \]

Where:
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 | H) = P(W_1 | H) P(W_2 | H) = 0.8 \times 0.3 = 0.24 \]
\[ P(W_1 \cap W_2 | \neg H) = P(W_1 | \neg H) P(W_2 | \neg H) = 0.2 \times 0.7 = 0.14 \]

Finally:
\[ P(H | W_1 \cap W_2) = \frac{0.24 \times 0.15}{0.155} = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.232 \]
