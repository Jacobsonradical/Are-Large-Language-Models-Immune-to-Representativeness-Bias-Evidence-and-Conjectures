To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' Theorem, which is expressed as:
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \times P(A)}{P(B)} \]
where:
- \( A \) is the event the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( B \) is the evidence consisting of the testimony of the witnesses.

Based on the information given:
- \( P(A) \), the probability that a random person from the population is 185 cm or taller, is 15% or 0.15.
- \( P(A^c) \), the probability that a random person from the population is shorter than 185 cm, is 85% or 0.85.

Witness accuracies:
- The first witness correctly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller 80% of the time when they actually are \( P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller} | A) = 0.80 \), and incorrectly flags a shorter person as taller 20% of the time \( P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller} | A^c) = 0.20 \).
- The second witness correctly identifies someone as being shorter than 185 cm 70% of the time when they actually are \( P(\text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter} | A^c) = 0.70 \), and incorrectly flags a taller person as shorter 30% of the time \( P(\text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter} | A) = 0.30 \).

We are evaluating \( P(A | \text{W}_1 = \text{Taller}, \text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter}) \).

Using the law of total probability and independence of witness observations of height conditioned on the actual height:
\[ P(B) = P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller}, \text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter}) = P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller} | A)P(\text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter} | A)P(A) + P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller} | A^c)P(\text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter} | A^c)P(A^c) \]
\[ = (0.80 \times 0.30 \times 0.15) + (0.20 \times 0.70 \times 0.85) \]
\[ = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155 \]

Thus:
\[ P(A | \text{W}_1 = \text{Taller}, \text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter}) = \frac{P(\text{W}_1 = \text{Taller} | A)P(\text{W}_2 = \text{Shorter} | A)P(A)}{P(B)} \]
\[ = \frac{0.80 \times 0.30 \times 0.15}{0.155} \]
\[ = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.2322 \]

This gives us the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the conflicting witnesses as approximately 0.2322.
