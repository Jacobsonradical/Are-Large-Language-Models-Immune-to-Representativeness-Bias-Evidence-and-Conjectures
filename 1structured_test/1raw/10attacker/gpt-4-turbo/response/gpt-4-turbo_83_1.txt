- Witness 1 claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller. The reliability of this witness (correctly identifying heights 185 cm or taller) is 80%, and the error rate (wrongly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller when they are not) is 20%. Thus:
  - \(P(\text{Witness 1 claims taller} | A) = 0.80\)
  - \(P(\text{Witness 1 claims taller} | B) = 0.20\)

- Witness 2 claims the attacker was shorter than 185 cm. The reliability of this witness (correctly identifying heights shorter than 185 cm) is 70%, but error rate (wrongly identifying someone as shorter when they are 185 cm or taller) is 30%. Thus:
  - \(P(\text{Witness 2 claims shorter} | A) = 0.30\)
  - \(P(\text{Witness 2 claims shorter} | B) = 0.70\)

- \(P(\text{Witness 1 claims taller}) = P(\text{Witness 1 claims taller} | A)P(A) + P(\text{Witness 1 claims taller} | B)P(B) = 0.8 \times 0.15 + 0.2 \times 0.85 = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29\)
