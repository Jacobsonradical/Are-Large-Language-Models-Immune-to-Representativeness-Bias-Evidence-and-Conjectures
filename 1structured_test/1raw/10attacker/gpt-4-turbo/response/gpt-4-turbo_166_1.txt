P(W2 | H) = Probability of W2 incorrectly saying someone is short (H) = 0.30
P(W2 | L) = Probability of W2 correctly identifying short people (L) = 0.70

We can calculate the joint probabilities:
P(W1 ∩ W2 | H) = P(W1 | H) * P(W2 | H) = 0.80 * 0.30 = 0.24
P(W1 ∩ W2 | L) = P(W1 | L) * P(W2 | L) = 0.20 * 0.70 = 0.14

Using the Law of Total Probability, we find:
P(W1 ∩ W2) = P(W1 ∩ W2 | H) * P(H) + P(W1 ∩ W2 | L) * P(L)
= 0.24 * 0.15 + 0.14 * 0.85
= 0.036 + 0.119
= 0.155

Now, we apply Bayes' Theorem to find P(H | W1 ∩ W2):
P(H | W1 ∩ W2) = (P(W1 ∩ W2 | H) * P(H)) / P(W1 ∩ W2)
= (0.24 * 0.15) / 0.155
= 0.036 / 0.155
≈ 0.2323

Thus, given the claims and accuracies of both witnesses, the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller is approximately 0.2323.
