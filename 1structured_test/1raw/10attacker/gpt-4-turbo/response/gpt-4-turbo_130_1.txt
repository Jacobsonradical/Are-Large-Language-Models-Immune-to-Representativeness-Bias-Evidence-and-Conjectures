- \( H \) be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( W_1 \) be the event that Witness 1 claims the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

- \( P(\neg H) = 0.85 \) (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

We need to combine the available information using Bayes' Theorem. We are combining evidence W1 (saying "taller") and W2 (saying "shorter"), thus finding \( P(H | W_1, W_2) \).

Bayes' Theorem in this context states:
\[ P(H | W_1, W_2) = \frac{P(W_1, W_2 | H) \times P(H)}{P(W_1, W_2)} \]

\[ P(W_1, W_2 | H) = P(W_1 | H) \times P(W_2 | H) = 0.80 \times 0.30 = 0.24 \]
\[ P(W_1, W_2 | \neg H) = P(W_1 | \neg H) \times P(W_2 | \neg H) = 0.20 \times 0.70 = 0.14 \]

\[ P(W_1, W_2) = P(W_1, W_2 | H) \times P(H) + P(W_1, W_2 | \neg H) \times P(\neg H) = 0.24 \times 0.15 + 0.14 \times 0.85 = 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155 \]

Then, plug these into Bayes' formula:
\[ P(H | W_1, W_2) = \frac{0.036}{0.155} \approx 0.2323 \]
