To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given both the witnesses' statements, we can use Bayes' theorem. This theorem helps us update our belief about a hypothesis based on new evidence. Let's denote:

- Event A: The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- Event B: The condition or evidence derived from the witnesses' statements.

From the problem:
- \(P(A) = 0.15\) (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller).

The complement of A, denoted as \(A^c\) (attacker is shorter than 185 cm):
- \(P(A^c) = 0.85\) (85% are shorter than 185 cm).

Each witness provided an assessment of the attacker's height:
- Witness 1 is accurate 80% of the time. Thus:
  - \(P(\text{W1 says 'tall' }\mid A) = 0.8\),
  - \(P(\text{W1 says 'short' }\mid A^c) = 0.8\).
- Witness 2 is accurate 70% of the time. Thus:
  - \(P(\text{W2 says 'tall' }\mid A^c) = 0.3\),
  - \(P(\text{W2 says 'short' }\mid A) = 0.3\).

From these:
- \(P(\text{W1 says 'tall'}) = P(\text{W1 says 'tall'} \mid A)P(A) + P(\text{W1 says 'tall'} \mid A^c)P(A^c)\)
  - \(= (0.8)(0.15) + (0.2)(0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29\).
- \(P(\text{W2 says 'short'}) = P(\text{W2 says 'short'} \mid A)P(A) + P(\text{W2 says 'short'} \mid A^c)P(A^c)\)
  - \(= (0.3)(0.15) + (0.7)(0.85) = 0.045 + 0.595 = 0.64\).

Now, we need to find \(P(A \mid \text{W1 says 'tall'}, \text{W2 says 'short'})\) using Bayes' theorem:
\[ \begin{align*}
P(A \mid \text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'}) = \frac{P(\text{W1 'tall'} \mid A) \times P(\text{W2 'short'} \mid A) \times P(A)}{P(\text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'})}
\end{align*} \]

Where \(P(\text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'})\) can be calculated by:
\[ \begin{align*}
P(\text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'}) = & \ P(\text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'} \mid A)P(A) + P(\text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'} \mid A^c)P(A^c) \\
= & \ P(\text{W1 'tall'} \mid A)P(\text{W2 'short'} \mid A)P(A) + P(\text{W1 'tall'} \mid A^c)P(\text{W2 'short'} \mid A^c)P(A^c) \\
= & \ (0.8)(0.3)(0.15) + (0.2)(0.7)(0.85) \\
= & \ (0.24)(0.15) + (0.14)(0.85) \\
= & \ 0.036 + 0.119 = 0.155.
\end{align*} \]

Now, calculate using Bayes:
\[ \begin{align*}
P(A \mid \text{W1 'tall', W2 'short'}) = \frac{(0.8)(0.3)(0.15)}{0.155} = 0.231.
\end{align*} \]

Thus:

  "probability": 0.231
