To estimate the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:
- P(A|B) is the probability of event A (attacker is 185 cm or taller) given event B (first witness's statement).
- P(B|A) is the probability of the first witness's statement given the attacker is 185 cm or taller, which is 0.8 (80%).
- P(A) is the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller, which is 0.15 (15%).
- P(B) is the probability of the first witness's statement, which we can calculate as:
  P(B) = P(B and A) + P(B and not A)
  = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|not A) * P(not A)]

We know P(B|A), P(A), and P(not A) (1 - P(A)). To calculate P(B|not A), consider that the first witness will incorrectly claim that a person is 185 cm or taller 20% of the time when they are not. Given that 85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm, the probability of the first witness's statement when the attacker is not 185 cm or taller is 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17.

Now we can calculate P(B):
P(B) = [0.8 * 0.15] + [0.17 * 0.85] ≈ 0.226

Finally, we can compute our desired probability:
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B) ≈ [0.8 * 0.15] / 0.226 ≈ 0.527
