**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

To combine the information and get an accurate estimate, we'll use Bayes' Theorem. It helps us update our beliefs (probabilities) based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(A|B):** The probability of event A happening given that event B has happened (what we want to find).  In our case, this is the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony.
* **P(B|A):** The probability of event B happening given that event A has happened. Here, it's the probability the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller given they actually are that height (80% from information 'a').
* **P(A):** The prior probability of event A happening. This is the probability that someone in the city is 185 cm or taller (15% from information 'b').
* **P(B):** The probability of event B happening. This is the probability the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, regardless of their actual height. We need to calculate this.

**Calculating P(B)**

To get P(B), we need to consider both scenarios:

* **Scenario 1:** The person is actually 185 cm or taller (probability 0.15). The witness identifies them correctly with a probability of 0.8, so the contribution to P(B) is 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12

Therefore, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Putting it Together**

Now we have all the components to apply Bayes' Theorem:
