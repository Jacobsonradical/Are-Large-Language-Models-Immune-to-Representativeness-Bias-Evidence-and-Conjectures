**Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **Witness Testimony:** The witness is accurate 80% of the time, but there's a 20% chance they're wrong.
* **Population Distribution:**  Only 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (in this case, the attacker being tall) given new evidence (the witness's testimony). 

Let:

* **A:** Event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
* **B:** Event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is tall given the witness's testimony.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down the components:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall *given* they actually are tall. This is 80% (from the witness accuracy).
* **P(A):** The prior probability that the attacker is tall, which is 15% (from the population distribution).
* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall (regardless of their actual height). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:

    *  P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
    *  P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.28

Now we can plug these values into Bayes' Theorem:
