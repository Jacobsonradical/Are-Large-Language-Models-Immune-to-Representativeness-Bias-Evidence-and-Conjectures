Here's how to break down the problem and arrive at the answer:

**Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **Prior probability:**  85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm, meaning 15% are 185 cm or taller.
* **Witness Testimony:** A witness claims the attacker was 185 cm or taller, but their reliability is only 80%.

We need to combine these pieces of information to estimate the probability the attacker was actually 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is a powerful tool for updating beliefs based on new evidence. It's formulated as follows:

```
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
```

Where:

* **P(A|B):** The probability of event A happening given that event B has happened (what we want to find).
* **P(B|A):** The probability of event B happening given that event A has happened.
* **P(A):** The prior probability of event A happening.
* **P(B):** The prior probability of event B happening.

**Let's Define Our Events:**

* **A:**  The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:**  The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem to Our Problem:**

* **P(A):** 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller).
* **P(B|A):** 0.80 (The witness correctly identifies height 80% of the time when the person is actually 185 cm or taller).
* **P(B|not A):** 0.20 (The witness incorrectly identifies height 20% of the time when the person is actually shorter than 185 cm).

To calculate **P(B)**, we need to consider both scenarios:

* **Scenario 1:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller (A) and the witness is correct (B|A): 0.15 * 0.80 = 0.12
* **Scenario 2:** The attacker is shorter than 185 cm (not A) and the witness is wrong (B|not A): 0.85 * 0.20 = 0.17

**P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29**

**Now we can calculate P(A|B):**

```
P(A|B) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 
       = 0.12 / 0.29

```
