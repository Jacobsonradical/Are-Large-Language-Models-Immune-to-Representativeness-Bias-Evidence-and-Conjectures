**Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **Population Distribution:** 15% of the population is 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is a useful tool for updating our beliefs based on new evidence. In this case:

* **Event A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker was tall given the witness's testimony.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability of the witness identifying someone as tall given they *are* tall (80% accuracy). 
* **P(A):** The prior probability of someone being tall in the population (15%).
* **P(B):** The overall probability of the witness identifying someone as tall. This requires considering both true positives and false positives:
    * True Positives: P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.80 * 0.15 = 0.12
    * False Positives: P(B|~A) * P(~A) = 0.20 * 0.85 = 0.17 
    * P(B) = True Positives + False Positives = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
