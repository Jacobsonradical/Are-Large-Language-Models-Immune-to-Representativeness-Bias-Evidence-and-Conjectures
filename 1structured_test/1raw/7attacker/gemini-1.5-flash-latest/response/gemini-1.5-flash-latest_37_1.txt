Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (the attacker's height) based on new evidence (the witness's statement).

* **A:** The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller given they *actually* are 185 cm or taller. This is the witness's accuracy, which is 0.8 (80%).
