**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is useful for updating our belief about an event (attacker's height) given new evidence (witness testimony). Here's the formula:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker is 185 cm or taller) given event B (witness identified them as such). This is what we want to find.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness correctly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller) given event A (the person is actually 185 cm or taller). This is the witness's accuracy, 80%.
* P(A): Prior probability of event A (attacker being 185 cm or taller). This is the population distribution, 15%.
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller). This requires a bit more calculation.

**Calculating P(B)**

To calculate P(B), we need to consider both scenarios:

* **Scenario 2:** The witness incorrectly identifies someone shorter than 185 cm (20% error rate * 85% population): 0.20 * 0.85 = 0.17
