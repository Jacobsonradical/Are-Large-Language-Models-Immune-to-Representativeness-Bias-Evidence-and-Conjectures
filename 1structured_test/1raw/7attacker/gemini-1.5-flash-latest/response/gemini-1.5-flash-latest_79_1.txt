**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

To combine this information, we can use Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B) = Probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller given the witness's statement (what we want to find).
* P(B|A) = Probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller given they actually are (80% from information b).

* P(B) = Probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller (this needs to be calculated).

* **Case 2:** The attacker is shorter than 185 cm (probability = 85%). The witness will incorrectly identify them as tall with 20% probability.

P(B) = (0.15 * 0.8) + (0.85 * 0.2) = 0.29

Now we can plug everything into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.414
