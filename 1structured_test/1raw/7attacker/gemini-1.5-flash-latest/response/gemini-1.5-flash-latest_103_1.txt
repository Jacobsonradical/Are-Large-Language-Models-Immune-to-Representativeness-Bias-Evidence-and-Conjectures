**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about an event (in this case, the attacker's height) based on new evidence (the witness's statement).

Let's define the events:

* **A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's statement.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller *given* the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller. This is 80% (from information b).
* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller. This is 15% (from information a).
* **P(B):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller. We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
