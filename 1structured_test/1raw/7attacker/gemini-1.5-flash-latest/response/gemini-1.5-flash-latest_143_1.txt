**Understanding the Problem**

We have two pieces of information:

* **Witness Testimony:** The witness is 80% accurate in judging height under similar conditions, meaning they have a 20% chance of being wrong.
* **Population Statistics:** 15% of the city's population is 185 cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

This problem can be solved using Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update our beliefs based on new evidence.  Here's how we'll apply it:

* **Event A:** The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller, given that they actually are that tall. This is the witness's accuracy, 80% (0.8).
* **P(A):** The prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller. This is the population statistic, 15% (0.15).
* **P(B):** The probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller. This is a bit trickier. We need to consider both scenarios: 
    * The witness is correct (attacker is actually tall)
    * The witness is wrong (attacker is actually short)

**Calculating P(B)**

P(B) can be calculated as follows:

P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|not A) * P(not A)]

* **P(B|not A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as tall, given they are actually short. This is the witness's error rate, 20% (0.2).
* **P(not A):** The probability the attacker is *not* 185 cm or taller, which is 85% (0.85).

Therefore:

P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.26

**Final Calculation**

Now we can plug everything into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.26 = 0.4615
