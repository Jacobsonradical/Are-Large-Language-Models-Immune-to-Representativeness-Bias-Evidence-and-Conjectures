H: the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
W: the event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller

From Bayes' theorem, we have:

P(H|W) = P(W|H) \* P(H) / P(W)

We are given the following information:

1. P(W|H) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a tall person 80% of the time)
2. P(H) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)

P(W) = P(W and H) + P(W and not H) = P(W|H) \* P(H) + P(W|not H) \* P(not H)

We are given P(W|H) = 0.8 and P(H) = 0.15. We also know that P(not H) = 1 - P(H) = 0.85. Since the witness errs in 20% of the cases when identifying shorter people, P(W|not H) = 0.2.

Now we can calculate P(W):

P(W) = (0.8 \* 0.15) + (0.2 \* 0.85) ≈ 0.28

Finally, we can calculate P(H|W) using Bayes' theorem:

P(H|W) = (0.8 \* 0.15) / 0.28 ≈ 0.4286
