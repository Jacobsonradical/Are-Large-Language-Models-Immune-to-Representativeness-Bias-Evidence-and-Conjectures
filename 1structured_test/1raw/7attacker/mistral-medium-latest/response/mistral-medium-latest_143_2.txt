To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem, which is a principle in the field of statistics that describes the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions that might be related to the event. * H: the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller
* W: the event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(H|W), the probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem, we have:

P(H|W) = P(W|H) \* P(H) / [P(W|H) \* P(H) + P(W|¬H) \* P(¬H)]

* P(H): the prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, which is 0.15 according to information a.
* P(¬H): the prior probability that the attacker is not 185 cm or taller, which is 1 - P(H) = 0.85.
* P(W|H): the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that they are 185 cm or taller, which is 0.8 according to information b.
* P(W|¬H): the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller given that they are not 185 cm or taller, which is 0.2 according to information b.

Plugging in these values, we get:

P(H|W) = (0.8 \* 0.15) / [(0.8 \* 0.15) + (0.2 \* 0.85)] ≈ 0.441 or 44.1%
