To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, given that the witness identified the attacker as such, we can use Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{P(W \mid H) \cdot P(H)}{P(W)} \]

Where:
\( P(H) \) is the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller (prior probability)
\( P(W) \) is the probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller
\( P(W \mid H) \) is the probability that the witness correctly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller
\( P(H \mid W) \) is the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such (posterior probability)

We already know:
\( P(H) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
\( P(W \mid H) = 0.8 \) (witness correctly identifies tall people 80% of the time)

We also need to calculate:
\( P(W \mid H^c) \), which is the probability that the witness incorrectly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller when they are not. We know that when confronted with someone shorter than 185 cm, the witness still incorrectly labels them as being 185 cm or taller 20% of the time. Thus, \( P(W \mid H^c) = 0.2 \).

From these, \( P(H^c) \) (probability that the attacker is shorter than 185 cm) is \( 0.85 \).

The law of total probability gives us \( P(W) \):
\[ P(W) = P(W \mid H) \cdot P(H) + P(W \mid H^c) \cdot P(H^c) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.8 \cdot 0.15 + 0.2 \cdot 0.85 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Then, applying Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{P(W \mid H) \cdot P(H)}{P(W)} \]
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H \mid W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]

Therefore, the estimated probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such is about 0.41.
