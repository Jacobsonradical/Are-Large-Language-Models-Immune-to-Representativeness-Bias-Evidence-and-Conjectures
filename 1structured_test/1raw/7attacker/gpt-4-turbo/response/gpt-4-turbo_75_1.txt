To solve this problem, we need to apply Bayesian probability analysis, which consists of using Bayes' theorem to update the probability estimate for a hypothesis as more evidence or information becomes available.

Using Bayes' Theorem:
\[ P(H | E) = \frac{P(E | H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]

\[ P(E) = P(E | H) \cdot P(H) + P(E | \neg H) \cdot P(\neg H) \]
\[ P(E) = 0.8 \cdot 0.15 + 0.2 \cdot 0.85 \]
\[ P(E) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

\[ P(H | E) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]
