To solve this problem, we can apply Bayes' theorem. 

Let's define:
1. \( A \) as the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
2. \( B \) as the event that the witness identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller.

According to the problem:
- \( P(A) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- \( P(A^c) = 0.85 \) (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

Given the reliability of the witness:
- \( P(B|A) = 0.80 \) (The probability that the witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, given that they are)
- \( P(B|A^c) = 0.20 \) (The probability that the witness incorrectly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, given that they are not)

We need to find \( P(A|B) \) which is the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such. 

Bayes' theorem states:
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) P(A)}{P(B)} \]

Where \( P(B) \), the probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, can be computed using the law of total probability:
\[ P(B) = P(B|A) P(A) + P(B|A^c) P(A^c) \]

Plugging in the values:
\[ P(B) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) \]

\[ P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Thus,
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.80 * 0.15}{0.29} \]

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]

This result means that there is approximately a 41.38% probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, given the witness's identification.
