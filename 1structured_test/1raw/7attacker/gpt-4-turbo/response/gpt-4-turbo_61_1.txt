To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony and the population distribution, we use Bayes' Theorem:

Bayes' Theorem is stated as:
\[ P(H | E) = \frac{P(E | H) \times P(H)}{P(E)} \]

Where:
- \( P(H | E) \) is the probability that the hypothesis (H: attacker is 185 cm or taller) is true given the evidence (E: witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller).
- \( P(E | H) \) is the probability of the evidence given the hypothesis is true (probability witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, which is 80% or 0.8).
- \( P(H) \) is the prior probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, not based on witness testimony (15% or 0.15 of the population).
- \( P(E) \) is the total probability of the evidence occurring (whether the hypothesis is true or not).

To find \( P(E) \), we use the law of total probability:
\[ P(E) = P(E | H) \times P(H) + P(E | H^c) \times P(H^c) \]
Where:
- \( P(H^c) \) is the probability of the hypothesis being false (attacker is shorter than 185 cm, or 85% or 0.85).
- \( P(E | H^c) \) is the probability of the evidence if the hypothesis is false (probability the witness incorrectly identifies a shorter person as being taller, also 20% or 0.2).

Calculate \( P(E) \):
\[ P(E) = (0.8 \times 0.15) + (0.2 \times 0.85) \]
\[ P(E) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Now, use Bayes' Theorem to calculate \( P(H | E) \):
\[ P(H | E) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(H | E) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]
