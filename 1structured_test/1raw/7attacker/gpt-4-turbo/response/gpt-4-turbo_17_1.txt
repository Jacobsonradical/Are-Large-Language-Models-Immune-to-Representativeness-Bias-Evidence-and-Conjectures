- Let \( B \) be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We need to find \( P(A|B) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's testimony. Using Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) P(A)}{P(B)} \]

\[ P(B) = P(B|A) P(A) + P(B|A^c) P(A^c) \]
\[ P(B) = (0.8)(0.15) + (0.2)(0.85) \]
\[ P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
