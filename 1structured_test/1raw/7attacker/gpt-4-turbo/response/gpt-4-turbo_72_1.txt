To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which helps us update our probability estimate based on new evidence. In this case, we want to find the probability that the attacker was 185 cm or taller given the witness statement.

Let:
- \( H \) be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \( E \) be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We know from the problem statement:
- \( P(H) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- \( P(\neg H) = 0.85 \) (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)
- \( P(E|H) = 0.8 \) (The probability that the witness correctly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller, if they are indeed that height)
- \( P(E|\neg H) = 0.2 \) (The probability that the witness incorrectly identifies someone as being 185 cm or taller, when they are not)

We want to find \( P(H|E) \), which is the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' theorem states:
\[
P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \times P(H)}{P(E)}
\]
where
\[
P(E) = P(E|H) \times P(H) + P(E|\neg H) \times P(\neg H)
\]

Plugging in the numbers:
\[
P(E) = (0.8 \times 0.15) + (0.2 \times 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
\]
\[
P(H|E) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} \approx \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138
\]

Therefore, the estimate for the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, given the witness's testimony, is about 0.414 (41.4%).
