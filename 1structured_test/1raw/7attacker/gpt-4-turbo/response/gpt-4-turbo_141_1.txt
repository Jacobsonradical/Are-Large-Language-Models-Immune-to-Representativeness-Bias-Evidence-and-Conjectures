To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' Theorem, which gives us a way to update our probability estimation for a hypothesis given new evidence. Let's define the following:

- Let A be the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- Let B be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

From the information given:
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- P(A^c) = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)
- P(B|A) = 0.80 (the probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, given that they are indeed 185 cm or taller)
- P(B|A^c) = 0.20 (the probability that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller, given that they are shorter than 185 cm)

We want to calculate P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' Theorem:

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

First, calculate P(B) using the Law of Total Probability:

\[ P(B) = P(B|A) \cdot P(A) + P(B|A^c) \cdot P(A^c) \]
\[ P(B) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Now, we calculate P(A|B) using Bayes' Theorem:

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.80 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]
