- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- B: The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identified the height 80% of the time when the person was 185 cm or taller)
- P(A) = 0.15 (the proportion of the population that is 185 cm or taller)
- P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.31 (the total probability of the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller)

Plugging in the values, we get:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.31 = 0.387
