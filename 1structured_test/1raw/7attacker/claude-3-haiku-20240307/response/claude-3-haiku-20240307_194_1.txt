To estimate the probability that the attacker's height was 185 cm or taller, we can use Bayes' theorem.

Let's define:
- A: the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
- B: the event that the witness correctly identifies the attacker's height as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that the witness correctly identified the height as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

Given information:
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time when the person is 185 cm or taller)
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- P(B) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.32 (the witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time when the person is 185 cm or taller, and incorrectly identifies the height 20% of the time when the person is shorter than 185 cm)

Plugging in the values:
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.32 = 0.375
