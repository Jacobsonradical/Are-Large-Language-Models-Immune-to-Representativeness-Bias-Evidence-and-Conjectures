* **Witness Reliability:** Information "b" tells us the witness isn't perfect. They have an 80% accuracy rate in judging height under similar conditions.
* **Goal:**  We want to combine the prior probability with the witness testimony to get a more accurate estimate.

**Using Bayes' Theorem (A simplified approach)**

1. **Scenarios:** Imagine 100 attacks happened under similar conditions.
   * Based on the city's height distribution (prior probability), roughly 15 out of these 100 attacks would likely involve attackers 185cm or taller.

3. **Calculation:** The estimated probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller given the witness testimony is approximately 12/29 = 0.41.
