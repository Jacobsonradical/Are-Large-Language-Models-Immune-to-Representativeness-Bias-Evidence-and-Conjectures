Here's how we can estimate the probability:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability (a):**  Before considering the witness testimony, there's a 15% chance a randomly chosen person from the city is 185cm or taller.
* **Witness Reliability (b):** The witness isn't perfect. They have an 80% accuracy rate in judging height under similar conditions.

**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating our belief based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller) given that event B (witness said they are 185cm or taller) occurred.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) given that event A (attacker is actually 185cm or taller) is true. This is the witness's accuracy for this height group: 80% or 0.8.
* P(A): Prior probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller): 15% or 0.15.
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) happening. We need to calculate this.

**Calculating P(B)**

P(B) has two components:

1.  Witness says 185cm+ correctly when the person IS 185cm+ (0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12)
2.  Witness says 185cm+ incorrectly when the person IS SHORTER than 185cm (0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17)

So, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Final Calculation**

Now we can plug the values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 
          = 0.4138

**Answer:**

{probability: 0.4138}
