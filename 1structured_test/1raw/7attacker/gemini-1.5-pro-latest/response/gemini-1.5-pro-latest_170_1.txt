Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

*  Event A: The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
*  Event B: The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B):** The probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

**We know:**

* **P(B|A) = 0.8:** The probability the witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller given they actually are.
* **P(B| not A) = 0.2:** The probability the witness incorrectly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller given they are shorter than 185cm.
* **P(A) = 0.15:**  The prior probability of someone in the city being 185 cm or taller.
* **P(not A) = 0.85:** The prior probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185 cm.

**Using Bayes' Theorem:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
