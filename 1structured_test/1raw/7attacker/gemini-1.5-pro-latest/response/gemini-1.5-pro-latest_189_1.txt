**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability (from "b"):** The witness isn't perfect. They have an 80% accuracy rate in judging height under similar conditions. 

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is ideal for updating probabilities based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller) given event B (witness said they were).
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) given event A (attacker actually is). This is the witness's accuracy for this scenario, 80% or 0.8.
* P(A): Prior probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller), which is 15% or 0.15.
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller).  We'll calculate this below.

**Calculating P(B)**

P(B) has two components:

1.  Witness says 185cm+ and is correct: P(witness correct | attacker is 185cm+) * P(attacker is 185cm+) = 0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12
2.  Witness says 185cm+ and is incorrect: P(witness incorrect | attacker is shorter than 185cm) * P(attacker is shorter than 185cm) = 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17

Therefore: P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
