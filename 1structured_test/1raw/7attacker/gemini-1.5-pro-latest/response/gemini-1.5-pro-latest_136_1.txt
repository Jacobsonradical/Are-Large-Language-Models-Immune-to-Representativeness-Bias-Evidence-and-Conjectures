Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Problem**

We want to find the probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller *given* the witness statement. This is a conditional probability problem where:

* **Prior information:** The general population height distribution in the city (85% shorter, 15% taller).
* **New evidence:** The witness testimony, which is not perfect (80% accurate).

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our prior belief based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A happening given that event B happened.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B happening given that event A happened.
* P(A): Prior probability of event A happening.
* P(B): Prior probability of event B happening.

**Applying to Our Problem**

Let's define:

* Event A: Attacker is 185cm or taller.
* Event B: Witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is actually tall given the witness said so.

Here's what we know:

* P(A) = 0.15 (Prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller)
* P(B|A) = 0.8 (Probability the witness is correct, given the attacker is actually tall)
* P(B| not A) = 0.2 (Probability the witness is incorrect, given the attacker is actually shorter)
* P(not A) = 0.85 (Prior probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185cm)

To use Bayes' Theorem, we need P(B), the probability the witness identifies someone as tall (regardless of actual height). We can calculate this using the law of total probability:

P(B) =  [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
