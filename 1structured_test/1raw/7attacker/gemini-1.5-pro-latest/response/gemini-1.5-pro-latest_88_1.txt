**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is perfect for this situation. It lets us update our initial belief (15%) with new evidence (witness testimony).  While we could use the full formula, let's take a more intuitive approach:

2. **Focusing on "Tall" Identifications:** The witness identified the attacker as tall. This means we're only interested in the 12 (correctly identified as tall) + 17 (incorrectly identified as tall) = 29 cases.

3. **Revised Probability:** Out of these 29 cases where the witness said "tall," 12 were actually tall.  So, the probability the attacker was actually 185cm or taller is 12/29.
