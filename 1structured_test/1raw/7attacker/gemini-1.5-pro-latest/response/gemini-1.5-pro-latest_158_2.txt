**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps calculate updated probabilities given new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker is 185cm or taller) given event B (witness said so).
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) given event A (attacker actually being 185cm or taller) = 80% (witness accuracy).
* P(A): Prior probability of event A (attacker being 185cm or taller) = 15%.
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller). This can be calculated using the law of total probability.
