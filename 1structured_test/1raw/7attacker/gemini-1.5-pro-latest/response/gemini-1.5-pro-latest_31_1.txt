**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

To combine these pieces of information and get a more accurate probability, we can use Bayes' Theorem.  Bayes' Theorem gives us a way to update our prior belief (the population distribution) with new evidence (the witness testimony).

Let:

* Event A = Attacker is 185cm or taller
* Event B = Witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* that the witness said they were.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
