Here's how we can approach this problem, combining the witness testimony with the population statistics:

**Understanding the Problem**

We're dealing with conditional probability. We want to find the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the witness's statement. 

* **Let "A" be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.**
* **Let "B" be the event that the witness identifies someone as 185 cm or taller.**

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller given the witness said so.

**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us with this type of problem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as 185cm or taller *given* the person actually is that tall. This is the witness's accuracy for this scenario, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of a person in the city being 185cm or taller. This is given as 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies someone as 185cm or taller (regardless of the attacker's actual height).  This is trickier and requires considering both correct and incorrect identifications:
    * The witness correctly identifies 80% of those who are actually 185cm or taller (0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12).
    * The witness incorrectly identifies 20% of those who are *shorter* than 185cm as being taller (0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17).
    * So, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Calculation**

Now we can plug the values into Bayes' Theorem:
