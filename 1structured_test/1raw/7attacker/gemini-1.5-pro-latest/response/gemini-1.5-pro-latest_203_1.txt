**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability (a):**  This tells us the general likelihood of someone in the city being 185cm or taller (15%). This is our starting point.
* **Witness Reliability (b):** This tells us how much we can trust the witness's statement. They aren't perfect but have a decent track record (80% accuracy).

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is perfect for updating our beliefs based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker being 185cm or taller) given that event B (witness statement) has occurred.
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) given event A (attacker actually being 185cm or taller) is true. This is 0.8 (80% accuracy).
* P(A):  Prior probability of event A (attacker being 185cm or taller) - 0.15.
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness saying 185cm or taller) happening regardless of the attacker's actual height.

To calculate P(B), consider these two scenarios:

* **Scenario 1:** Attacker IS 185cm or taller (15% chance). The witness has an 80% chance of being correct. So, 0.15 * 0.8 = 0.12
* **Scenario 2:** Attacker IS NOT 185cm or taller (85% chance). The witness has a 20% chance of being wrong. So, 0.85 * 0.2 = 0.17
* P(B) is the sum of these scenarios: 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

**Calculation**

Now we can plug everything into Bayes' Theorem:
