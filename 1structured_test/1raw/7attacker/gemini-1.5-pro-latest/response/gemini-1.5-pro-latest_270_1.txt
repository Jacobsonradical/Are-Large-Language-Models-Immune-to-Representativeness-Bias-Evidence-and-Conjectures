*  P(B| not A) = 0.2  (The witness incorrectly identifies short people as tall 20% of the time)

**3. Apply Bayes' Theorem:**

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is actually tall given the witness said they were.  Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
