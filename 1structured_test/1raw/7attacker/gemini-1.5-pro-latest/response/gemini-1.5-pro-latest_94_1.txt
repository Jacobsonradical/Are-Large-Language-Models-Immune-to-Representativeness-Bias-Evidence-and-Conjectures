Here's how to approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**1. Define the Events:**

* **A:** The attacker's height is actually 185 cm or taller.
* **B:** The witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

**2. State the Given Probabilities:**

* **P(A):** Prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller, which is 0.15 (15% of the population).
* **P(B|A):** Probability the witness correctly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller when they actually are (sensitivity) = 0.80 (80%).
* **P(B| not A):** Probability the witness incorrectly identifies someone as 185 cm or taller when they are not (false positive) = 0.20 (20%).
* **P(not A):**  Prior probability of the attacker being shorter than 185 cm = 1 - P(A) = 0.85.

**3. Apply Bayes' Theorem:**

We want to find **P(A|B):** the probability the attacker is actually 185 cm or taller *given* the witness identified them as such.

Bayes' Theorem states: 
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B) 

To find P(B), we use the law of total probability:
P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
