Here's how to approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability (from information "a"):**  Before considering the witness, there's a 15% chance a randomly selected person from the city is 185cm or taller. This is our starting point.
* **Witness Reliability (from information "b"):** The witness isn't perfect. They have an 80% accuracy rate in similar conditions.
* **We want:** The probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller *given* the witness's statement.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem gives us a way to update our belief (the prior probability) based on new evidence (the witness testimony).  

Let:

*  Event A = Attacker is 185cm or taller
*  Event B = Witness identifies the person as 185cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the witness said so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(B|A) = Probability the witness would say "185cm or taller" IF the attacker is actually 185cm or taller (this is the witness's accuracy for this scenario - 80% or 0.8).
* P(A) = Prior probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller (0.15).
* P(B) = Probability of the witness saying "185cm or taller" (this needs further calculation).

**Calculating P(B)**

The witness could say "185cm or taller" in two ways:

1.  The attacker IS 185cm or taller, and they identify them correctly.
2.  The attacker is SHORTER than 185cm, and they identify them incorrectly.

So:
* P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)] 
* P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
