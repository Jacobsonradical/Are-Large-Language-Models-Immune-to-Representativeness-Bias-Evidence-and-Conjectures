**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

To combine these pieces of information and find the probability of the attacker being tall *given* the witness's statement, we can use Bayes' Theorem. 

Let's define the events:

* **Event A:** The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find **P(A|B):** the probability the attacker is tall given the witness said they were.

**Bayes' Theorem states:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(B|A):** Probability the witness would say "tall" IF the attacker was actually tall (this is their 80% accuracy).
* **P(A):** Prior probability of an attacker being tall (15% based on the city's population).
* **P(B):** Probability of the witness saying "tall" (this can happen in two ways: the attacker is tall and they're right, or the attacker is short and they're wrong).

**Calculations**

* **P(B|A) = 0.8** (Witness is 80% accurate when someone is actually tall)
* **P(A) = 0.15** (15% of the population is tall)
* **P(B) =  [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|~A) * P(~A)]** 
    * Where ~A means "attacker is NOT tall"
    * P(B|~A) = 0.2 (Witness is wrong 20% of the time when someone is short)
    * P(~A) = 0.85 (85% of the population is not tall)

* P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
