**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating our belief based on new evidence:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B):  The probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller (A) *given* the witness's statement (B). This is what we want to find.
* P(B|A): The probability of the witness saying "185cm or taller" *if* the attacker was actually 185cm or taller. This is their accuracy rate for identifying tall people (80% or 0.8).
* P(A): The prior probability of the attacker being 185cm or taller (15% or 0.15).
* P(B): The probability of the witness saying "185cm or taller" (regardless of the actual height). We need to calculate this.

**Calculating P(B)**

* **Scenario 1: Attacker is 185cm or taller.**  The witness correctly identifies this with 80% probability.
* **Scenario 2: Attacker is shorter than 185cm.** The witness incorrectly identifies them as tall with 20% probability.

We weight these scenarios by their prior probabilities:

P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Final Calculation**

Now we can plug the values into Bayes' Theorem:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.414
