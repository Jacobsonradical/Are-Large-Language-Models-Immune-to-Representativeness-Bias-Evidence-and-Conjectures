**Understanding the Problem**

We need to update our prior belief about the attacker's height (based on population data) with the new information from the witness testimony. This is a classic example where Bayes' Theorem can be applied.

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B) is the probability of event A happening given that event B has already happened.
* P(B|A) is the probability of event B happening given that event A has already happened.
* P(A) is the prior probability of event A happening.
* P(B) is the prior probability of event B happening.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem to our problem**

Let's define the events:

* Event A: The attacker is 185 cm or taller.
* Event B: The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

* P(A) = 0.15 (prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller)
* P(B|A) = 0.8 (probability the witness correctly identifies someone as tall given they are actually tall)
* P(B| not A) = 0.2 (probability the witness incorrectly identifies someone as tall given they are actually shorter than 185cm)
* P(not A) = 0.85 (prior probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185cm)

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B| not A) * P(not A) 
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29
