Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Problem**

We need to update our prior belief about the attacker's height (based on population data) using the new information from the witness. 

* **Prior Probability (P(A)):** The probability of a randomly chosen person in the city being 185 cm or taller (15%).
* **Likelihood (P(B|A)):** The probability that the witness would identify someone as 185 cm or taller *given* that the person is actually 185 cm or taller (80%).
* **Likelihood (P(B|~A)):** The probability that the witness would identify someone as 185 cm or taller *given* that the person is actually shorter than 185 cm (20%, the error rate).
* **Posterior Probability (P(A|B)):**  The probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller *given* that the witness identified them as such (what we want to calculate).

**Bayes' Theorem**

```
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B) 
```

To find P(B), the probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller, we use the law of total probability:

```
P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|~A) * P(~A)]
```

Where P(~A) is the probability of a randomly chosen person being shorter than 185 cm (85%).
