**Combining the Information**

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem gives us a way to update our belief in an event (attacker being 185cm or taller) given new evidence (witness testimony).

Let:

* Event A: Attacker is 185cm or taller.
* Event B: Witness identifies the attacker as 185cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller given the witness said so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(B|A): Probability the witness would say "185cm or taller" IF the attacker was actually 185cm or taller (this is 80% or 0.8).
* P(A): Prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller (this is 15% or 0.15).
* P(B): Probability the witness would say "185cm or taller" (this can happen in two ways: the attacker is actually tall and the witness is right, OR the attacker is shorter, and the witness is wrong).
