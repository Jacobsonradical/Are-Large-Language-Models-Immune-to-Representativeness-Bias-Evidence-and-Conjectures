Here's how we can approach this problem, combining witness testimony with population statistics:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability:** The witness is 80% accurate in judging height under similar conditions. This means their testimony isn't perfect.
* **Population Distribution:** Only 15% of people in the city are 185cm or taller. This is our prior probability before considering the witness.

**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating our belief (the prior probability) based on new evidence. Here's how it applies:

Let:

*  A = Event that the attacker is 185cm or taller
*  B = Event that the witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is actually 185cm or taller given the witness's testimony.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each component:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would identify the attacker as tall *given* the attacker is actually tall. This is the witness's accuracy for tall people, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being tall, which is 15% or 0.15 based on the city's population.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness would identify someone as tall (whether they are actually tall or not). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
   -  P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B| not A) * P(not A)
   -  P(B| not A) is the probability the witness would identify a short person as tall (their error rate for short people), also 20% or 0.2.
   -  P(not A) is the probability of someone being shorter than 185cm, which is 85% or 0.85.

**Calculation**

1. **Calculate P(B):**
   - P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

2. **Calculate P(A|B):**
   - P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
