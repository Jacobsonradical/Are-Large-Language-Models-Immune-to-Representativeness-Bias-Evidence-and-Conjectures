2. **Scenario 2: The attacker IS NOT 185cm or taller.**  There's still a 20% chance the witness incorrectly identified them as being 185cm or taller (false positive).

**Calculation (Using Bayes' Theorem)**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating probabilities based on new evidence.  Let:

* Event A: The attacker is 185cm or taller.
* Event B: The witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the witness's identification.

* **P(B| not A) = 0.2** (Probability of witness incorrectly identifying height given the attacker is NOT that tall)
