Here's how we can approach this problem, combining witness testimony with population statistics:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability:** The witness is 80% accurate in judging height under similar conditions. This means their testimony isn't perfect.
* **Population Distribution:** This tells us the general likelihood of someone in the city being 185cm or taller.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a framework for updating probabilities based on new evidence. Here's how we can apply it:

Let:

*  Event A = Attacker is 185cm or taller
*  Event B = Witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* the witness's identification.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Let's break down each component:

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies someone as 185cm or taller *given* they actually are. This is the witness's accuracy rate for this specific case, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller, which is 15% or 0.15.

* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies someone as 185cm or taller (regardless of the attacker's actual height). We can calculate this using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B| not A) * P(not A)
    * P(B| not A): The probability the witness incorrectly identifies someone shorter than 185cm as being taller. This is their error rate, 20% or 0.2.
    * P(not A): The probability of someone in the city being shorter than 185cm, which is 85% or 0.85.

1. **Calculate P(B):**
   P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

2. **Calculate P(A|B):**
   P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
