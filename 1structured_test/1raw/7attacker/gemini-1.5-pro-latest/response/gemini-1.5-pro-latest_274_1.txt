**Understanding the Information**

* **Witness Reliability (a):** The witness is 80% accurate in judging height under similar conditions. This means their statement isn't perfect but holds valuable information.
* **Population Distribution (b):** This tells us the general likelihood of someone in the city being 185cm or taller. 

**Combining the Information**

We need to combine the witness testimony with the general population data. This is a classic example where Bayes' Theorem can help us update our belief based on evidence.

**Let's define some terms:**

* **Event A:** The attacker is 185cm or taller.
* **Event B:** The witness identified the attacker as 185cm or taller.

**We want to find P(A|B):**  The probability the attacker is 185cm or taller *given* that the witness said so.

**Bayes' Theorem states:**
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* **P(B|A):** Probability the witness would say "185cm or taller" IF the attacker actually was 185cm or taller (This is the witness's accuracy for this height group, 80% or 0.8). 
* **P(A):** Prior probability of someone in the city being 185cm or taller (15% or 0.15).
* **P(B):** Probability of the witness saying "185cm or taller" (This can happen in two ways: they are right, or they are wrong).

* P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B| not A) * P(not A)]
