- \( P(B|\neg A) = 0.2 \) (The witness incorrectly identifies height 20% of the time when the person is shorter than 185 cm)

We need to find \( P(A|B) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

\[ P(B) = P(B|A) \cdot P(A) + P(B|\neg A) \cdot P(\neg A) \]

\[ P(B) = (0.8 \cdot 0.15) + (0.2 \cdot 0.85) \]

\[ P(B) = 0.29 \]

\[ P(A|B) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
