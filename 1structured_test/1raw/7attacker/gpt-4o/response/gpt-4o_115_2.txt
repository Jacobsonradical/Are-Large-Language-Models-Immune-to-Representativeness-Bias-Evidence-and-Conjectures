- \( T \): The event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.

- \( W \): The event that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

We need to find \( P(T | W) \), the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

Bayes' theorem states:
\[ P(T | W) = \frac{P(W | T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W)} \]

Where:
- \( P(T) \) is the prior probability of the attacker being 185 cm or taller.
- \( P(W | T) \) is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller given that the attacker is indeed 185 cm or taller.
- \( P(W) \) is the overall probability that the witness identifies the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

\[ P(W) = P(W | T) \cdot P(T) + P(W | \neg T) \cdot P(\neg T) \]
\[ P(W) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.29 \]

\[ P(T | W) = \frac{P(W | T) \cdot P(T)}{P(W)} \]
\[ P(T | W) = \frac{0.80 \cdot 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(T | W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
