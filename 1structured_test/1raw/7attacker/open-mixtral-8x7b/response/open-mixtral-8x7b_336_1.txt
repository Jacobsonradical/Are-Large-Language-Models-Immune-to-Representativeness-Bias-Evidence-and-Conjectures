To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which is a formula that describes how to update the probabilities of hypotheses when given evidence.

Let's define:
- H: the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
- E: the event that the witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller

From the question, we have:
P(H) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
P(E|H) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a tall person as tall 80% of the time)
P(E|H') = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identifies a short person as tall 20% of the time, so they identify a tall person as short 20% of the time)
P(H') = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

Bayes' theorem states:
P(H|E) = [P(E|H) * P(H)] / [P(E|H) * P(H) + P(E|H') * P(H')]

P(E|H) * P(H) + P(E|H') * P(H') = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
