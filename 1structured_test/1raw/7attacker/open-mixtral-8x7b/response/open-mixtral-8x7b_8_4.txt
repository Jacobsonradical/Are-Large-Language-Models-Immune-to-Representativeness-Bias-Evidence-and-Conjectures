To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, the two events are the attacker being 185 cm or taller and the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Let's denote:
- A as the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller,
- B as the event that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

We are asked to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

According to Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

We are given:
1. P(A): The probability that an attacker is 185 cm or taller is 15% or 0.15.
2. P(B|A): The probability that a truly 185 cm or taller attacker is identified as such is 80% or 0.8.
3. P(B|not A): The probability that a shorter-than-185-cm attacker is identified as 185 cm or taller is 20% or 0.2 (the error rate of the witness).

To find P(B), we can use the law of total probability:
P(B) = P(B and A) + P(B and not A) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

P(not A) = 1 - P(A) = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85

P(B|not A) = 0.2

P(B) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.18 (18%)

Now we can calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B) = 0.8 * 0.15 / 0.18 = 0.6667 or approximately 67%
