- B as the event that the witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that a witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identified the height 80% of the time)

P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)

P(¬A) = 0.85 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

Bayes' theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)]

Now, let's plug in the values:

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / [(0.8 * 0.15) + (0.03 * 0.85)]

Calculating P(A|B):

P(A|B) ≈ 0.5649
