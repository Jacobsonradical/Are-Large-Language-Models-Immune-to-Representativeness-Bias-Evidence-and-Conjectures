We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given the witness's identification.

- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time for people 185 cm or taller)
- P(B|¬A) = 0.5 (witness randomly identifies the height 50% of the time for people shorter than 185 cm)

Using Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)]

First, calculate P(¬A):

P(¬A) = 1 - P(A) = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85

Now, calculate P(B):

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.5 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.425 = 0.545

Now, calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.545 ≈ 0.2165
