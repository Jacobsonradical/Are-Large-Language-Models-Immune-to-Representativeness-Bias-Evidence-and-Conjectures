A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
W: The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller

P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
P(W|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies an 185 cm or taller person 80% of the time)
P(W|not A) = 0.2 * 0.5 = 0.1 (the witness erroneously identifies a shorter person as 185 cm or taller 20% of the time, and 50% of the population is shorter than 185 cm)

Now we can calculate P(A|W), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller:

P(A|W) = P(W|A) * P(A) / P(W)
