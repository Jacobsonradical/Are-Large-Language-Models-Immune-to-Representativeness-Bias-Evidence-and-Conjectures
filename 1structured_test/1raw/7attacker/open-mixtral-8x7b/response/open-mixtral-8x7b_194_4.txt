- A as the event that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- B as the event that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

P(B|A) = 0.8 (witness accurately identifies a person as 185 cm or taller)

P(A) = 0.15 (prior probability of a person being 185 cm or taller)
P(¬A) = 0.85 (prior probability of a person being shorter than 185 cm)

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / (P(B|A) * P(A) + P(¬B|A) * P(¬A))
