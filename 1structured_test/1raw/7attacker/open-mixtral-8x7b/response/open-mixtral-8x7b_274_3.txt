To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, let A be the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller, and E be the event that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller. We want to find P(A|E), the probability that the attacker is indeed 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified him as such.

Bayes' theorem states that P(A|E) = P(E|A) * P(A) / P(E)

From the problem, we are given:
1. P(E|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a person as 185 cm or taller 80% of the time)
2. P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
3. To find P(E), we need to consider both the case where the attacker is 185 cm or taller and the case where the attacker is shorter than 185 cm:
   P(E) = P(E|A) * P(A) + P(E|not A) * P(not A)
   Since the witness is assumed to sometimes make mistakes, there is a chance that the witness identifies a person as 185 cm or taller even when they are not: P(E|not A) = 1 - P(not E|not A) = 1 - 0.8 = 0.2
   And since 85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm: P(not A) = 0.85

So, P(E) = (0.8)*(0.15) + (0.2)*(0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

Now we can calculate P(A|E) = P(E|A) * P(A) / P(E) = (0.8)*(0.15) / (0.29) = 0.345 approximately.
