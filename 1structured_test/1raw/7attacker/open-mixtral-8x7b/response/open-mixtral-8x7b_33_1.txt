P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time when the person is actually 185 cm or taller)
P(B|¬A) = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identifies the height 20% of the time when the person is not actually 185 cm or taller)

We need to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, given that the witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Bayes' theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)]

First, calculate P(¬A):

P(¬A) = 1 - P(A) = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85

Now, calculate P(B):

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)
P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

Now, we can calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 ≈ 0.345
