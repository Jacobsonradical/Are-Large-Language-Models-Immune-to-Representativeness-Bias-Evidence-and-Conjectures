P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is 185 cm or taller)
P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time when the person is 185 cm or taller)
P(B|¬A) = 0.4 (the witness incorrectly identifies the height 20% of the time when the person is not 185 cm or taller, and since 85% of the population is not 185 cm or taller, the probability of observing the witness's statement for a randomly chosen person who is not 185 cm or taller is 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17)

Now, we can calculate P(A|B) using Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / [P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|¬A) * P(¬A)]
= [0.8 * 0.15] / [(0.8 * 0.15) + (0.17 * 0.85)]
