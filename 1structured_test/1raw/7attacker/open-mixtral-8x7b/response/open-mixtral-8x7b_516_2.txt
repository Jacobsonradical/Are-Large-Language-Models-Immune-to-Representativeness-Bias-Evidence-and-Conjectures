To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, the events are the attacker's height being 185 cm or taller and the witness identifying the attacker as 185 cm or taller.

Let's denote:
- A as the event "attacker's height is 185 cm or taller"
- B as the event "witness identified the attacker as 185 cm or taller"

We are asked to find P(A|B), the probability of A given B. According to Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

From the given information, we know:
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies the height 80% of the time)
- P(A) = 0.15 (85% of the population is shorter than 185 cm and 15% are 185 cm or taller)

To find P(B), we need to consider two possibilities:
1. The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, P(B|A) * P(A) = 0.8 * 0.15 = 0.12
2. The attacker's height is shorter than 185 cm, P(B|A') * P(A') = 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17

So, P(B) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
