- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller.
- B: The witness identified the attacker as being 185 cm or taller.

P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

Where:
- P(B|A) is the probability of the witness correctly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller, given that the person is indeed 185 cm or taller. From the information given, this is 0.8 or 80%.
- P(A) is the prior probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller, which is given as 0.15 or 15%.
- P(B) is the probability of the witness identifying someone as 185 cm or taller, regardless of their actual height.

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|A') * P(A')

Where A' is the event that the attacker's height is shorter than 185 cm, and P(A') = 1 - P(A) = 0.85.

P(B|A') is the probability of the witness incorrectly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller when they are shorter than that. Since the witness has a 20% error rate, P(B|A') = 0.2.

Substituting the values, we get:

P(B) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.29

Now, we can calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.414
