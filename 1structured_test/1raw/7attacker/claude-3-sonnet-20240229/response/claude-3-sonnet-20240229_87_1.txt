- Sensitivity (probability of correctly identifying someone as 185 cm or taller when they are): 0.8
- Specificity (probability of correctly identifying someone as shorter than 185 cm when they are): 1 - 0.2 = 0.8

Let's define the following events:
- A: The attacker's height is 185 cm or taller
- B: The witness identifies the attacker as 185 cm or taller

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker's height is 185 cm or taller given that the witness identified them as such.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

P(B|A) is the sensitivity, which is 0.8.
P(A) is the prior probability that a person is 185 cm or taller, which is 0.15.

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
     = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85
     = 0.12 + 0.17
     = 0.29

Substituting the values in Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
