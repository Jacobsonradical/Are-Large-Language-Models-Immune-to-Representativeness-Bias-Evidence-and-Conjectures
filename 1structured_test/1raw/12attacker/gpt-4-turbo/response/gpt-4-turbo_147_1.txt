To estimate the probability that the attacker is male ({P(M)}), we need to use Bayes' theorem. Let's denote:

- P(M) as the probability of an attacker being male.
- P(F) as the probability of an attacker being female.
- P(W|M) as the probability of the witness correctly identifying the attacker as male given the attacker is male.
- P(W|F) as the probability of the witness incorrectly identifying the attacker as male given the attacker is female.

From the problem:
- P(M) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)
- P(F) = 0.85 (85% of the population is female)
- P(W|M) = 0.8 (The witness correctly identifies a male as male 80% of the time)
- P(W|F) = 0.2 (The witness wrongly identifies a female as male 20% of the time)

We apply Bayes' theorem which states:
\[ P(M|W) = \frac{P(W|M) P(M)}{P(W)} \]
Where:
\[ P(W) = P(W|M) P(M) + P(W|F) P(F) \]
\[ P(W) = (0.8 \times 0.15) + (0.2 \times 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

Thus, substituting back:
\[ P(M|W) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138 \]

Therefore, the probability that the attacker is male, given the witness identifying the attacker as male, is about 0.4138.
