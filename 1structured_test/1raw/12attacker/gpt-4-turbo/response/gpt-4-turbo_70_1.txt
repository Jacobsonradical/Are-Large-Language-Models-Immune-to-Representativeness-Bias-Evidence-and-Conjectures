To solve the problem, we use Bayes' Theorem to update our belief about the probability that the attacker is male given the witness's testimony and the population demographics.

Let \( M \) represent the event that the attacker is male and \( \neg M \) the event that the attacker is female. Let \( W \) represent the event the witness identifies the attacker as male.

From the information given:
- \( P(M) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is male)
- \( P(\neg M) = 0.85 \) (85% of the population is female)

The witness's accuracy:
- \( P(W | M) = 0.80 \) (80% chance the witness correctly identifies a male as male)
- \( P(W | \neg M) = 0.20 \) (20% chance the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male)

We aim to find \( P(M | W) \) (the probability the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male).

Using Bayes' Theorem, we have:
\[
P(M | W) = \frac{P(W | M) P(M)}{P(W)}
\]
where \( P(W) \) is the total probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male. Using the Law of Total Probability, we get:
\[
P(W) = P(W | M) P(M) + P(W | \neg M) P(\neg M)
\]

Substituting in the values:
\[
P(W) = (0.80 \times 0.15) + (0.20 \times 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
\]

Therefore:
\[
P(M | W) = \frac{0.80 \times 0.15}{0.29} = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.4138
\]

Hence, the probability that the attacker is male, given the witness's identification and the population distribution, is approximately 0.414.
