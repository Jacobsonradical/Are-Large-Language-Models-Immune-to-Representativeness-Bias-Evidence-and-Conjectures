To calculate the probability that the attacker is male given the information provided, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem states that:

\[ P(M|W) = \frac{P(W|M) \times P(M)}{P(W)} \]

where:
- \( P(M|W) \) is the probability the attacker is male given the witness identified the attacker as male.
- \( P(W|M) \) is the probability the witness identifies the attacker as male given the attacker is male.
- \( P(M) \) is the prior probability the attacker is male (without considering the witness's testimony).
- \( P(W) \) is the total probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male.

From the problem statement:
- \( P(M) = 0.15 \) (since 15% of the population is male)
- \( P(F) = 0.85 \) (since 85% of the population is female)
- \( P(W|M) = 0.8 \) (probability that the witness correctly identifies a male)
- \( P(W|F) = 0.2 \) (probability that the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male)

We need to find \( P(W) \), which is the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as male, whether the attacker is actually male or female:
\[ P(W) = P(W|M) \times P(M) + P(W|F) \times P(F) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.8 \times 0.15 + 0.2 \times 0.85 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(W) = 0.29 \]

Plugging these into Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(M|W) = \frac{0.8 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(M|W) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
\[ P(M|W) \approx 0.4138 \]
