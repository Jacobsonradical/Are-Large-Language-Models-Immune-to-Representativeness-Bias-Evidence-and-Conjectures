To solve this problem, let's use Bayes' Theorem. Let's define the events as follows:
- M: The attacker is male.
- F: The attacker is female.
- W: The witness identifies the attacker as male.

- The probability of a person being female (P(F)) is 85%, or 0.85.
- The witness correctly identifies gender 80% of the time, and makes an error 20% of the time.

- P(W | M) (probability witness correctly identifies a male as male) = 0.8
- P(W | F) (probability witness incorrectly identifies a female as male) = 0.2

\[ P(M | W) = \frac{P(W | M) * P(M)}{P(W)} \]

where:
\[ P(W) = P(W | M) * P(M) + P(W | F) * P(F) \]

Calculating P(W):
\[ P(W) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) \]
\[ P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29 \]

\[ P(M | W) = \frac{0.8 * 0.15}{0.29} \]
