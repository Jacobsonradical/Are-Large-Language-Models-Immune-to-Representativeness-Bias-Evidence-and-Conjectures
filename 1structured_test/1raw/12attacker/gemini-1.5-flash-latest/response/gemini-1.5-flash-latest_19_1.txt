Here's how we can calculate the probability:

**1. Prior Probability:**

* The prior probability of the attacker being male is simply the proportion of males in the city: 15% or 0.15.

**2. Likelihood of the Evidence:**

* **Correct Identification:** The witness correctly identified a male as male 80% of the time.
* **Incorrect Identification:** The witness incorrectly identified a female as male 20% of the time.

**3. Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us update our belief about the attacker's gender based on the witness's testimony:

P(Male | Witness) = [P(Witness | Male) * P(Male)] / P(Witness)

Where:

* P(Male | Witness): Probability of the attacker being male given the witness's testimony.
* P(Witness | Male): Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male given the attacker is actually male (80% or 0.8).
* P(Male): Prior probability of the attacker being male (0.15).
* P(Witness): Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male (we'll calculate this below).

**4. Calculating P(Witness)**

To get P(Witness), we need to consider both scenarios:

* **Scenario 1:** Attacker is male (probability 0.15): Witness identifies correctly (0.8)
* **Scenario 2:** Attacker is female (probability 0.85): Witness identifies incorrectly (0.2)

P(Witness) = (0.15 * 0.8) + (0.85 * 0.2) = 0.29

**5. Final Calculation**

P(Male | Witness) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.4138

{probability: 0.4138}
