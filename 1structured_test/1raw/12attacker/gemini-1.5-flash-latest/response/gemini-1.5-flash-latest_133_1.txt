We have two sources of information:

**Using Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem helps us calculate this:

P(Male | Witness) = [P(Witness | Male) * P(Male)] / P(Witness)

* P(Male | Witness) = Probability of the attacker being male given the witness's testimony.
* P(Witness | Male) = Probability of the witness identifying someone as male, given they are actually male (80% from the information).

* P(Witness) = Probability of the witness making a specific identification (we'll calculate this below).
