**Understanding Bayes' Theorem**

This problem requires Bayes' Theorem, which helps us update our beliefs about an event based on new evidence.  The formula is:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B): The probability of event A happening given that event B has happened.
* P(B|A): The probability of event B happening given that event A has happened.
* P(A): The prior probability of event A happening.
* P(B): The prior probability of event B happening.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem to our Problem**

* **Let's define our events:**
    * A: The attacker is male.
    * B: The witness identified the attacker as male.

* **Let's assign probabilities based on the given information:**
    * P(A): The prior probability of the attacker being male is 0.15 (15% of the population).
    * P(B|A): The probability of the witness correctly identifying a male as male is 0.8 (80% accuracy).
    * P(B|not A): The probability of the witness incorrectly identifying a female as male is 0.2 (20% error rate).

* **To find P(B), we need to consider both scenarios (attacker is male or female):**
    * P(B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] + [P(B|not A) * P(not A)] 
    * P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.26

* **Now we can calculate P(A|B) - the probability the attacker is male given the witness's testimony:**
    * P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
