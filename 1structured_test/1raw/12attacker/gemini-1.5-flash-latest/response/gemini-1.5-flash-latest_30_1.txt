Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Problem**

We have prior information about the gender distribution in the city (85% female, 15% male). This is our "prior probability."  We also have information from a witness who identified the attacker as male, but we know this witness isn't perfect.  We need to combine this witness information with our prior knowledge to get a more accurate estimate of the probability that the attacker is male.

**Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Where:

* P(A|B) is the probability of event A happening given that event B has happened. This is what we want to find: the probability the attacker is male (A) given the witness's testimony (B).
* P(B|A) is the probability of event B happening given that event A has happened. This is the witness's accuracy in identifying a male: 80%.
* P(A) is the prior probability of event A happening. This is the probability of a randomly chosen person in the city being male: 15%.
* P(B) is the probability of event B happening. This is a bit more complex, as it's the overall probability of the witness identifying someone as male, regardless of their actual gender. 

**Calculating P(B)**

To find P(B), we need to consider both scenarios:

1. **Witness identifies a male correctly:** This happens with a probability of 80% * 15% = 12% 
2. **Witness identifies a female incorrectly as male:** This happens with a probability of 20% * 85% = 17%

Therefore, P(B) = 12% + 17% = 29%

**Putting it Together**

Now we can plug everything into Bayes' Theorem:

P(Male | Witness says Male) = [0.80 * 0.15] / 0.29
