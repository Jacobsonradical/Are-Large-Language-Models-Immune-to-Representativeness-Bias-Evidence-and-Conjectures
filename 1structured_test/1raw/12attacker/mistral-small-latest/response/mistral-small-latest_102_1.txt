Based on the provided information, we can use Bayes' theorem to calculate the probability. Let's denote:

- M: The attacker is male
- F: The attacker is female
- W: The witness identified the attacker as male

Now we want to find P(M|W), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

Using Bayes' theorem: P(M|W) = [P(W|M) * P(M)] / [P(W|M) * P(M) + P(W|F) * P(F)]

Plugging in the values, we get:

P(M|W) ≈ [0.8 * 0.15] / [0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85] ≈ 0.36
