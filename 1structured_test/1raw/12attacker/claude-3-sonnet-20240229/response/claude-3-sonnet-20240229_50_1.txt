Given:
- A witness identified the attacker as male, with an accuracy of 80% (true positive rate).
- The probability of misidentifying a male as a female is 20% (false negative rate).
- The prior probability that the attacker is male is 15% (based on the population statistics).

- M: The attacker is male.
- W: The witness identified the attacker as male.

Bayes' theorem states:
P(M|W) = (P(W|M) × P(M)) / P(W)

Where:
- P(W|M) is the probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male, given that the attacker is male (true positive rate = 0.8).
- P(M) is the prior probability that the attacker is male (0.15).
- P(W) is the total probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male, which can be calculated using the law of total probability:
  P(W) = P(W|M) × P(M) + P(W|¬M) × P(¬M)
  = 0.8 × 0.15 + 0.2 × 0.85 (assuming the false positive rate is 0.2)

  = 0.29

P(M|W) = (0.8 × 0.15) / 0.29
