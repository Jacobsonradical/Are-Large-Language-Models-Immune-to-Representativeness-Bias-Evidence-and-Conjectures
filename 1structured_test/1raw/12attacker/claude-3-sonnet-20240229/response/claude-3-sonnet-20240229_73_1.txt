Let M denote the event that the attacker is male, and D denote the evidence that the witness identified the attacker as male.

We want to find P(M|D), the probability that the attacker is male given the witness's identification.

According to Bayes' theorem:

P(M|D) = (P(D|M) * P(M)) / P(D)

Given:
- P(M) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)
- P(D|M) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a male 80% of the time)
- P(D|~M) = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male 20% of the time)
