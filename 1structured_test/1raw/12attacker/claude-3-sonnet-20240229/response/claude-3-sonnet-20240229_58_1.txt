- 85% of the population in the city is female, and 15% is male.

- A: The attacker is male.
- B: The witness identifies the attacker as male.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B)

P(B|A) is the probability that the witness identifies the attacker as male, given that the attacker is actually male. This is the accuracy of the witness, which is 0.8 or 80%.

P(A) is the prior probability that the attacker is male, which is 0.15 or 15% based on the population distribution.

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|A') * P(A')
     = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85
     = 0.29

Substituting the values in Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
