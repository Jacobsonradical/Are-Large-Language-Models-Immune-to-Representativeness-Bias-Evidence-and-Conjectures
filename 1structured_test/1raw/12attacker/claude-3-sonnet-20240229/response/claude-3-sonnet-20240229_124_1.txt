To estimate the probability that the attacker is male, we need to apply Bayes' theorem, which combines the prior probability (based on the population statistics) with the likelihood (based on the witness's testimony).

Given:
- The prior probability of the attacker being male is 0.15 (based on 15% of the population being male).
- The probability of the witness correctly identifying a male as male is 0.8 (80% accuracy).
- The probability of the witness incorrectly identifying a female as male is 0.2 (20% error rate).

Let's denote the event that the attacker is male as M, and the event that the witness identifies the attacker as male as W.

We want to find P(M|W), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

Using Bayes' theorem:
P(M|W) = (P(W|M) × P(M)) / (P(W|M) × P(M) + P(W|¬M) × P(¬M))

Where:
- P(W|M) = 0.8 (the probability of the witness correctly identifying a male as male)
- P(M) = 0.15 (the prior probability of the attacker being male)
- P(W|¬M) = 0.2 (the probability of the witness incorrectly identifying a female as male)
- P(¬M) = 0.85 (the prior probability of the attacker being female)

Substituting the values:
P(M|W) = (0.8 × 0.15) / (0.8 × 0.15 + 0.2 × 0.85)
       = 0.12 / (0.12 + 0.17)
       = 0.12 / 0.29
       ≈ 0.4138
