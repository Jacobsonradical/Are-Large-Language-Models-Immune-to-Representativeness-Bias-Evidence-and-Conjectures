- \( M \) be the event that the attacker is male.
- \( F \) be the event that the attacker is female.
- \( W_M \) be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as male.

We want to find \( P(M|W_M) \), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

Bayes' Theorem states:
\[ P(M|W_M) = \frac{P(W_M|M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W_M)} \]

Where:
- \( P(M) \) is the prior probability that the attacker is male.
- \( P(W_M|M) \) is the likelihood that the witness identifies the attacker as male given the attacker is male.
- \( P(W_M) \) is the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as male.

- \( P(M) = 0.15 \) (15% of the population is male).

\[ P(W_M) = P(W_M|M) \cdot P(M) + P(W_M|F) \cdot P(F) \]
\[ P(W_M) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85) \]

\[ P(M|W_M) = \frac{P(W_M|M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W_M)} \]
