1. \( P(W_M | M) = 0.80 \) (the probability that the witness correctly identifies a male as a male)
2. \( P(W_M | F) = 0.20 \) (the probability that the witness incorrectly identifies a female as a male)

4. \( P(F) = 0.85 \) (the prior probability that any randomly chosen person in the city is female)

First, we need to find \( P(W_M) \), the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as male. This can be done using the law of total probability:

\[ P(W_M) = P(W_M | M) P(M) + P(W_M | F) P(F) \]

Plugging in the values, we get:

\[ P(W_M) = (0.80 \times 0.15) + (0.20 \times 0.85) \]
\[ P(W_M) = 0.12 + 0.17 \]
\[ P(W_M) = 0.29 \]

Next, we use Bayes' Theorem to find the posterior probability that the attacker is male given the witness's identification as male:

\[ P(M | W_M) = \frac{P(W_M | M) P(M)}{P(W_M)} \]

Substituting the known values:

\[ P(M | W_M) = \frac{0.80 \times 0.15}{0.29} \]
\[ P(M | W_M) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \]
