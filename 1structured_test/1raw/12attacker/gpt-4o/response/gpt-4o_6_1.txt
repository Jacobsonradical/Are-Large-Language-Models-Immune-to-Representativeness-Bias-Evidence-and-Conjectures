- \( M \) represent the event that the attacker is male.
- \( F \) represent the event that the attacker is female.
- \( W_M \) represent the event that the witness identifies the attacker as male.

- Probability of the witness incorrectly identifying a female as a male (P(W_M | F)) is 0.20.

We need to find the posterior probability that the attacker is male given the witness's identification as male (P(M | W_M)). By Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(M | W_M) = \frac{P(W_M | M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W_M)} \]

\[ P(W_M) = P(W_M | M) \cdot P(M) + P(W_M | F) \cdot P(F) \]

\[ P(M | W_M) = \frac{P(W_M | M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W_M)} \]
