- \( M \): The attacker is male.
- \( F \): The attacker is female.
- \( W \): The witness identified the attacker as male.

2. \( P(F) = 0.85 \) (prior probability that the attacker is female).
3. \( P(W|M) = 0.8 \) (probability that the witness correctly identifies a male).
4. \( P(W|F) = 0.2 \) (probability that the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male).

Using Bayes' Theorem, we have:

\[
P(M|W) = \frac{P(W|M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W)}
\]

Where \( P(W) \) is the total probability that the witness identifies the attacker as male. This can be found using the law of total probability:

\[
P(W) = P(W|M) \cdot P(M) + P(W|F) \cdot P(F)
\]

\[
P(W) = 0.8 \cdot 0.15 + 0.2 \cdot 0.85

\[
P(W) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

\[
P(M|W) = \frac{0.8 \cdot 0.15}{0.29}
