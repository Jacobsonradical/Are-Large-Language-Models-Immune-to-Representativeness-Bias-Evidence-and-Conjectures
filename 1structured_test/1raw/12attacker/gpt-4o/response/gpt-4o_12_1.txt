To estimate the probability that the attacker is male given the witness's identification, we can use Bayes' theorem. Here's the process to get the answer:

1. **Define the Events**:
   - \( M \): The attacker is male.
   - \( F \): The attacker is female.
   - \( W_{\text{male}} \): The witness identifies the attacker as male.

2. **Given Probabilities**:
   - \( P(M) = 0.15 \)
   - \( P(F) = 0.85 \)
   - \( P(W_{\text{male}} | M) = 0.80 \)  (Correct identification if male)
   - \( P(W_{\text{male}} | F) = 0.20 \)  (Incorrect identification if female)

3. **Bayes' Theorem**:
   \[
   P(M | W_{\text{male}}) = \frac{P(W_{\text{male}} | M) \cdot P(M)}{P(W_{\text{male}})}
   \]
   where \( P(W_{\text{male}}) \) can be found using the law of total probability:
   \[
   P(W_{\text{male}}) = P(W_{\text{male}} | M) \cdot P(M) + P(W_{\text{male}} | F) \cdot P(F)
   \]

4. **Calculate \( P(W_{\text{male}}) \)**:
   \[
   P(W_{\text{male}}) = (0.80 \cdot 0.15) + (0.20 \cdot 0.85)
   \]
   \[
   P(W_{\text{male}}) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
   \]

5. **Apply Bayes' Theorem**:
   \[
   P(M | W_{\text{male}}) = \frac{0.80 \cdot 0.15}{0.29}
   \]
   \[
   P(M | W_{\text{male}}) = \frac{0.12}{0.29} \approx 0.414
   \]

Therefore:

\{probability: 0.414\}
