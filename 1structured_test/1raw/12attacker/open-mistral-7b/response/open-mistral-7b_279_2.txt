To estimate the probability that the attacker is male, we can use Bayes' theorem. Bayes' theorem is a fundamental concept in probability theory and is used to update the probability of a hypothesis based on evidence.

Let's denote:
- H: the hypothesis that the attacker is male
- E: the evidence that the witness identified the attacker as male

Bayes' theorem states that the probability of H given E (P(H|E)) is equal to the likelihood of E given H (P(E|H)) multiplied by the prior probability of H (P(H)) divided by the sum of the likelihood of E for all possible hypotheses multiplied by their prior probabilities (P(E|H1) * P(H1) + P(E|H2) * P(H2) + ...).
