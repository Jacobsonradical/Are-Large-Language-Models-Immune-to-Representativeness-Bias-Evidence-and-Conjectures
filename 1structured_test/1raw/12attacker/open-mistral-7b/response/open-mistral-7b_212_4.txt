Given the information provided, we can use Bayes' theorem to estimate the probability that the attacker is male. Bayes' theorem is a mathematical formula that updates the probability for a hypothesis based on evidence. In this case, the hypothesis is that the attacker is male, and the evidence is the witness's identification.

The formula for Bayes' theorem is:

P(H | E) = [P(E | H) * P(H)] / [P(E)]

Where:
- H is the hypothesis (attacker is male),
- E is the evidence (witness's identification),
- P(H | E) is the probability of the hypothesis given the evidence,
- P(E | H) is the probability of the evidence given the hypothesis,
- P(H) is the prior probability of the hypothesis,
- P(E) is the probability of the evidence.

- P(E | H) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies the gender 80% of the time when the person is male)
- P(H) = 0.15 (85% of the population is female, so 15% is male)
- P(E) = P(E | H) * P(H) + P(E | ¬H) * P(¬H)
    Where ¬H means "not H" (the attacker is female),
    And P(E | ¬H) = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identifies the gender 20% of the time when the person is female)
    And P(¬H) = 0.85 (85% of the population is female)

Now we can calculate P(E):

P(E) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

Finally, we can calculate P(H | E):

P(H | E) = [P(E | H) * P(H)] / P(E)
        = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
        ≈ 0.345
