Instead, we can use Bayes' theorem to find the posterior probability of the attacker being male given the witness's observation.

Bayes' theorem states:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

Here,
- A is the event that the attacker is male,
- B is the event that the witness identified the attacker as male,
- P(A) is the prior probability of the attacker being male (which we can find from the general population demographics),
- P(B|A) is the likelihood of the witness identifying a male as male (which we know is 80%),
- P(B|~A) is the likelihood of the witness identifying a female as male (which we can find by subtraction), and
- P(B) is the marginal likelihood of the witness identifying someone as male (which is the sum of the likelihood of the witness identifying a male as male and the likelihood of the witness identifying a female as male).

We can rewrite P(B) as:

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|~A) * P(~A)

Since we know P(B|A) and P(B|~A), we can find P(B) by plugging in the values and solving for it. Once we have P(B), we can find P(A|B) using Bayes' theorem.
