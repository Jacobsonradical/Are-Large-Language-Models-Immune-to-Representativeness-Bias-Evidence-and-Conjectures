To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which is:

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

where:
- P(A|B) is the probability of A given B
- P(B|A) is the probability of B given A
- P(A) and P(B) are the prior probabilities of A and B respectively

In our case, let A be the event that "the attacker is male" and B be the event that "the witness identified the attacker as male". We are asked to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that a witness identified the attacker as male.

From the information provided, we have:
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population in the city is male)
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identified the attacker's gender as male 80% of the time)
- P(B|not A) = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identified the attacker's gender as male 20% of the time, which means the witness incorrectly identified the attacker as female 20% of the time)
- P(not A) = 0.85 (since 85% of the population in the city is female)
- P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
= (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85)
= 0.12 + 0.17
= 0.29

Now we can calculate P(A|B):

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)
= (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
