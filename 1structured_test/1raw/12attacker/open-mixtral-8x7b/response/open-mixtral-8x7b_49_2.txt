To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, the two events are "the attacker is male" and "the witness identified the attacker as male".

Let's denote:
- A: the event "the attacker is male"
- B: the event "the witness identified the attacker as male"

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

According to Bayes' theorem:
P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

We know:
P(B|A) is the probability that the witness correctly identifies a male as male, which is 0.8 (80%).
P(A) is the prior probability that the attacker is male, which is 0.15 (15%) because 15% of the population is male.

To find P(B), we can use the law of total probability:
P(B) = P(B and A) + P(B and not A)
     = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

We know:
P(B|A) is 0.8 (80%) as previously stated.
P(A) is 0.15 (15%).
P(not A) is 0.85 (85%) because 85% of the population is female.

We still need to find P(B|not A), the probability that the witness identifies a female as male. This is the complement of the witness's ability to identify a female as female, which is 80%, so P(B|not A) is 1 - 0.8 = 0.2 (20%).

Now we can find P(B):
P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
     = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85
     = 0.12 + 0.17
     = 0.29

Finally, we can find P(A|B):
