A - the event that the attacker is male,
B - the event that the witness identified the attacker as male.

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

According to Bayes' theorem:

P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

- P(B|A) = 0.8 (probability of correct identification when the attacker is male)
- P(A) = 0.15 (prior probability that the attacker is male, from the city's population statistics)

P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

P(B) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
