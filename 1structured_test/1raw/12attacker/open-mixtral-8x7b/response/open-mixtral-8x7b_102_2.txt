To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. Here, the events are A (the attacker being male) and B (the witness identifying the attacker as male).

Bayes' theorem: P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

We have been given:
1. P(A): Probability of an attacker being male = 0.15 (15%)
2. P(B|A): Probability of the witness identifying a male as male = 0.8 (80%)
3. P(A'): Probability of an attacker being female = 0.85 (85%)
4. P(B|A'): Probability of the witness identifying a female as male = 0.2 (20%)

P(B) = P(B and A) + P(B and A')
