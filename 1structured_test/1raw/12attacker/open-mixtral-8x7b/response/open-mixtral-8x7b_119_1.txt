To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. Let's denote:

A = Attacker is male
W = Witness identifies the attacker as male

We want to find P(A|W), the probability that the attacker is male given that the witness identified the attacker as male.

According to Bayes' theorem:
P(A|W) = P(W|A) * P(A) / P(W)

From the problem, we know:
P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)
P(W|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a male as male 80% of the time)

To find P(W), we need to consider both possibilities - that the attacker is male and that the attacker is female. Using the law of total probability, we can calculate P(W) as follows:
P(W) = P(W|A) * P(A) + P(W|A') * P(A')
where A' denotes not A (attacker is female) and P(A') = 0.85 (85% of the population is female).

Also, P(W|A') = 0.2 (the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male 20% of the time).

Substituting these values into the equation for P(W):
P(W) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29
