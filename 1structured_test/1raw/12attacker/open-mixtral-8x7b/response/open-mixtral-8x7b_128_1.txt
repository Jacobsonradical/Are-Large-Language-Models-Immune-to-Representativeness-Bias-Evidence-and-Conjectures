To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, the two events are "the attacker is male" and "the witness identified the attacker as male".

Let's denote:
- A as the event "the attacker is male"
- B as the event "the witness identified the attacker as male"

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that a witness identified the attacker as male.

According to Bayes' theorem, P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

From the given information, we know that P(B|A) is the accuracy of the witness which is 80% or 0.8.

The probability that the attacker is male, P(A), is what we are trying to estimate. However, without any other information, a reasonable estimate might be the proportion of males in the population, which is 15% or 0.15.

Calculating P(B) requires us to consider all possibilities that lead to the witness identifying the attacker as male. This includes both the true positive cases (attacker is male and identified as male) and the false positive cases (attacker is female but identified as male). 

Given the witness's accuracy, the probability of a true positive is 0.8 (for males) and the probability of a false positive is 0.2 (for females). Since 85% of the population is female, P(B) = 0.8 * 0.15 (males) + 0.2 * 0.85 (females) = 0.187 + 0.17 ≈ 0.357.

Substituting these values into Bayes' theorem, we get:

P(A|B) = 0.8 * 0.15 / 0.357 ≈ 0.336
