Bayes' theorem formula:
P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

Let A be the event that the attacker is male, and B be the event that the witness identified the attacker as male.

P(B|A): Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male given that the attacker is male. Since the witness correctly identifies gender 80% of the time, P(B|A) = 0.8.

P(A): Probability of the attacker being male, which we want to find.

P(B): Probability of the witness identifying a person as male.

To calculate P(B), we can use the law of total probability.
P(B) = P(B and A) + P(B and not A)
= P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)

We know P(B|A) = 0.8, P(not A) = 1 - P(A), and P(B|not A) = 1 - P(B|A) = 0.2 (20% error rate). To find P(B), we need to calculate P(B|not A) * P(not A) for both possible values of P(A).

Case 1: P(A)=1 (attacker is definitely male)
P(B) = 0.8 * 1 + 0.2 * 0 = 0.8

Case 2: P(A)=0 (attacker is definitely female)
P(B) = 0.8 * 0 + 0.2 * 1 = 0.2

Since we don't know the attacker's gender, we'll consider both cases:
P(B) = max(0.8, 0.2) = 0.8

Now we can calculate P(A|B) (probability that the attacker is male):
P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)
= 0.8 * P(A) / 0.8
= P(A)
