To solve this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of statistical quantities.

Let's define:
- Event A: The attacker is male
- Event B: A witness identified the attacker as male

We want to find P(A|B), the probability that the attacker is male given that a witness identified the attacker as male.

According to Bayes' theorem, P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

From the information given, we know that:
- P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)
- P(B|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a male as male 80% of the time)

- P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A) + P(B|not A) * P(not A)
- P(B|not A) is the probability that the witness incorrectly identifies a female as male, which is 0.2 (the complement of the witness's 80% accuracy rate)
- P(not A) is the probability that the attacker is female, which is 0.85 (85% of the population is female)

So, P(B) = (0.8)*(0.15) + (0.2)*(0.85) = 0.18 + 0.17 = 0.35
