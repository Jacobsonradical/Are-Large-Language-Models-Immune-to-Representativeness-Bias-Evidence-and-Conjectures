To reason through this problem, we can use Bayes' theorem which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of two events. In this case, let A represent the event that the attacker is male, and E represent the event that the witness identified the attacker as male. We want to find P(A|E), the probability that the attacker is male given that a witness identified the attacker as male.

Bayes' theorem states that P(A|E) = P(E|A) * P(A) / P(E)

We are given:
P(E|A) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies males as males 80% of the time)
P(A) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)

To calculate P(E), we need to consider both the case where the attacker is male and the case where the attacker is female. Using the law of total probability, we get:

P(E) = P(E|A) * P(A) + P(E|not A) * P(not A)

We are given that P(E|A) = 0.8, and since P(not A) = 1 - P(A) = 0.85 and the witness is wrong 20% of the time when identifying females, we have P(E|not A) = 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.17

So, P(E) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.17 * 0.85 = 0.203
