Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayesian reasoning:

**1. Define Events:**

* **M:** The attacker is male.
* **F:** The attacker is female.
* **W:** The witness identifies the attacker as male.

**2. Prior Probabilities (from information "a"):**

* **P(M) = 0.15** (Prior probability of the attacker being male)
* **P(F) = 0.85** (Prior probability of the attacker being female)

**3. Likelihood (from information "b"):**

* **P(W|M) = 0.80** (Probability the witness identifies male given the attacker is male)
* **P(W|F) = 0.20** (Probability the witness identifies male given the attacker is female)

**4. Bayes' Theorem:**

We want to find **P(M|W)**, the probability the attacker is male given the witness identified them as male. Bayes' Theorem states:

```
P(M|W) = [P(W|M) * P(M)] / P(W)
```

To calculate the denominator, we use the law of total probability:

```
P(W) = P(W|M) * P(M) + P(W|F) * P(F)
```

**5. Calculation:**

* **P(W) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29**
* **P(M|W) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414**

**Answer:**

{probability: 0.414}

Even though the witness has a relatively high accuracy (80%), the prior probability of a male attacker in the city is significantly lower.
