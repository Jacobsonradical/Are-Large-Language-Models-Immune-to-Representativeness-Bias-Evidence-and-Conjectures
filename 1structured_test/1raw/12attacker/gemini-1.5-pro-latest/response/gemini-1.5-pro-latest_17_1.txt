Here's how to approach this problem, combining base rates and witness testimony:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Base Rate (Prior Probability):**  The city's population makeup gives us the base rate.  If we knew nothing else, there's an 85% chance a randomly chosen person is female and a 15% chance they are male.
* **Witness Testimony (Likelihood):** The witness's accuracy is crucial. They aren't perfect, correctly identifying gender 80% of the time. This means there's a chance they could misidentify a female attacker as male or vice-versa.

**Calculations**

We can't directly use the 80% accuracy because it doesn't inherently factor in the base rate.  Here's a way to think about it:

1. **Scenario 1: Attacker is actually male.**
   * There's a 15% chance of this being true (base rate).
   * If they are male, there's an 80% chance the witness correctly identifies them.

2. **Scenario 2: Attacker is actually female.**
   * There's an 85% chance of this being true (base rate).
   * If they are female, there's a 20% chance the witness incorrectly identifies them as male.

To get a better estimate, we need to consider both scenarios and how likely they are:

* **Probability of male attacker AND correct identification:** 0.15 * 0.80 = 0.12
* **Probability of female attacker AND incorrect identification:** 0.85 * 0.20 = 0.17

Now, the probability of the witness saying "male" is the sum of these two probabilities: 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

Finally, to get the probability the attacker IS male GIVEN the witness said "male", we use:

(Probability of male attacker AND correct ID) / (Probability of witness saying "male") 

This gives us: 0.12 / 0.29 = approximately 0.414
