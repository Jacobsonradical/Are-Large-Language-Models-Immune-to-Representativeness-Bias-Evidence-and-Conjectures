Here's how to approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Let:**

*  Event A: The attacker is male.
*  Event B: The witness identifies the attacker as male.

**We want to find:** P(A|B), the probability the attacker is male given the witness identified them as male.

**Bayes' Theorem states:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

**Let's break down each term:**

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as male *given* the attacker is actually male. This is the witness's accuracy for identifying males, which is 80% or 0.8.

* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being male. This is based on the city's population and is 15% or 0.15. 

* **P(B):** The probability the witness identifies the attacker as male, regardless of the attacker's actual gender. This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) =  P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
    * Where ~A is the event that the attacker is not male (i.e., female).
    * P(B|~A) is the probability the witness misidentifies a female as male, which is 20% or 0.2.
    * P(~A) is the probability the attacker is female, which is 85% or 0.85.

**Calculations:**

1. **P(B):**  (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

{probability: 0.414}
