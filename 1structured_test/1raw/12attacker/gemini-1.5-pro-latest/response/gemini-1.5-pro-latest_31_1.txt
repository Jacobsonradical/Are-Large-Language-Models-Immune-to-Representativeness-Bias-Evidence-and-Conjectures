**Explanation:**

We can use Bayes' theorem to update our prior belief (the population proportions) with the new evidence from the witness testimony.

* **Prior probability of attacker being male (P(Male)):** 0.15 (15% of the population)
* **Prior probability of attacker being female (P(Female)):** 0.85 (85% of the population)
* **Likelihood of witness saying "male" given the attacker is male (P("Male" | Male)):** 0.80 (80% accuracy)
* **Likelihood of witness saying "male" given the attacker is female (P("Male" | Female)):** 0.20 (20% error rate)

We want to find the **posterior probability of the attacker being male given the witness said "male" (P(Male | "Male"))**.

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(Male | "Male") = [P("Male" | Male) * P(Male)] / P("Male")

To calculate P("Male"), we use the law of total probability:

P("Male") =  [P("Male" | Male) * P(Male)] + [P("Male" | Female) * P(Female)]
P("Male") = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

**Now we can plug the values back into Bayes' Theorem:**

P(Male | "Male") = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.52

**Therefore, the estimated probability that the attacker is male, given the witness testimony, is 0.52 or 52%.**
