Here's how to approach this problem, breaking it down into parts:

**1. Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability:** The information about the city's population (85% female, 15% male) gives us the *prior probability* of the attacker being male.  If we knew nothing else, we'd say there's a 15% chance the attacker is male.

* **Witness Reliability:** The witness's 80% accuracy rate is crucial. This tells us how much we can trust their statement, given that they aren't perfect.

**2. Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem is ideal for this kind of problem. It helps us update our prior belief (15% chance of a male attacker) with new evidence (witness testimony). 

Let:

*  "M" be the event that the attacker is male
*  "F" be the event that the attacker is female
*  "W" be the event that the witness identifies the attacker as male

We want to find P(M|W), the probability the attacker is male *given* the witness said so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(M|W) = [P(W|M) * P(M)] / P(W)

Let's break down the terms:

* **P(M|W):** What we want to find.
* **P(W|M):** The probability the witness would say "male" *if* the attacker was actually male. This is their accuracy rate for identifying males, which is 80% or 0.8.
* **P(M):** The prior probability of the attacker being male, which is 15% or 0.15.
* **P(W):** The probability the witness would say "male" regardless of the attacker's actual gender.  This is trickier and requires us to consider both scenarios (attacker is male and attacker is female).

**3. Calculating P(W)**

We can calculate P(W) using the law of total probability:

P(W) = P(W|M) * P(M)  +  P(W|F) * P(F)

* We already know P(W|M) = 0.8 and P(M) = 0.15
* P(W|F) is the probability the witness would say "male" if the attacker was actually female. This is their error rate for identifying females, which is 20% or 0.2.
* P(F) is the prior probability of the attacker being female, which is 85% or 0.85.

Therefore:
P(W) = (0.8 * 0.15) + (0.2 * 0.85) = 0.29

**4. Putting it all Together**

Now we can plug the values back into Bayes' Theorem:

P(M|W) = (0.8 * 0.15) / 0.29
