We can use Bayes' Theorem to update our prior belief (the population distribution of genders) with the new information from the witness testimony. 

* **Prior probability of attacker being male (P(Male)):** 0.15 (given as 15% of the population)
* **Prior probability of attacker being female (P(Female)):** 0.85 (given as 85% of the population)
* **Likelihood of witness correctly identifying male given the attacker is male (P(Correct | Male)):** 0.80 (80% accuracy)
* **Likelihood of witness incorrectly identifying male given the attacker is female (P(Correct | Female)):** 0.20 (20% error rate)

We want to find the posterior probability of the attacker being male given the witness said it was a male (P(Male | Correct)).

**Bayes' Theorem:**

P(Male | Correct) = [P(Correct | Male) * P(Male)] / P(Correct)

To find P(Correct), we use the law of total probability:

P(Correct) = [P(Correct | Male) * P(Male)] + [P(Correct | Female) * P(Female)]
P(Correct) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

Now we can plug everything back into Bayes' Theorem:

P(Male | Correct) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 = 0.414
