Here's how we can approach this problem, combining prior information (population demographics) with witness testimony:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability:** The chance of a random person in the city being male is 15% or 0.15. This is our starting point.
* **Witness Reliability:** The witness is not perfect. They have an 80% chance of being correct about a person's gender under similar conditions. 
* **Goal:** We want to update our initial belief (15% chance the attacker is male) based on the witness's testimony.

**Using Bayes' Theorem (A Simplified Approach)**

While we can use Bayes' Theorem directly, let's simplify:

1. **Scenarios:** Imagine 100 attacks happened under identical circumstances. 
    * Based on the population, we'd expect around 15 of those attacks to be by males.
    * Of those 15 attacks by males, the witness would likely identify 80% correctly (0.80 * 15 = 12). 

2. **False Positives:** The witness also has a 20% error rate.  Out of the 85 attacks we'd expect to be by females, the witness would incorrectly identify 20% as male (0.20 * 85 = 17).

3. **Total "Male" Identifications:** The witness would identify around 29 cases as male (12 correctly + 17 incorrectly).

4. **Updated Probability:** Out of those 29 cases, only 12 were actually males. The probability the attacker is male given the witness testimony is approximately 12/29 = 0.41.
