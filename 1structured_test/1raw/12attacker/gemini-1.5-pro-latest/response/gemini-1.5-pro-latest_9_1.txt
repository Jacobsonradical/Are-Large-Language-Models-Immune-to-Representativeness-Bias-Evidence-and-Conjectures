Here's how we can approach this problem using Bayes' Theorem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability:** The information about the city's population (85% female, 15% male) gives us the *prior probability* of the attacker being male. This is our baseline before considering the witness testimony.
* **Witness Reliability:** The witness's 80% accuracy rate tells us how much we should update our belief based on their testimony. This is not perfect information, so we need to adjust accordingly.

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

Bayes' Theorem provides a way to calculate the probability of an event (attacker being male) given new evidence (witness testimony), taking into account the prior probability.

Let:

*  M = Event that the attacker is male
*  W = Event that the witness identifies the attacker as male

We want to find P(M|W), the probability the attacker is male given the witness said so.

Bayes' Theorem states:

P(M|W) = [P(W|M) * P(M)] / P(W)

Let's break down each term:

* **P(W|M):** Probability the witness would identify the attacker as male *given* the attacker is actually male (witness accuracy for males). This is 0.80 (80%).
* **P(M):** Prior probability of the attacker being male (from city demographics). This is 0.15 (15%).
* **P(W):** Probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male (regardless of the attacker's actual gender). This can be calculated using the law of total probability: 
    * P(W) = P(W|M) * P(M) + P(W|~M) * P(~M) 
    * Where ~M is the event that the attacker is not male (i.e., female).
    * We know P(W|~M) is 0.20 (the witness misidentifies females as male 20% of the time) and P(~M) is 0.85.

**Calculation**

1. **Calculate P(W):**
    * P(W) = (0.80 * 0.15) + (0.20 * 0.85) = 0.29

2. **Calculate P(M|W):**
    * P(M|W) = (0.80 * 0.15) / 0.29 â‰ˆ 0.414
