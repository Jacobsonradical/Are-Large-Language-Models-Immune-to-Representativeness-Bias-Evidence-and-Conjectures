**Understanding the Problem**

* **Witness Testimony:** The witness has a decent, but not perfect, track record of identifying gender in similar conditions.

To get the most accurate probability, we'll use a Bayesian approach.

**Let's break it down:**

* **Event A:** The attacker is male.
* **Event B:** The witness identifies the attacker as male.

**We want to find P(A|B):** The probability the attacker is male *given* the witness said it was.

**Here's the Bayesian formula:**

P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)

**Let's break down each term:**

* **P(B|A):** The probability the witness would identify the attacker as male *if* the attacker was actually male (this is the witness's accuracy for identifying males).  This is **0.80** (80%).
* **P(A):** The prior probability of the attacker being male. We'll use the city's population data for this: **0.15** (15%).
* **P(B):** The probability of the witness identifying the attacker as male (regardless of the attacker's actual gender). This can be calculated using the law of total probability:
    * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)  +  P(B|~A) * P(~A) 
    * Where ~A is the event that the attacker is not male (i.e., female).
    * P(B|~A) is the probability the witness would misidentify a female as male, which is **0.20** (20%).
    * P(~A) is the probability the attacker is female, which is **0.85** (85%).
