**Reasoning**

We need to combine the witness testimony with the base rate to get an accurate estimate. Here's a way to think about it:

1. **Imagine 100 similar attacks.**  Given the base rates, we'd expect around 15 of these attacks to be committed by males and 85 by females.

2. **The witness's accuracy.** Of those 15 attacks committed by males, the witness would correctly identify the attacker as male 80% of the time (15 * 0.8 = 12). They would misidentify 20% (15 * 0.2 = 3) as female.

3. **False positives.**  Of the 85 attacks committed by females, the witness would incorrectly identify 20% (85 * 0.2 = 17) as male. 

4. **Total "male" identifications.** The witness would identify a total of 29 attackers as male (12 correctly identified males + 17 incorrectly identified females).

5. **Probability:** Out of these 29, only 12 were actually male.  Therefore, the probability that the attacker is male given the witness's identification is 12/29.

**Calculation**

The probability can be calculated as:

(0.15 * 0.8) / ((0.15 * 0.8) + (0.85 * 0.2)) = 0.4138
