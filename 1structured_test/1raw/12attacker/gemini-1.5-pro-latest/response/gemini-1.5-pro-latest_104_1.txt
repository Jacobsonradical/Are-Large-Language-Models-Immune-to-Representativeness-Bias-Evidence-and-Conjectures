**Understanding the Problem**

**Applying Bayes' Theorem**

While we don't need the full calculation of Bayes' Theorem, it provides a framework for understanding how to update our belief based on new evidence:

* **P(A|B) = [P(B|A) * P(A)] / P(B)**

Where:

* P(A|B): Probability of event A (attacker is male) given event B (witness said male)
* P(B|A): Probability of event B (witness said male) given event A (attacker is male) = 80% (witness accuracy)
* P(A):  Prior probability of event A (attacker is male) = 15% 
* P(B): Probability of event B (witness said male) - This is more complex to calculate directly.

1. **Start with the base rate:** 15% chance the attacker is male.
2. **The witness is 80% accurate:**  This suggests a higher likelihood than the base rate, but it's not a guaranteed 80% chance the attacker is male.
3. **Consider the error rate:**  The witness is wrong 20% of the time.

Considering the witness's accuracy and the base rate, the probability of the attacker being male is likely higher than the 15% base rate but lower than the 80% witness accuracy.  A reasonable estimate would be somewhere between these two values.
