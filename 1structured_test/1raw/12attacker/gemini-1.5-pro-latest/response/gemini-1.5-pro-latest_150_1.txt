Here's how to approach this problem:

**Understanding the Information**

* **Prior Probability:** The information about the city's population (85% female, 15% male) gives us the *prior probability* of the attacker being male.  If we had no other information, we'd say there's a 15% chance the attacker is male.

* **Witness Reliability:** The witness's accuracy (80%) is crucial. However, they are not perfect. We need to consider the possibility they misidentified the attacker's gender.

* **Combining Probabilities:**  This problem requires us to combine the prior probability with the witness's testimony to get a more accurate estimate. This is a classic example where Bayes' Theorem would be helpful, but we can approach it intuitively.

**Reasoning**

Let's imagine 100 similar attack scenarios in this city:

* Based on the population, we'd expect around 15 of these attacks to have a male attacker and 85 to have a female attacker.
* Of the 15 attacks with male attackers, the witness would correctly identify roughly 80% of them, or about 12 cases.
* However, the witness would also incorrectly identify 20% of the 85 attacks with female attackers as male, which is about 17 cases.

**Estimating the Probability**

Now, considering only the cases where the witness claimed the attacker was male, we have:

* Around 12 cases where they were correct (actual male attacker).
* Around 17 cases where they were incorrect (actual female attacker).

Therefore, the probability that the attacker is actually male, given the witness's testimony, is closer to 12/(12+17) than it is to the original 15%.
