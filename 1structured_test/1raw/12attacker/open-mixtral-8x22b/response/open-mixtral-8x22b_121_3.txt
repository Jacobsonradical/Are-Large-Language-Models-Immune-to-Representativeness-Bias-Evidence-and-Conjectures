- F: The attacker is female
- W: The witness identified the attacker as male
- P(A): The prior probability of event A
- P(A|B): The probability of event A given that B is true

According to Bayes' theorem:
P(M|W) = P(W|M) * P(M) / P(W)

- P(W): The overall probability that the witness identifies someone as male, which is a weighted average of P(W|M) and P(W|F)

P(W) = P(W|M) * P(M) + P(W|F) * P(F)
P(W) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.12 + 0.17 = 0.29

P(M|W) = P(W|M) * P(M) / P(W)
P(M|W) = 0.8 * 0.15 / 0.29 = 0.12 / 0.29 = 0.4137931034482759
