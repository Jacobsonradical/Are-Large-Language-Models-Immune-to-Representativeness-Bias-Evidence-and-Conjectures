- M as the event that the attacker is male
- W as the event that the witness identified the attacker as male

According to Bayes' theorem:
P(M|W) = P(W|M) * P(M) / P(W)

- P(W|M) = 0.8 (the witness correctly identifies a male as male 80% of the time)
- P(M) = 0.15 (15% of the population is male)

P(W) = P(W|M) * P(M) + P(W|not M) * P(not M)

P(W) = 0.8 * 0.15 + 0.2 * 0.85 = 0.295
