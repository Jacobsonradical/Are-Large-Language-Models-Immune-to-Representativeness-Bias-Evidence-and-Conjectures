To solve the problem, we can apply Bayes' Theorem, which relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of random events. In this case, we want to find the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the attack occurred in a neighborhood where 20% of the residents are 185 cm or taller.

Here are the key probabilities we need to use:
- P(T): Probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- P(N): Prior probability considering the neighborhood, which is the probability that a person in the neighborhood is 185 cm or taller.

Given:
- P(T | N) = 20% or 0.20
- P(T) = 15% or 0.15
- P(N): Probability that the attack occurred in that specific neighborhood.
- P(T âˆ© N): Joint probability of both the attacker being 185 cm or taller and the attack occurring in the specific neighborhood.

Using Bayes' theorem:
\[P(T | N) = \frac{P(N | T) * P(T)}{P(N)}\]

Let's calculate step-by-step:

1. Find \(P(N)\):
Since the attack definitely happened in the neighborhood, \(P(N)\) would be 1.

2. \(P(T)\):
From the neighborhood data, we know the prior probability of any person being 185 cm or taller is \(0.20\).

Thus:
\[P(T | N) = \frac{P(N | T) * P(T)}{P(N)}\]
\[P(T | N) = \frac{0.2 \times 0.15}{1}\]
\[P(T | N) = 0.03\]

So, the probability that the attacker is 185 cm or taller given that the attack occurred in that specific neighborhood is 0.03 (or 3%).

{probability: 0.03}
