\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

- \(A\) is the event that the attacker is 185 cm or taller.
- \(B\) is the event that the attack occurred in the neighborhood where the height distribution is known.

- \(P(A^c)\) (probability that a person in the neighborhood is shorter than 185 cm) = 1 - 0.20 = 0.80

Assuming that the probability of being attacked is independent of height:
- \(P(B|A)\) (probability that the attack occurred given the attacker is 185 cm or taller) = 1
- \(P(B|A^c)\) (probability that the attack occurred given the attacker is shorter than 185 cm) = 1

To find \(P(B)\), we use the law of total probability:
\[ P(B) = P(B|A) \cdot P(A) + P(B|A^c) \cdot P(A^c) \]
\[ P(B) = 1 \cdot 0.20 + 1 \cdot 0.80 = 1 \]

Now we can apply Bayes' theorem:
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]
\[ P(A|B) = \frac{1 \cdot 0.20}{1} = 0.20 \]
